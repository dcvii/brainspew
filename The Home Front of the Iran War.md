The war in Iran has come home.

On Thursday afternoon, a Lebanese-born U.S. citizen named Ayman Mohamad Ghazali drove a vehicle packed with explosives into Temple Israel in West Bloomfield Township, Michigan—one of the largest Reform Jewish congregations in the United States—while 140 children under the age of 5 were attending preschool inside. The building was set ablaze. A security guard was struck by the vehicle. More than 50 first responders were later [treated for smoke inhalation](https://www.cbsnews.com/detroit/news/50-law-enforcement-officers-sought-medical-treatment-after-temple-israel-attack/).

After trading gunfire with security, Ghazali killed himself with a gunshot to the head. His was the only death.

The motivation for the attack has not yet been confirmed, but according to federal authorities, just days earlier, two of Ghazali’s brothers were killed in an Israeli drone strike in Lebanon. Ghazali [was reportedly](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/synagogue-shooting-michigan-what-we-know/) “devastated” by their deaths and withdrew from his family in the days before staging the attack.

Then, on Thursday evening, the most important detail emerged: His brothers were members of the Iran-backed terror group Hezbollah.

At this point, the story should have been clear: Two bona fide terrorists were killed for their roles in an Iranian-aligned militant group dedicated to the extermination of the West. Then their brother hatched a terror plot against the Jewish community of Michigan.

Four attacks. In less than three weeks. All emerging from the same ideological ecosystem.

But that’s not how much of the media chose to report it.

“Man Who Rammed Into Michigan Synagogue Had Just Lost Family in an Israeli Strike in Lebanon,” read a Friday headline [from the Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/michigan-synagogue-attack-fbi-investigation-58331bad2c28e78c6c99a4a764637873). A conservative commentator on X [wrote that](https://x.com/KimKatieUSA/status/2032238048159924450) the attacker “had recently suffered the loss of family members due to an air strike in the Middle East.” Mo Baydoun, the mayor of Dearborn Heights, [said Ghazali](https://abcnews.com/US/suspect-michigan-synagogue-attack-lost-family-israeli-strike/story?id=131031752&cid=social_twitter_abcn) “lost several members of his own family... in an Israeli attack on their home in Lebanon.” *The New York Times*, for its part, [reminded readers](https://x.com/honestreporting/status/2032259327768019040?s=46) that Temple Israel was “dedicated to the formation of a Jewish state.”

As though the brothers were merely innocent victims of Israeli violence. As though there were a moral equivalence between a domestic terrorist and a state sworn to protect its citizens. As though the relevant context for an attempted mass murder of preschoolers is the geopolitical alignment of their house of worship.

This was a terror attack on American soil. And it wasn’t the only one that week, or even that day.

Earlier on Thursday, 700 miles away at Old Dominion University in Virginia, a convicted ISIS supporter screamed “Allahu Akbar” before opening fire, killing one person and injuring two others. He would likely have murdered more had a group of Reserve Officers’ Training Corps (ROTC) cadets not surrounded and killed him.

Days earlier, two terror suspects hurled explosives during a protest outside the home of New York City mayor Zohran Mamdani. They were reportedly inspired by ISIS propaganda; both cited the terrorist group when speaking to investigators. “This was a planned attack motivated by extremist ideology and inspired by a violent foreign terrorist organization,” said New York Police Commissioner [Jessica Tisch](https://www.cbsnews.com/newyork/news/zohran-mamdani-gracie-mansion-ied-nypd-explosive-jessica-tisch/).

Two weeks before that, a shooter killed three people and injured more than a dozen more in downtown Austin, Texas. Authorities say the suspect was wearing a “Property of Allah” sweatshirt and shirt with an Iranian flag design, and they are currently investigating whether the attack was connected to the war.

Four attacks. In less than three weeks. All emerging from the same ideological ecosystem. It is the same rotten worldview that underpins the Islamic Republic—and that the mullahs have helped spread far and wide.

The war has come home. And yet so many struggle to see what is in front of their own faces.

When a man whose brothers were Hezbollah terrorists attempts to bomb a synagogue, we should be able to say so plainly.

The Michigan synagogue attack was not the only act of terror the media distorted. As *The Free Press* ’s Mene Ukueberuwa [wrote last week](https://www.thefp.com/p/terror-on-the-upper-east-side), if you were reading mainstream coverage about the bombing attempt in New York City, or “listening to the words of the city’s top elected official, you would assume the bombs were placed by white supremacists.” Why? Because each of these sources omitted every detail indicating otherwise. As recently as Thursday, five days after investigators had extensively reported the attackers’ motivations, *The New York Times* published the [following headline](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/12/nyregion/gracie-mansion-attack-nyc.html) about one suspect: “At 13, He Was Selling Sneakers. At 18, He’s Facing Terror Charges.”

But sometimes the thing staring us in the face is exactly what it appears to be. Militant movements across the globe are openly declaring their hostility toward Western societies, Jews, and liberal democracy. Those movements operate globally, and their ideologies travel easily across borders.

Of course, this is not entirely new. Terror has reached American shores before: 9/11, the Boston Marathon bombing, the Pulse nightclub massacre in Orlando. But in each of those moments, American society had little trouble calling them what they were. Today, even as such attacks occur with increasing frequency, our willingness to confront them seems to have diminished.

When a man whose brothers were Hezbollah terrorists attempts to bomb a synagogue, we should be able to say so plainly. When two men throw explosives at a protest in New York City, we should not feel compelled to obscure the ideology that motivated them. Rather, we should say it explicitly.

But clarity alone is not enough. It must be matched with vigilance.

If there had been no security guarding that Michigan synagogue, dozens of children might now be dead. If those ROTC students had not sprung into action, many of their classmates might be as well. If NYPD Assistant Chief Aaron Edwards and Sergeant Luis Navarro had not [leaped over a barricade](https://abc7ny.com/post/gracie-mansion-terror-plot-nypd-officers-stopping-2-suspects-accused-trying-set-off-bombs-during-counterprotests/18704052/) to chase down the suspects outside Gracie Mansion, those men might have tried again—and succeeded.

Two months before Thursday, staff at Temple Israel participated in an active-shooter drill. “Everybody in our building did exactly what they were supposed to do,” [said Rabbi Josh Bennett](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/synagogue-shooting-michigan-what-we-know/). “And because of that, we are sitting here today with what could have been a far worse tragedy.”

It’s hard not to feel that the world is more dangerous than it used to be. We shouldn’t need to have armed guards outside our houses of worship, nor practice security drills within them. But staging trained guards at synagogues is not a sign of weakness. It is a sign that a free society responds to danger not by locking down, but by buckling up. That instead of closing its doors, it fortifies them.

The war at home cannot be won by pretending it does not exist. It can be won only by first recognizing what it is. After all, a society that cannot name its enemies cannot protect itself against them.
