There are actually two separate cases here, and it's worth flagging up front that neither one matches the "Democrat operatives registering homeless people for partisan advantage" framing. Both turn out to be about ballot-petition signature fraud rather than a partisan voter-mobilization scheme.

The older case: In November 2020, then–LA County DA Jackie Lacey charged nine people with felonies alleging they offered money and cigarettes to homeless people on LA's Skid Row in exchange for false and forged signatures on ballot petitions and voter registration forms. The alleged offenses occurred during the 2016 and 2018 election cycles, and the charges included circulating a petition with false names, use of false names on a petition, and voter fraud by registering fictitious or nonexistent persons. Prosecutors alleged there were hundreds of solicitations for false or forged signatures and homeless people were offered a dollar or cigarettes for participation. [CBS News + 3](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/voter-fraud-los-angeles-prosecutor-charges-9-accused-bribing-skid-row-homeless)

The recent case (from a few weeks ago): The DOJ federally charged Brenda Lee Brown Armstrong, 64, of Marina del Rey, who has agreed to plead guilty to one count of paying another person to register to vote, a federal charge that carries a penalty of up to five years. The key detail explaining the mechanics: she was identified by federal authorities in court documents as a long-time "petition circulator" who returned the petitions to her coordinators, who then paid her a set amount for each registered voter's signature. Because her coordinators only paid for signatures attributable to registered voters, Armstrong endeavored to ensure the people who signed her petitions were registered voters. When she found that many of the homeless people were not registered to vote in California, she paid them to fill out registration forms so their petition signatures would count — and on several occasions, Armstrong provided a homeless individual with her own former address in Los Angeles so they had something to write on the registration form. [ABC7 + 5](https://abc7.com/post/la-county-woman-plead-guilty-paying-people-skid-row-vote/19131061/)

So the motive in both cases, as charged, was financial rather than partisan: petition circulators are paid per valid signature, and a signature only counts if it comes from a registered voter. Registration was a means to monetize signatures, not a scheme to swing elections for a party. The reporting doesn't tie either case to the Democratic Party or to any party as an organized actor — the "Democrat operatives" label appears to be a partisan gloss layered onto what prosecutors describe as signature-gathering fraud.

One caveat worth noting: the recent federal case was announced by the DOJ's Civil Rights Division under the current administration, framed explicitly as part of an effort to crack down on what they claim is widespread voter fraud, so the political framing around the prosecution itself is part of the story. But the underlying conduct charged is petition-signature fraud, not a partisan registration drive. [U.S. Department of Justice](https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/california-woman-federally-charged-paying-individuals-including-homeless-people-las-skid-row)
