### The Mooch Report—March 24, 2026

There’s a lot of rumbling about the potential for the US to put ground troops in harms way for Operation Epic Fury. Marines and their ARGs are on the move, with the Tripoli transiting the Strait of Malacca and the Boxer ARG just deployed from San Diego, though it’s unclear if the Boxer float is related to the Iran conflict.

We posted two live episodes last week to get deep into the potential deployment and the gamesmanship being played as shipping through the Straits of Hormuz reduces to a trickle and global oil prices rise from the reduced flow.

Additionally, we took a look at the strange incident where Iran claimed to have shot down an F-35 Lightning II. Mooch and Rain discussed in a live stream the CENTCOM response, the available video evidence and what might have happened based on the F-35’s capabilities.

Stay tuned for more updates, and as always, keep us posted on your suggestions and tips in the comments.

## RIP Chuck Norris

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xjIW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F08b38382-2350-43a4-a3bf-99b3782cc100_670x513.heic)

The man who launched a thousand memes, Chuck Norris died last week at the age of 86*.* Norris, who served as an Air Force Air Policeman (now Security Forces) Airman from 1958-1962, has a permanent place in the hearts of many US servicemembers because of is tough, no-compromise on-camera presence and his steely-eyed stare. From *Enter the Dragon* to *The* *Delta Force* to *Walker Texas Ranger*, Norris became a pop-culture icon whose hilarious memes bedecked many public areas on bases overseas. Fair Winds and Following Seas, Mr. Norris…you will be missed.

The USS Tripoli (LHA 7), a U.S. Navy amphibious assault ship has been ordered to move from the western Pacific to the Middle East to join forces involved in the war against Iran.  
  
The Tripoli, loaded with an air combat element of F-35Bs, V-22s, H-53s, and AH-1s along with 2,500 Marines from the 31st Marine Expeditionary Unit, is a force multiplier wherever it’s deployed. But the question as the ship has just passed through the Strait of Malaca near Singapore is how it will be used by CENTCOM once it reaches the North Arabian Sea in about a week from now.  
  
Instead of destroying Kharg Island's oil resources due to the adverse effect that would have on energy markets and the price of gas domestically, the Trump Administration could order CENTCOM to seize it. And the ideal option to attempt that based on assets in the region is the Marines of the 31st MEU.  
  
But what would that operation look like and what are the risks?
