TEL AVIV— [Iran](https://www.wsj.com/topics/place/iran) fired missiles toward Israel on Sunday, after a deadly Israeli airstrike on Beirut hours earlier targeting the Tehran-backed militants Hezbollah, Israel’s military said.

It marks the first time Iran has targeted Israel during its ceasefire with the U.S. that went into force in early April.

The attack came after Tehran threatened to hit [Israel](https://www.wsj.com/topics/place/israel) and American bases in the Middle East in response to the airstrike on the Lebanese capital, the first time Israeli warplanes have targeted Beirut since a [ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon](https://www.wsj.com/world/middle-east/israel-and-lebanons-renewed-ceasefire-is-already-under-strain-6a984630?mod=article_inline) was announced by the U.S. last week.

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu said he had ordered his military to strike a Hezbollah headquarters in the Dahieh district of [Beirut](https://www.wsj.com/topics/place/beirut), a stronghold of the Iranian-backed militant group. Israel’s military said it had earlier intercepted rockets fired by Hezbollah into northern Israel.
