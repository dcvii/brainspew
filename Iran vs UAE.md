Why the Islamic regime is so obsessed with the UAE, and my advice as an Iranian to Abu Dhabi.

Almost two years ago, in Jan 2024, one of my Iranian-American friends had predicted today on X clearly:

“When you put the mullah and the Burj Khalifa next to each other, you can’t help but think that even if the mullah has just one day left in his life, he’ll ultimately fire a missile at that tower.”

He was shockingly accurate. They proved it again.

We Iranians who have lived under this regime can read the mullahs like an open book. We predict their moves easily because we understand their psychology, their deep resentments, and their twisted calculations.

In the previous escalation, the very first thing they did, shocking the entire world, was attack the UAE, with strikes landing dangerously close to Burj Khalifa.

Today, after Operation Freedom (which had nothing to do with the UAE), they once again launched missiles and drones at their territory, reportedly hitting oil infrastructure.

So why this repeated obsession with the UAE?

Here are the reasons, and the last one is the most important one that many outsiders miss:

1\. Resentment and victimhood mentality: The Islamic Republic’s ideology is built on eternal Shia oppression. In their psychopathic worldview, the original oppressors were always the Sunnis.

The UAE represents everything they hate and envy: a successful, modern, prosperous Sunni state that has boldly normalized relations with Israel and maintains close ties with the United States. To them, you have “sold out” your Islamic identity. Your very success is a daily humiliation.

2\. Revenge: After the previous strikes, the UAE cooperated closely with the US to shut down the regime’s money-laundering networks and block regime's oil sales through your ports. This was a severe financial blow to the mullahs. In their twisted minds, you “betrayed” them, and now they are taking revenge.

3\. Easy target: You are the closest high-value prize for their missiles and drones.

4\. Unresolved dispute over islands: The mullahs know that many Iranians remain wary of Gulf states due to history: past support for the regime (specially against the Shah) and perceptions of opportunism.

And there is one specific to UAE:

There is the old dispute over the three Iranian islands (Abu Musa, Greater Tunb, and Lesser Tunb). These islands were under Iran's control, the issue was largely settled decades ago, yet the UAE has continued to claim them from time to time.

For Iranians, this touches the deepest nerve: territorial integrity. We are extremely sensitive about our map and our borders. The regime knows this perfectly. When under pressure, the Islamists suddenly become ultra-nationalist and wave the “UAE want our islands” card to create a wedge.

Their goal is to make ordinary Iranians think, “We hate the regime, but we won’t side with the UAE.”

My advice to UAE leaders and influencers:

If you want the Iranian people truly on your side (and you should, we are your best long-term insurance in the region), consider the following:

1\. Speak directly to the Iranian people, not the regime. Just like @netanyahu and @POTUS. Make a clear, loud distinction between the Iranian nation and the mullahs. Publicly acknowledge the suffering and massacres we have endured for 47 years, your silence on the January massacres has not gone unnoticed.

2\. Respect the clear choice of the Iranian people: Prince @PahlaviReza is the representative of our national will. He is the one who stands for the continuity of Iran, the unbreakable territorial integrity of our country, and a future free from the mullahs. Any positive signal toward him and what he represents will carry enormous weight.

3\. Most importantly, be explicit: Tell Iranians that you want a strong, stable, and prosperous Iran as a true partner and ally, not a weakened neighbor you plan to dominate or betray once the regime falls. Iranians have long memories.

This kind of reassurance is the most powerful weapon against the regime’s propaganda.

Iranians are natural allies for the UAE in the long term. Operation “Epic Fury” is succeeding in part because the Iranian people overwhelmingly trust President Trump and Israel's intention.

The mullahs fear a genuine understanding between the Iranian people and the UAE more than any missile defense.

Don’t let the regime exploit old suspicions. Reach the Iranian street with honesty and vision.

To our bright future together!
