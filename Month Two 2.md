### The Mooch Report—March 31, 2026

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tvL4!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F078567dc-2755-4982-97f2-2a36baeda928_711x370.heic)

Operation Epic Fury has entered its second month, and the conflict is broadening in scope and intensity. This week brings significant developments on multiple fronts — from sea lanes to ground forces — and the pace isn’t letting up.

An Iranian ballistic missile and drone strike hit Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia on March 27, injuring ten U.S. airmen and damaging several KC-135 tankers and an AWACS aircraft — the third known attack on a base only 400 miles from Iran’s coast.

Meanwhile, the 82nd Airborne’s 1st Brigade Combat Team has received a mobilization order, and two Marine Amphibious Ready Groups are converging on the region. Once they’re on station, CENTCOM will have approximately 6,000 troops positioned and ready to give Adm. Cooper options he doesn’t currently have.

Sal Mercogliano and “Rain” Waters rejoin Mooch for a live stream covering the state of shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, A-10s striking the PMF in Iraq in what is now a four-front conflict, and the concerning counter-drone capability gaps that visiting Ukrainian experts flagged in both U.S. and host-nation forces.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fXBB-OauESk)

On March 27 an Iranian ballistic missile and drone attack on Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia damaged several KC-135 aerial refueling aircraft and one AWACS early warning aircraft and left at least 10 U.S. Air Force airmen injured, two severely.  
  
This is the third known attack on PSAB, which is only 400 miles from the coast of Iran. The air base has been a strategic hub for American forces for years and remains so as part of Epic Fury, hosting several dozen tankers, AWACS, and surveillance aircraft. The factors that make it an important base to the Americans, also make it a target to the Iranians.

---

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u_eHlDaIVoM)

A component of the U.S. Army’s 82nd Airborne Division has been given mobilization order, which is the step before a go order. 1,000 soldiers of the 1st Brigade Combat Team, the 82nd’s immediate response force, also known as the “ready brigade,” can deploy within 18 hours of getting the go order and could be airlifting aboard a conga line of C-17s from their base at Fort Bragg to war zone any day now.  
  
The 1,000 paratroopers are in addition to the two U.S. Marine Corps Amphibious Ready Groups that have been ordered to join Epic Fury.
