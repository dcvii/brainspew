[Microsoft](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/MSFT) [MSFT -0.74%decrease; red down pointing triangle](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/MSFT) Chief Executive [Satya Nadella](https://www.wsj.com/topics/person/satya-nadella) took the stand in a landmark trial, testifying at length about OpenAI’s temporary ouster of [Sam Altman](https://www.wsj.com/topics/person/sam-altman) in a case pitting the tech giant and the startup against billionaire [Elon Musk](https://www.wsj.com/topics/person/elon-musk).

After Altman was [briefly pushed out of OpenAI](https://www.wsj.com/tech/sam-altman-departs-open-ai-mira-murati-interim-ceo-41f6d51e?mod=article_inline) by its board members in November 2023, Nadella said he pledged his support to potential new leaders but also wanted to ensure that Altman and others didn’t create a competing company or join Google.

“That was obviously very concerning to me,” said Nadella. “Given all of that competition, I just wanted to make sure we could hang on to the band that created all this technology.”

Musk’s team has sought to paint Microsoft as a powerful company exerting control over OpenAI’s fate due to its billions of dollars of investments in the startup.

Musk attorney Steven Molo pointed to a news release that OpenAI’s board published shortly after Altman’s firing in which they said he hadn’t been “consistently candid in his communications with the board, hindering its ability to exercise its responsibilities.”

“Pretty definitive statement as to why they fired him, right?” asked Molo.

“Yeah,” said Nadella.

Musk [is suing OpenAI and its leaders](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/elon-musk-takes-stand-in-second-day-of-trial-against-openai-59d50fbf?mod=article_inline), Sam Altman and Greg Brockman, for allegedly manipulating him into giving tens of millions of dollars to a nonprofit organization, only for them to turn the AI lab into a for-profit venture. Musk is also suing Microsoft—OpenAI’s largest investor—for aiding Altman and Brockman in their alleged deception.

Remedies that Musk is asking the court to grant include removing Altman and [Brockman from their leadership roles](https://www.wsj.com/tech/musk-openai-trial-greg-brockman-diary-journal-6950270e?mod=article_inline) and as much as $180 billion in damages to be transferred from the company’s for-profit company to its nonprofit parent.

OpenAI has said that Musk not only knew about the for-profit conversion plan, but that he also supported it and asked for unilateral control. OpenAI leadership refused to give him a majority stake. Soon after in 2019, OpenAI secured a large investment from Microsoft. In total, Microsoft has committed $13 billion into OpenAI.

Other witnesses who have so far testified in this blockbuster trial include Musk, Brockman, as well as former OpenAI board member and [Musk’s romantic partner](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/elon-musks-romantic-partner-testifies-about-her-role-on-openais-board-1fc6d89b?mod=article_inline) Shivon Zilis.

Altman is also expected to take the stand this week, though he has shown up for court nearly every day of the trial.

During Nadella’s testimony, he made clear to jurors that Musk never reached out to him to express concerns when Microsoft made its initial investment in 2019 to OpenAI, nor when Microsoft announced its exclusive license for GPT 3 in 2020. Musk also didn’t reach out to Nadella when Microsoft committed an additional $10 billion in investment in January 2023, he testified.

“Does Mr. Musk know how to contact you?” asked a Microsoft attorney.

“We have each other’s phone numbers,” said Nadella, glancing at the jury.

Attorneys for Musk have pointed to an interview Nadella gave shortly after the episode, often called “the blip,” in which Altman was fired and then returned in a matter of days. “We are below them, above them, around them,” Nadella said at the time.

“It had nothing to do with control,” said Nadella on Monday during his testimony. “It had to do with just the strategic partnership which gave us the rights to the I.P. — but it’s not about control.”

Molo pressed the Microsoft CEO on his influence on OpenAI, including selecting new board members after Altman was reinstated as CEO of OpenAI post-blip.

A group chat exchange between Altman, Nadella and Microsoft chief technology officer Kevin Scott flashed on TV screens in the courtroom. Altman proposed a number of names as potential new board members, including Sue Desmond-Hellmann who Nadella voiced approval for in the group chat. Desmond-Hellmann joined OpenAI as a board member in 2024.

Nadella told jurors he was never explicitly given a specific reason for why Altman was ousted. That made him comfortable enough to want to hire Altman and Brockman at Microsoft when Altman was ousted, he testified.

Asked to reflect on what the partnership between Microsoft and OpenAI has accomplished, Nadella testified he was “proud” that it had “created one of the largest nonprofits in the world.”
