## The Specific Geographical Divide in China

The specific boundary that separates Eastern and Western China is known as the **Lüshun Line** or the **Lüshun Boundary**.

### Overview of the Lüshun Line

The Lüshun Line is often illustrated as a rough demarcation:

- **East of the Line**:
	- Includes major cities like Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou.
	- Characterized by higher population density and more developed infrastructure.
- **West of the Line**:
	- Encompasses regions like Xinjiang, Tibet, and Qinghai.
	- Features a more rugged terrain, lower population density, and varied ethnic groups.

This division reflects significant differences in economic development, climate, and cultural diversity across the country.

---

The **Lüshun Line** is crucial for understanding regional disparities within China, influencing policies and development strategies in its eastern and western regions.
