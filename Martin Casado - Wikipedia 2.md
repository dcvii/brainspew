**Martín Casado** is an American [software engineer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_engineer "Software engineer"), [entrepreneur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entrepreneur "Entrepreneur"), and [investor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Investor "Investor"). He is a [general partner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_partner "General partner") at [Andreessen Horowitz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andreessen_Horowitz "Andreessen Horowitz"), was a pioneer of [software-defined networking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software-defined_networking "Software-defined networking"), and was a co-founder and the chief technology officer of [Nicira Networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicira_Networks "Nicira Networks").

## Early life and education

Martín Casado was born around 1976 in [Cartagena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartagena,_Spain "Cartagena, Spain"), [Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain "Spain").[^1] He received his bachelor's degree from [Northern Arizona University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Arizona_University "Northern Arizona University") in 2000.[^2] In 2017, he received an honorary doctorate from the same university.[^3] Casado was a researcher at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory from 1998 until 2003.[^4] While at LLNL, he ran large-scale computer simulations for the [United States Department of Defense](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Defense "United States Department of Defense").[^5]

Casado attended [Stanford University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_University "Stanford University"), earning both his Masters and PhD in computer science in 2007.[^6] While at Stanford, he began the development of OpenFlow,[^7] an open-source protocol that enabled [software-defined networking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software-defined_networking "Software-defined networking"). His Ph.D. thesis, "Architectural Support for Security Management in Enterprise Networks,” under advisors [Nick McKeown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_McKeown "Nick McKeown"), [Scott Shenker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scott_Shenker "Scott Shenker") and [Dan Boneh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dan_Boneh "Dan Boneh"), was published in 2008.[^8]

## Career

In 2007, Casado co-founded Nicira Networks along with McKeown and Shenker, a [Palo Alto, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California "Palo Alto, California") based company working on network virtualization. Casado also served as Nicira’s chief technology officer.[^9]

Along with McKeown and Shenker, Casado promoted software-defined networking.[^7] His Ph.D. work at Stanford University led to the development of the OpenFlow protocol, which was promoting using the term [software-defined networking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software-defined_networking "Software-defined networking") (SDN).[^10] McKeown and Shenker co-founded the [Open Networking Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_Networking_Foundation "Open Networking Foundation") (ONF) in 2011 to transfer control of OpenFlow to a not-for-profit organization.[^11]

In July 2012, [VMware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VMware "VMware") acquired Nicira for $1.26 billion.[^12] [^13] At VMware he was made a [fellow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fellow "Fellow") and held the positions [chief technology officer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_technology_officer "Chief technology officer") (CTO) for networking and security and [general manager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_manager "General manager") of the Networking and Security Business Unit.[^14]

Casado left VMware and joined [venture capital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venture_capital "Venture capital") firm [Andreessen Horowitz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andreessen_Horowitz "Andreessen Horowitz") in February 2016 as its ninth general partner.[^15] [^16] [^17] Andreessen Horowitz had been one of the investors in Nicira, contributing $17.7 million to the start-up venture.[^13]
