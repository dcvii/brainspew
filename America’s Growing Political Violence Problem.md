Death threats, assassination attempts, and political intimidation are now so frequent they barely make headlines. Another danger is how quickly we’ve grown used to it.

We are living through an age of political violence in America. One sign of that: Threats of violence against public figures are now so commonplace that they barely make the front page.

Consider the past 48 hours.

On Tuesday morning, we learned from a [CBS News report](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/pardoned-capitol-rioter-charged-threatening-hakeem-jeffries-nyc-trump/) that Christopher Moynihan, a participant in the January 6, 2021 riot at the U.S. Capitol, was arrested for allegedly threatening to kill House Minority Leader Hakeem Jeffries.

The previous evening, we learned that a 28-year-old Texas man named Nicholas Ray also was arrested for allegedly [making death threats](https://www.politico.com/news/2025/10/21/florida-charges-texas-man-over-threats-to-jewish-conservative-media-figures-00616547); this time, the targets were conservative media figures in Florida, including *Free Press* contributors Seth Dillon and Josh Hammer.

Coincidentally, on the same day of Ray’s arrest, we published [an essay by Dillon](https://www.thefp.com/p/the-foolishness-of-no-enemies-to-the-right) on the dangers of failing to stand up to poisonous ideas. Ray used the handle @ZionistAreScum on social media and said in one post: “You’re gonna get killed soon buddy. It’s coming, traitors get hung.”

---

It’s been just six weeks since the murder of Charlie Kirk, but any sense that his assassination might shock the country into a turn away from political violence and violent political rhetoric has already faded.

Earlier this month, a [man was arrested](https://www.washingtonpost.com/dc-md-va/2025/10/06/bomb-threat-catholic-church-supreme-court/) with more than 200 handmade destructive devices—including Molotov cocktails—outside St. Matthew’s Cathedral in Washington, D.C., where Supreme Court justices were expected to attend Mass. In Virginia, Jay Jones, the Democratic candidate for attorney general, has been caught in leaked text messages allegedly fantasizing about putting “ [two bullets](https://www.politico.com/news/2025/10/04/virginia-elections-jay-jones-texts-00594261) ” in the head of his opponent in the race. He has not stepped down. Two weeks ago, Attorney General Pam Bondi [announced the arrest](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/bondi-announces-arrest-coward-who-allegedly-threatened-conservative-influencer-following-kirks-murder) of a suspect who had sent a letter to the home of conservative political commentator Benny Johnson threatening to kill him.

It’s been just six weeks since the murder of Charlie Kirk, but any sense that his assassination might shock the country into a turn away from political violence and violent political rhetoric has already faded.

Such incidents now pass through the news cycle without much comment.

The lack of alarm should alarm all Americans.

Thankfully, and with huge credit to fast-acting law enforcement, many of these wannabe assassins have announced their plans in advance—averting tragedy. But Moynihan already had a history of political violence, which was excused and then pardoned by the president.

Trump survived not one but two assassination attempts last year, including one that bloodied his left ear with a bullet intended for his head. That attack killed one person and wounded two others.

---

And yet the president’s own pardon of all of the rioters from that terrible January day—violent and not—have sent exactly the wrong message. In the case of Christopher Moynihan, Trump’s pardon meant the release of someone who would threaten the life of a congressional leader.

Moynihan, the man arrested for threatening Jeffries’s life, made it to the [floor of the Senate](https://www.axios.com/2025/10/21/pardoned-jan-6-rioter-charged-with-plotting-jeffries-assassination) on January 6. He rifled through the senators’ desk, saying, “There’s gotta be something we can fucking use against these scumbags.” He was sentenced to 21 months in prison in February 2023.

Now he is back in custody. “Hakeem Jeffries makes a speech in a few days in NYC I cannot allow this terrorist to live,” he [allegedly wrote](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/oct/21/january-6-rioter-threaten-hakeem-jeffries) in a text, along with “Even if I am hated, he must be eliminated,” and “I will kill him for the future.”

Our lawmakers have a responsibility to lower the temperature and return us to a degree of political civility. So far, with some [notable exceptions](https://www.thefp.com/p/spencer-cox-says-america-is-broken) like Republican Utah governor Spencer Cox, they have not shown themselves to be up to the task.

---

Trump is using Kirk’s assassination to help justify a crackdown on his political opponents. (Read *Free Press* reporter Gabe Kaminsky [in our pages](https://www.thefp.com/p/inside-the-trump-induced-chilling-effect-on-liberal-philanthropy) on the chilling effect it has had on liberal philanthropy.)

Leading Democrats, meanwhile, have [long called](https://waters.house.gov/media-center/press-releases/congresswoman-maxine-waters-blasts-baseless-indictment-of-james-comey-trump-is-dragging-the-united-states-down-the-dark-and-dangerous-path-toward-dictatorship) Trump an authoritarian, or “an existential threat to our democracy,” to [quote a headline](https://democrats.org/news/reminder-donald-dictator-on-day-one-trump-is-an-existential-threat-to-our-democracy/) on their party’s website. In the last election, then–Vice President Kamala Harris [called him a fascist](https://time.com/7096898/harris-trump-facist-speech/), a description Democrats [keep returning to](https://www.notus.org/democrats/dnc-chair-ken-martin-party-plays-by-rules?utm_source=chatgpt.com). If you refer to a politician as someone who would destroy American democracy, it logically follows that that person should be stopped by any means necessary.

There is no outside force that will fix the division that ails this country. We have to do it ourselves. It just takes work.

Elsewhere on the left, political violence is both tolerated and celebrated. Luigi Mangione, accused of murdering a healthcare CEO, has become a cult hero swooned over by progressive fangirls. When two Israeli embassy employees were murdered by an anti-Israel gunman in Washington, the Democratic Socialists of America [weren’t sure](https://www.thefp.com/p/dsa-torn-over-condemning-murder) whether they should condemn the violence or not. DSA member Zohran Mamdani, likely the next mayor of New York, did not [until recently](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/jul/16/zohran-mamdani-israel-gaza-intifada) see any issue with the phrase “globalize the intifada,” a call for violence against Jews worldwide.

Then came Charlie Kirk’s murder, which [was excused](https://www.thefp.com/p/students-celebrating-murder-charlie-kirk) and minimized by many on the left—a grim irony given that Kirk embodied exactly the right way to disagree.

There is no outside force that will fix the division that ails this country. We have to do it ourselves. It just takes work. That means vocally resisting calls to political violence and always refusing to participate in anything that resembles it.

It also means that however serious our disagreements become, we must realize that our opponents aren’t some alien threat, but our fellow Americans. We need to talk to each other again, reason through whatever [arguments we have](https://www.thefp.com/p/yuval-levin-have-an-argument-politics-free-speech), listen to the other side, and accept that good people can have political beliefs we don’t share.
