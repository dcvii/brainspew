](https://www.quora.com/notifications)[Why have the wages of the American middle class stagnated for decades?](https://www.quora.com/Why-have-the-wages-of-the-American-middle-class-stagnated-for-decades?no_redirect=1)

This question previously had details. They are now in a comment.

The simple answer is that economically speaking there is no more low hanging fruit. And yes there are more 7 foot tall people in America than there ever were, but the overwhelming majority of us are not going to be 7 feet tall. You get what falls to the ground.

I take rather seriously the idea that America benefited from the spoils of war. We won WW2 and in that endeavor we built a nuclear empire, the most sophisticated application of theoretical scientific research ever. And we funded the space race. Huge. But outside of that, and think really hard about it, what else have we done to build a future that never existed before. Most people in the world have run out of ideas, and are chasing these things that America did many years previously.

We built skyscrapers. We mass produced cars. We built modern hospitals. We processed petroleum. We created banks that allowed ordinary people to have mortgages and live in houses they don’t even know how to build, on credit. We built roads, aircraft, heavy equipment and the logistics to put all that together. And the one thing we did that nobody else has done in human history is built the ability to mobilize and project military force around the globe at a moment’s notice. If you’re not part of those projects, then why would you even expect that your middle class skills are going to be more and more valuable over time? It’s a foolish, ahistorical expectation.

What did Asia do in the 20th Century? To be very blunt and brief, they went from feudal to Western, sorta. They started building skyscrapers, cars, hospitals and processing petroleum. And all over the world, illiterate rural populations are becoming literate urban populations. That’s just what America did starting after the Civil War 150 years ago. But it’s not something fundamentally new and different.

Everybody talks about income inequality and that’s real and permanent. But what is the 1% doing that is fundamentally different from the rest? Nothing. They don’t have flying cars, they have ridiculously expensive cars with four wheels just like everybody else. They don’t have anti-gravity watches, they have $20,000 watches that don’t tell time any differently or any better than my $100 watch. They don’t have mansions with swimming pools full of the elixir of youth, they swim in water just like we do at the public pool.

And what is the middle class going to do when income inequality gets unbearable? They’re going to start murdering people, or electing dictators who murder people. Just like it has always been throughout human history. If you want something that’s going to deliver the middle class from stagnant mediocrity, it requires a war, a revolution or a plague
