The progressive campaign to discredit the current Supreme Court escalated this week with the publication of confidential memos from a decade ago about the Obama Clean Power Plan. The real scandal is the methods the political left is using to subvert the Court.

[The New York Times](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/NYT) this week delivered an over-egged scoop of internal Court memos from early February 2016. At the time, state Attorneys General were asking the Justices to stay Barack Obama’s far-reaching climate rule while it was litigated at the D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals.

On Feb. 9, 2016, days before Justice Antonin Scalia’s death, a 5-4 majority stayed the rule. In the reporters’ telling, “that night marks the birth, many legal experts believe, of the court’s modern ‘shadow docket’” when conservative Justices “short-circuited the deliberations of lower courts” and embarked “on a questionable new way of operating.”

This tendentious narrative conveniently fits the left’s current campaign against the Court but it omits crucial facts and legal context. The 2015 Environmental Protection Agency rule illegally conscripted states into serving as hand-maidens for the left’s green-energy transition. States were required to submit plans to meet the Obama EPA’s CO2 emissions reductions all the way through 2030.

If states didn’t submit plans by September 2016, or if the EPA disapproved of them, the feds would take over. The Clean Air Act lets EPA regulate pollutants from individual power plants, but the Obama Administration’s novel interpretation let the feds reorder the entire power industry.

The Supreme Court memos reflect concerns by conservative Justices that states would be compelled to embark on an expensive and irreversible green-energy transition based on a mistaken interpretation of the law. Without a stay, “both the states and private industry will suffer irreparable harm from a rule that is—in my view—highly unlikely to survive,” Chief Justice [John Roberts](https://www.wsj.com/topics/person/john-roberts) wrote.

The Chief correctly warned that the regulation would force a “reordering of the domestic power sector before this court has an opportunity to review its legality.” The Court might not get to rule on the regulation until 2018, the Chief wrote, and by then it could become a “fait accompli.”

That’s what had happened with the EPA’s 2012 mercury rule, which the Court finally struck down in 2015. The Chief cited an Obama regulator’s boast that the ruling didn’t matter because power plants were already forced to come into compliance. The memos show the Justices stayed the Clean Power Plan to prevent the Obama team from short-circuiting judicial review again.

In any event, the Court ultimately struck down the Obama climate rule in 2022 (*West Virginia v. EPA*) using the major questions doctrine, which the Chief cited in his 2016 memo. The stay preserved the status quo while litigation played out, averting the hefty costs and damage that would otherwise have come to pass.

#### \*\*\*

The 2016 stay also wasn’t the birth of the “shadow docket.” University of Chicago law professor William Baude coined the term in 2015 for cases that the Justices were asked to address on an emergency posture and drew little attention. The Court had in prior decades issued many emergency orders in election and capital punishment cases.

Its emergency docket has since grown as Presidents of both parties have stretched their powers, and as lower-court judges issue far more nationwide injunctions. A 6-3 Court majority blocked the Covid eviction moratorium on the emergency docket only after President Biden boasted about gaming the litigation process to delay a High Court ruling.

To avoid hearing more emergency cases, the Justices have begun taking up more cases on an expedited basis, including one next week challenging Mr. Trump’s termination of Temporary Protected Status for some immigrants. But Chief Justice John Roberts and his conservative colleagues get no credit from the left even when they rule against Mr. Trump.

Justice Samuel Alito expressed concern in one 2016 memo that failure to stay the Clean Power Plan would render “our institutional legitimacy—a nullity.” That’s the broader goal of the left and whoever leaked the memos. They don’t care that the leaks could chill deliberation and collegiality on the Court.

Leaking confidential documents may violate the judicial and American Bar Association’s rules of conduct. The High Court has long maintained its own confidentiality rules for Justices and clerks. After a draft of its *Dobbs* abortion decision was leaked in 2022, the Chief in 2024 required clerks for the Justices to sign nondisclosure agreements, though these don’t apply to clerks from earlier terms.

Let’s hope the Court’s marshal investigates the leak of the memos, interrogates clerks who served in that term, and exposes whoever the miscreant is.
