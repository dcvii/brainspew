From the bustling labs of the Northeast to the remote deserts of the Southwest, American ingenuity can be found everywhere. This map traces locations historically associated with the breakthroughs that have defined the nation’s 250-year legacy.

Airplane Flight

**Kitty Hawk, N.C.:** On the desolate dunes of the Outer Banks, the Wright brothers conquered the sky in 1903, proving that controlled, powered flight was no longer a human fantasy.

Moon Landing

**Kennedy Space Center, Fla.:** This coastal launch site served as the terrestrial gateway for humanity’s greatest leap, projecting American technological prestige into the cosmos.

Lightbulb

**Menlo Park, N.J.:** Thomas Edison’s “invention factory” transformed a rural hamlet into the birthplace of the modern age, by perfecting the first commercially viable incandescent light.

Cotton Gin

**Savannah, Ga.:** Eli Whitney’s mechanical engine exponentially increased production, cementing the South’s economic power and tragically entrenching the institution of slavery.

Global Positioning System

**Los Angeles:** Originally a military project managed at the Los Angeles Air Force Base in the 1970s, this satellite-based system changed how the world navigates.

Personal Computer

**Albuquerque, N.M.:** As the home of MITS, maker of the Altair 8800, Albuquerque sparked the computer revolution in the mid-1970s, drawing a young Bill Gates and Paul Allen to start [Microsoft](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/MSFT).

Phillips Screw

**Portland, Ore.:** By preventing the stripping of fasteners, Henry Phillips’s design enabled the use of high-speed power tools essential for World War II-era assembly lines.

Fast Food

**Wichita, Kan.:** White Castle standardized the production of the 5-cent burger, establishing the business model for the modern global franchise and redefining the American diet.

Smartphone

**Las Vegas:** [IBM](https://www.wsj.com/market-data/quotes/IBM) unveiled a device that merged telephony and computing in 1992 at a Las Vegas trade show, previewing the coming mobile era.

Electric Traffic Light

**Salt Lake City:** Developed by Lester Wire, this innovation brought order to the chaos of the early automotive era, creating the blueprint for urban traffic management.

Internet

**Los Angeles:** The first message sent between UCLA and Stanford in 1969 birthed a digital nervous system that has become the fundamental infrastructure of the 21st century.

Telephone

**Boston:** From a Boston laboratory, Alexander Graham Bell’s first successful transmission, in 1876, collapsed the barriers of distance, forever altering human interaction.

Stock Ticker

**New York City:** By streaming the pulse of Wall Street, this invention democratized financial information and laid the groundwork for the modern, high-speed global economy.

Refrigeration

**Philadelphia:** Through the pioneering designs of inventor Oliver Evans, Philadelphia became a hub for the mechanical ice-making technology that stabilized the global food-supply chain.

Polio Vaccine

**Pittsburgh:** Developed at the University of Pittsburgh in the 1950s, this vaccine transformed a devastating childhood disability into a preventable disease, marking a triumph in medical history.

Assembly Line

**Detroit:** Henry Ford’s integration of moving parts and stationary labor in 1913 revolutionized global manufacturing, turning the automobile from a luxury into a middle-class staple.

Steel Plow

**Grand Detour, Ill.:** John Deere’s “self-polishing” steel blade allowed settlers to slice through the sticky Midwest sod, unlocking the agricultural potential of the American heartland.

Atomic Energy

**Los Alamos, N.M.:** Under the veil of the Manhattan Project, this remote mesa became the site of a profound scientific pivot that ended a world war and ushered in the nuclear age.
