The Easter weekend brought the kind of news that stops the scroll: both crew members of the downed F-15E have been recovered. The pilot’s “Sandy” rescue by A-10 Warthogs was already one for the books — but the recovery of the weapons systems officer, a USAF colonel who evaded capture in the Iranian mountains for days, may be the more remarkable story. Special operations forces and the CIA came under heavy IRGC fire to pull him out, and they got him home without losing a single American life.

Operation Epic Fury has now crossed the one-month mark, and this week’s roundup covers the full scope of where things stand. Jack Murphy — who broke the WSO recovery story — joins Mooch to detail the mission. Retired USAF Colonel Tucker “Cinco” Hamilton talks F-35, AI in tactical aviation, and the personal loss that drove him into test piloting. And Sal Mercogliano and “Rain” Waters are back to break down the latest developments in the conflict, including a shipping industry still navigating an unresolved Hormuz crisis and the Trump administration’s $1.5 trillion defense budget proposal.
