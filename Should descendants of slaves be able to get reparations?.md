(https://www.quora.com/notifications)[Is it possible for America to pay reparations to the descendants of slaves?](https://www.quora.com/Is-it-possible-for-America-to-pay-reparations-to-the-descendants-of-slaves?no_redirect=1)

It will never happen. Three practical reasons. A couple of assumptions. One overwhelming reason.

Assumption 1: The reparation amount is more than a token amount. IE more than food stamps, and more than ‘the cost of racism’. That is to say if the average black man with a high school diploma makes 24,000 per year and the average white man with a high school diploma make 30,000 per year it will be more than 6,000 per year.

Assumption 2: Everybody in America is subject to a new DNA test and national ID, and everybody accepts this as legit.

1. The only way it happens, it happens through an act of Congress. That means lobbyists will get their hands on it and it will be perverted. Don’t forget what happened to Reconstruction.
2. The political majority to get that act of Congress will not materialize. Think about it this way. Half of America wants a welfare state and the other half does not. Even for the half that does want welfare, how comfortable do you think the descendants of slaves will be in accepting welfare?
3. Most importantly of all, it’s unconstitutional for people to be tried in absentia or charged for a crime they didn’t commit. 5th Amendment. In other words, it would have to be a civil suit targeted against ‘slave industries’.

That being the case, any non-trivial amount of money (Assumption 1) would end up being stock in those industries, which I suspect the new owners would seek to liquidate. But this gets back to Problem #1.

Take for example that you wanted to go for the obvious. Cotton. The cotton industry was built on slave labor. So the biggest cotton producer in the US would be JG Boswell. They may about 750 million in revenue per year and hold 100s of thousand of acres of farmland. Now let’s say the Reparations Act of 2020 passes and all such business are to be sold to holding companies and 49% of that ownership is passed on to the newly designated Class S (for Slave) shareholders. It sells for 3 billion, and therefore 1.47 billion in stock is now theirs. Do they hold the stock or do they immediately sell it for cash? Let’s assume half the new stock is immediately sold. There is no company on the planet that can survive a collapse in price where some 20% of their stock is liquidated. And who do you think is going to win in the end? All this sudden new money, or the old money?

So the mechanics and economics of this is essentially self-defeating.

No matter what the practical moral hazards are of reparations, here is the most difficult thing to stare down. That is the fact that the idea has been around for over 100 years and the cost of not doing it has been negligible. In other words, there is nothing to fear from saying ‘No’. No has always been the answer, and it will continue to be the answer until Americans are ready to break the Constitution, impose DNA based class citizenship, breakup billions of dollars of businesses, and make Congress impervious to corruption.

Good luck.
