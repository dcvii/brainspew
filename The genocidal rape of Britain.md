## This is genocidal rape

Over the course of my career as a journalist, I have interviewed survivors of genocidal violence who were Jewish, Russian, Cambodian, Rwandan, Greek, Guatemalan, Rohingya, Uyghur, Tibetan, Ukrainian, Nigerian, and Tigrayan. But whether or not we can use the term *genocidal rape* does not depend on the numbers, as I said above, but rather on the intention. Rape satisfies the underlying requirement for genocide under at least three of the enumerated genocidal acts in the [Genocide Convention Art. II](https://www.ohchr.org/en/instruments-mechanisms/instruments/convention-prevention-and-punishment-crime-genocide) (as well as the [Rome Statute Art. 6](https://www.icc-cpi.int/sites/default/files/2024-05/Rome-Statute-eng.pdf)):

> In the present Convention, genocide means any of the following acts committed with intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a national, ethnical, racial or religious group, as such:
> 
> (a) Killing members of the group;
> 
> (b) Causing serious bodily or mental harm to members of the group;
> 
> (c) Deliberately inflicting on the group conditions of life calculated to bring about its physical destruction in whole or in part;
> 
> (d) Imposing measures intended to prevent births within the group;
> 
> (e) Forcibly transferring children of the group to another group.

These terrorist groups have killed people because they were white British non-Muslims. They have caused serious bodily and mental harm to more girls than we know, or probably will ever know. They have deliberately sought to destroy, *in whole* and in part, white British people. They have made girls have abortions and have killed infants. ## Conclusion

This may be one of the worst tragedies ever to befall the people of Britain. The Black Death killed up to half the nation. The Great Irish Famine killed roughly a million. The First World War left 886,000 British dead. The Second left 450,000 more. These stand among the gravest horrors ever visited on these shores. But Britain survived its plagues and its famines. It survived an empire’s worth of grief and stood against literal Nazis. That is a wound a nation can carry and still call itself unbroken, because the wound was done to them, not by them, and they fought the whole way and triumphed.

But ask yourself which is worse: watching your son take the blows of a disease, the ravages of starving, an empire’s violence, and surviving it all — or watching him cut his own throat, with your help. That is the difference. Plague, famine, and war are things done to a people. What this is, is something a people in part decided to do to themselves, and silenced the victims or anyone who spoke out about it, arresting them if they so much as complained on social media. A nation can heal from famine or war far better than it can come back from something like this. And worst of all, I still see no indication that Britain will even do even a fraction of what is required to stop it.
