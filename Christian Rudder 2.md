**Christian Rudder** (born September 1, 1975) <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American technology [entrepreneur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entrepreneur "Entrepreneur") and writer known for co-founding the online dating service [OkCupid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OkCupid "OkCupid") and publishing the site's popular blog posts, which offered insights on dating based on data of user behavior. Following OkCupid's sale to the [owner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IAC_Inc. "IAC Inc.") of [Match.com](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Match.com "Match.com") in 2011, Rudder remained at the company for four years, and in 2014 published the book *[Dataclysm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dataclysm "Dataclysm")* with further analysis of online dating statistics. He left OkCupid in 2015.

Rudder graduated from [Little Rock Central High School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Little_Rock_Central_High_School "Little Rock Central High School") in 1993. He attended [Harvard University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University "Harvard University"), graduating with a degree in [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics") in 1998.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>

Rudder joined [SparkNotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SparkNotes "SparkNotes") in October 1999, a few months after its founding. Rudder was the creative voice of TheSpark.com,<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which was the viral content arm of [SparkNotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SparkNotes "SparkNotes") during the site's early rise to popularity. He became TheSpark's [creative director](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creative_director "Creative director") in March 2001. Soon after the site's sale to [Barnes & Noble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barnes_%26_Noble "Barnes & Noble"), Rudder began building [OkCupid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OkCupid "OkCupid") with SparkNotes founders Chris Coyne, [Sam Yagan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan "Sam Yagan"), and Max Krohn.

### OkCupid and writing

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Christian_Rudder&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: OkCupid and writing")\]

The online dating site OkCupid launched on January 19, 2004, with Rudder and his three business partners as co-founders. Rudder focused on the front-end product and developed the site's editorial voice.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> From 2009 to 2011, OkCupid published statistical observations and analysis of members' preferences and connections; the blog posts were written by Rudder and gained widespread media attention.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In February 2011, OkCupid was sold to [IAC Inc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IAC_Inc. "IAC Inc.")., the owner of Match.com and other dating properties, for $90 million.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Following the sale, Rudder served as president of OkCupid until he left in 2015.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>

Rudder expanded his writings for OkCupid into the non-fiction book *[Dataclysm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dataclysm "Dataclysm")*, which became a [New York Times Best Seller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Times_Best_Seller "New York Times Best Seller") in September 2014 and was a finalist for the [Los Angeles Times Book Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times_Book_Prize "Los Angeles Times Book Prize") that year in the Science & Technology category.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>

Rudder played guitar in the [hardcore punk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hardcore_punk "Hardcore punk") band Pissed Officers and [indie rock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indie_\(music\) "Indie (music)") group [Bishop Allen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bishop_Allen "Bishop Allen"), both with [Justin Rice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justin_Rice "Justin Rice") on bass and Rudder and Rice sharing songwriting and vocal duties.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On Bishop Allen's first three albums, Rudder played most of the instruments except drums and piano.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Through Bishop Allen, Rudder has contributed to the soundtracks of the movies *[Saved!](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saved! "Saved!")*, *[Sleepwalk with Me](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sleepwalk_with_Me "Sleepwalk with Me")*, *[No Strings Attached](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No_Strings_Attached_\(2011_film\) "No Strings Attached (2011 film)")*, *[Nick and Norah's Infinite Playlist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_and_Norah%27s_Infinite_Playlist "Nick and Norah's Infinite Playlist")*, *[Bully](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bully_\(2011_film\) "Bully (2011 film)")*, and several other smaller films and commercials.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>

He appeared in [Andrew Bujalski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrew_Bujalski "Andrew Bujalski") 's film *[Funny Ha Ha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funny_Ha_Ha "Funny Ha Ha")*, as Alex, and appeared as himself in [Peter Sollett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Sollett "Peter Sollett") 's film *[Nick and Norah's Infinite Playlist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_and_Norah%27s_Infinite_Playlist "Nick and Norah's Infinite Playlist")*.

Christian met his wife, Reshma Patel, at a concert in Boston. Patel ran a public relations firm. They married in 2006.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-how_he_spends_his_Sunday-15">[15]</a></sup>

They lived in the [Williamsburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Williamsburg,_Brooklyn "Williamsburg, Brooklyn") neighborhood of [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "New York City") for some time, but moved to Costa Rica in 2020.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
