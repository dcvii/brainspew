What's the deal with Nicholas Kristof and rape dogs.

---

**Claude**

Short version: on May 11, 2026, Nicholas Kristof published a New York Times opinion column titled "The Silence That Meets the Rape of Palestinians," alleging widespread sexual abuse of Palestinian detainees by Israeli guards, soldiers, and settlers. Buried in the piece was an especially inflammatory claim — that Israeli authorities had trained or "coached" dogs to sexually assault prisoners. Kristof detailed the account of an unnamed Gaza journalist who says guards summoned a dog when he was imprisoned in 2024, writing, "He tried to dislodge the dog, he said, but it penetrated him," and cited other prisoners and pro-Palestinian human rights monitors reporting similar experiences. [The Times of Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/from-rutgers-speaker-to-kristof-column-bogus-anti-israel-dog-rape-claim-goes-mainstream/)

The backlash was immediate and came from several directions:

**Plausibility.** Canine experts pushed back hard. James Crosby, a retired police lieutenant and canine aggression expert affiliated with Harvard's Canine Brain Project, told JTA it was "highly unlikely that anybody is going to be able to train a dog to successfully commit a sexual assault." Another canine behavior expert, Michael S. Gould, told National Review the idea was "absurd," citing dogs' sexual instincts, anatomy, and physical mechanics. [The Jerusalem Post](https://www.jpost.com/diaspora/antisemitism/article-896135) [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/media/protesters-demand-new-york-times-retract-controversial-dog-rape-article-fire-nicholas-kristof)

**Sourcing.** The dog claim had been circulating for weeks before Kristof's column, pushed largely by the Euro-Med Human Rights Monitor, which critics characterize as a Hamas-aligned advocacy group. A Rutgers commencement speaker had been canceled a week earlier over a tweet making the same claim. Critics argued Kristof was laundering a propaganda talking point into the paper of record. [The Free Press](https://www.thefp.com/p/nick-kristof-dog-torture-claim-israel-palestine)

**Reaction.** Jewish groups called it "a modern-day blood libel," and Prime Minister Netanyahu announced a defamation lawsuit against the Times over the piece. Protesters gathered outside the Times building on May 14 demanding retraction and Kristof's firing. [The Times of Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/from-rutgers-speaker-to-kristof-column-bogus-anti-israel-dog-rape-claim-goes-mainstream/) [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/media/protesters-demand-new-york-times-retract-controversial-dog-rape-article-fire-nicholas-kristof)

**Kristof's and the Times's defense.** Kristof defended the claim on Twitter, arguing that at least three medical journal articles document rectal injuries in humans from anal penetration by dogs. The Times said Kristof relied on "on-the-record accounts" and cited analyses documenting sexual violence by Israeli security forces, and rejected the notion of retraction. Kristof himself acknowledged in the column that there is no evidence Israeli leaders ordered rapes. [The Times of Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/from-rutgers-speaker-to-kristof-column-bogus-anti-israel-dog-rape-claim-goes-mainstream/) [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/media/protesters-demand-new-york-times-retract-controversial-dog-rape-article-fire-nicholas-kristof)

**Aftermath.** By mid-June, the Times had opened a separate investigation into Kristof for reportedly writing about individuals who had donated to his 2021 Oregon gubernatorial campaign without disclosing it — unrelated to the dog story, but piled onto the credibility questions. [The Jewish Chronicle](https://www.thejc.com/news/usa/nyt-israeli-rape-dog-nicholas-kristof-disclose-donors-k3xxovzh)

That's the deal. It's one of those stories where the underlying, broader allegation (sexual abuse in Israeli detention) has some independent documentation from human rights groups, but Kristof attached it to a specific claim that most experts consider biologically implausible and that traces back to a single advocacy source — which is why even people sympathetic to Palestinian prisoners' accounts winced at the column.
