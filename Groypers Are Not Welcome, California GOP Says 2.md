The California Republican Party is imposing what it calls a “vetting process” on candidates in local elections to block the influence of far-right influencer Nick Fuentes within the party.

According to a memo dated February 19 that was sent to all 58 of California’s county Republican parties and seen by *The Free Press*, party leaders should “refrain from recruiting, supporting, or endorsing candidates” who “espouse,” “promote,” or “campaign” on Fuentes’s ideas. The memo also instructed local GOP leadership to “review organizational bylaws” and ensure that those bylaws empower leaders to purge members aligned with Fuentes.

“The ideology is characterized as White Nationalist; hostile to constitutional, limited-government conservatism; highly critical of MAGA and President Trump; and opposing of the civil rights of women, gays, and racial minorities,” said the memo, which has not been previously reported.

Fuentes’s racist and antisemitic livestream rants rack up [millions of views](https://www.thefp.com/p/nick-fuentes-plan-to-infiltrate-politics), and he told listeners last fall that a nonprofit group he founded in 2020 had begun revamping to “infiltrate politics” and “guide people and help them understand who to vote for” in the upcoming midterm elections.

While Republican leaders across America have condemned Fuentes, 27, and occasionally [castigated or rebuked](https://www.texastribune.org/2024/02/10/texas-republican-party-antisemite-resolution-defend-liberty-fuentes/) Republicans who associate with him, California’s memo is the first known blanket crackdown on candidates who espouse Fuentes’s ideology, known as “groyperism.”

Many people in the national conservative movement have tolerated or even encouraged Fuentes as he moved from the irrelevant fringe toward the mainstream. Tucker Carlson interviewed Fuentes in October, triggering an explosion inside the Heritage Foundation when the think tank’s president [defended Carlson](https://www.thefp.com/p/the-meltdown-at-heritage-tucker-carlson-nick-fuentes). President Donald Trump has [distanced himself](https://www.thefp.com/p/the-meltdown-at-heritage-tucker-carlson-nick-fuentes) from Fuentes in the past but not explicitly condemned him.

“There was a complete void in leadership at the national level” about the rise of Groyperism, Elizabeth Barcohana, the Jewish engagement chair of the California Republican Party, told me. “That has more to do with the culture on the right of not fighting each other.... They just don’t know how to handle it, not that they don’t see it as a problem.”

The party cannot stop candidates from running as Republicans, but it can limit their chances by withholding an endorsement or supporting alternative candidates. In California, primary races are open to all parties, and any Republican that advances to the general, or the otherwise top-performing Republican, can become a delegate to the state party. Delegates can vote on endorsements, platforms, and leadership. Just 25 percent of [registered voters](https://www.ppic.org/publication/california-voter-and-party-profiles/) in California are Republicans, compared with 45 percent who are Democrats.

The memo sent to California’s county Republican parties said that there is an “organized effort” for Fuentes-aligned candidates to run in uncompetitive elections as the sole Republican in order “to eventually influence or take over the Party from within.”

Self-identified Groyper Kyle Langford was a [Republican candidate](https://www.ncronline.org/news/trad-catholic-running-california-governor-hes-also-nazi-sympathizer) for governor and polling at 2 percent when he switched to run for Congress as a Democrat. The California Republican Party passed a resolution condemning his “candidacy, rhetoric, and conduct” and said “that there is [no place in our party](https://cagop.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Resolutions-Report-Long.pdf) for antisemites, white supremacists, Holocaust deniers, or any individual who aligns with the hatred seen on today’s college campuses.”

Feitosa sponsored what became known as the “Groyper booth” at the Turning Point USA conference in December and was running in the heavily Democratic 30th Congressional District. The state GOP refused to endorse him and instead [recruited another candidate](https://www.jns.org/california-republicans-strategize-to-limit-antisemitic-influence-in-key-congressional-race/) to counter Feitosa.

“This was really more about heading it off at the pass, more than trying to squash a big problem in California,” said John Park, vice chairman of the California GOP. The vetting process outlined in the memo “is something they should be doing anyway,” he added.

Sam Markstein, the national political director of the Republican Jewish Coalition, said that “this is a battle that we must continue to fight and win, and we applaud the CAGOP for their efforts.” He added, “A sovereign nation without borders is no nation at all, and a conservative movement without borders is no conservatism.”
