When Vice President J.D. Vance arrives Saturday in Islamabad for talks with the remnants of Iran’s regime, he will be negotiating with what the U.S. government believes is a five-man senior council that is effectively running the Islamic Republic, according to current and former U.S. officials.

This is the “new regime” that President Donald Trump pledged on [Truth Social](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116368825638596650) “to work closely with” in the coming days to negotiate the removal of more than 900 pounds of highly enriched uranium. This group, Trump says, is who is running Iran and making the decisions, and it is they who have agreed to end threats to international shipping in the Strait of Hormuz, despite the ongoing drone and missile fire that followed the announcement of the ceasefire, and the countervailing statements from official Iranian sources about what the ceasefire framework entails.

The council includes:

The speaker of Iran’s legislature, the Majles, [Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-war-2026-trump-deadline-latest-news/card/iran-s-ghalibaf-says-cease-fire-talks-meaningless-after-violations-VhreaBT4Cnx03m52eJAo) —a pilot and former officer in the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) with a reputation for violence and corruption. One former U.S. intelligence official told *The Free Press* that Ghalibaf has boasted of personally beating up student protesters in the 1999 university demonstrations.

Foreign Minister [Abbas Araghchi](https://x.com/araghchi/status/2041655156215799821), the wily diplomat who publicly announced the two-week ceasefire with Trump on Tuesday. He played an important role in the regime’s successful nuclear negotiations with the Barack Obama administration and led the Iranian side in talks with Trump’s envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner that preceded the war.

Secretary of the Supreme National Security Council [Mohammad Baqer Zolqadr](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/mohammad-baqer-zolqadr-appointed-secretary-irans-top-security-body-presidential-2026-03-24/), a former IRGC commander who replaced Ali Larijani after he was targeted and killed by an Israeli air strike last month. The former supreme leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, delegated most governing authority to Larijani before the current war.

IRGC commander [Ahmad Vahidi,](https://www.interpol.int/ar/1/1/2009/INTERPOL-statement-clarifying-its-role-in-case-involving-Iranian-minister-wanted-by-Argentina) a former defense minister in the late 2000s who was charged in an Argentine court for his role in the 1994 bombing of a Jewish cultural center in Buenos Aires. In 2007, Interpol issued a “ [red notice](https://www.i24news.tv/en/news/middle-east/iran-eastern-states/artc-iranian-defense-minister-vahidi-wanted-for-1994-buenos-aires-amia-bombing) ” seeking the arrest of Vahidi for his connection to that incident.

President [Masoud Pezeshkian](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/irans-president-says-israeli-strikes-lebanon-render-negotiations-meaningless-2026-04-09/), a relatively weak politician who is said to have been bullied by the Revolutionary Guards and has warned the regime that Iran is facing a fiscal calamity if it does not receive economic relief soon. The London-based *Iran International* has reported that Pezeshkian has [privately warned](https://www.iranintl.com/en/202604074692) senior officials that the IRGC’s recklessness may ruin the chances of a successful ceasefire.

It is clear that Iran’s regime has survived a brutal five-week war led by the two most capable air forces in the world.

Current and former U.S. administration officials say that the five-man leadership council was appointed by the current supreme leader and son of the last one, Mojtaba Khamenei. He has not been seen in public since the war started on February 28, and U.S. officials say that he is still recovering from serious injuries caused by a U.S.-Israeli bombing attack. “He is alive,” a U.S. defense official said. “He is not involved in the day-to-day decisions for the regime. He has delegated that responsibility.”

This new five-man council has reestablished a degraded kind of command and control for the regime’s security agencies, military, and regional proxies, according to the U.S. assessment. This means that agreements reached in Islamabad can reasonably be expected to be observed by Iranian forces and proxies. That said, the regime is not giving specific orders, but rather general guidelines, to front-line commanders. These commands take more time to reach the officers in the field and proxies in Lebanon, Yemen, and Iraq than they would have under the regime’s prewar communications structure.

It’s still unclear who will represent the Iranian delegation in Islamabad, or even if the talks will go forward. Iran has complained that the ceasefire should also include a halt to Israel’s military campaign in southern Lebanon against Iran’s proxy, Hezbollah. Vance on Wednesday said the ceasefire does not include the [fighting in Lebanon](https://x.com/nicksortor/status/2041965290687209623), but also indicated that Israel may be willing to stop the campaign.

This led some of Iran’s ruling council to harden the regime’s public positions. Ghalibaf [wrote on X](https://iranwire.com/en/news/151014-qalibaf-lebanon-is-inseparable-from-the-ceasefire-we-will-respond-to-fire-with-fire/) Thursday that “Lebanon and the entire Axis of Resistance, as allies of Iran, are an inseparable part of the ceasefire.” Pezeshkian [echoed that sentiment](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/irans-president-says-israeli-strikes-lebanon-render-negotiations-meaningless-2026-04-09/), saying Thursday that without a ceasefire in Lebanon, negotiations in Islamabad would be “meaningless.” By Thursday afternoon, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announced that Israel would begin negotiations with the Lebanese government, in response to that country’s call for a ceasefire.

It’s still unclear whether Araghchi will arrive in Islamabad and will represent the regime in the talks that begin this weekend. Iran’s ambassador to Pakistan on Thursday [posted and then deleted](https://x.com/JoumannaTV/status/2042161533774692477) a message on X that confirmed Araghchi and the delegation would arrive for talks on Thursday evening.

But it is clear that Iran’s regime has survived a brutal five-week war led by the two most capable air forces in the world, the U.S. and Israel. Despite the fact that the new supreme leader has not been seen in public, he has made arrangements that have kept the regime more or less functional.

“I don’t think the selection of Mojtaba changes the way the regime operates from before he was announced,” Reuel Gerecht, a former CIA Iran targeting officer told *The Free Press*. “It’s a consensual leadership, it will remain a consensual leadership.”

Gerecht does not see much of a chance for reaching a comprehensive ceasefire agreement anytime soon. “I think there is zero chance for a larger agreement. Compromise would compromise the internal position of the current leadership in Iran,” he said. But he also thinks that could change if Trump persuaded his Iranian interlocutors that he would be willing to open the Strait of Hormuz with the U.S. military. “If the president is willing to do what is necessary to open the strait, if Trump is willing to commit the resources to open the strait, then the Iranian position would collapse,” Gerecht said. “Right now we have signaled to the Iranians we are not willing to do that.”
