***Pink Flamingos*** is a 1972 American [independent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Independent_film "Independent film") [black comedy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_comedy "Black comedy") [crime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crime_film "Crime film") film [^1] by [John Waters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Waters "John Waters").[^4] It is part of what Waters has labelled the "Trash Trilogy", which also includes *[Female Trouble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Female_Trouble "Female Trouble")* (1974) and *[Desperate Living](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desperate_Living "Desperate Living")* (1977).[^4] The film stars the countercultural [drag queen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drag_queen "Drag queen") [Divine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divine_\(performer\) "Divine (performer)") as a criminal living under the name of Babs Johnson, who is proud to be "the filthiest person alive". While living in a trailer with her mother Edie ([Edith Massey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edith_Massey_\(actress\) "Edith Massey (actress)")), son Crackers (Danny Mills), and companion Cotton ([Mary Vivian Pearce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mary_Vivian_Pearce "Mary Vivian Pearce")), Divine is confronted by the Marbles ([David Lochary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Lochary "David Lochary") and [Mink Stole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mink_Stole "Mink Stole")), a pair of criminals envious of her reputation who try to outdo her in filth. Characters caught in their feud include the Marbles’ sadistic butler Channing ([Channing Wilroy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Channing_Wilroy "Channing Wilroy")), their spy Cookie ([Cookie Mueller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cookie_Mueller "Cookie Mueller")), and The Egg Man ([Paul Swift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Swift "Paul Swift")), an egg salesman whom Edie is infatuated with. The characters engage in several grotesque, bizarre, and explicitly crude situations, and upon the film's re-release in 1997 it was rated [NC-17](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NC-17 "NC-17") by the [MPAA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MPAA "MPAA") "for a wide range of perversions in explicit detail". It was filmed in the vicinity of [Baltimore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltimore "Baltimore"), Maryland, where Waters and most of the cast and crew grew up.

Displaying the tagline "An exercise in [poor taste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taste_\(sociology\) "Taste (sociology)") ", *Pink Flamingos* is notorious for its "outrageousness", [nudity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nudity_in_film "Nudity in film"), [profanity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Profanity "Profanity"), and "pursuit of [frivolity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frivolity "Frivolity"), [scatology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scatology "Scatology"), [sensationology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sensationalism "Sensationalism") \[*[sic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "Sic")*\] and skewed [epistemology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epistemology "Epistemology") ".[^5] It features a "number of increasingly revolting scenes" that center on [exhibitionism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exhibitionism "Exhibitionism"), [voyeurism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voyeurism "Voyeurism"), [sodomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodomy "Sodomy"), [masturbation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Masturbation "Masturbation"), [gluttony](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gluttony "Gluttony"), [vomiting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vomiting "Vomiting"), [rape](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rape "Rape"), [incest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incest "Incest"), [murder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murder "Murder"), [animal cruelty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cruelty_to_animals "Cruelty to animals"), [cannibalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_cannibalism "Human cannibalism"), [zoophilia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoophilia "Zoophilia"), [castration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Castration "Castration"), [foot fetishism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foot_fetishism "Foot fetishism"), and concludes, to the accompaniment of " [How Much Is That Doggy in the Window?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/\(How_Much_Is\)_That_Doggie_in_the_Window%3F "(How Much Is) That Doggie in the Window?")", with Divine's [consumption of dog feces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coprophagia "Coprophagia") – "The real thing!" narrator Waters assures the viewers. The film is considered a preliminary exponent of [abject art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abjection#In_art "Abjection").[^6]

The film, at first semi-clandestine, has received a warm reception from film critics and, despite being [banned](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Film_censorship "Film censorship") in several countries and even in some parts of the United States, became a [cult film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cult_film "Cult film") in subsequent decades. In 2021, the film was selected for preservation in the United States [National Film Registry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Film_Registry "National Film Registry") by the [Library of Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_of_Congress "Library of Congress") as being "culturally, historically, or aesthetically significant".[^7]

## Plot

Notorious criminal [Divine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divine_\(performer\) "Divine (performer)") lives under the pseudonym "Babs Johnson" with her mother Edie, delinquent son Crackers, and traveling companion Cotton. They share a [trailer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mobile_home "Mobile home") on the outskirts of [Phoenix, Maryland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenix,_Maryland "Phoenix, Maryland"), next to a [gazing ball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yard_globe "Yard globe") and a pair of [plastic pink flamingos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plastic_flamingo "Plastic flamingo"). After learning that Divine has been dubbed "the filthiest person alive" by a [tabloid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tabloid_\(newspaper_format\) "Tabloid (newspaper format)") paper, jealous rivals Connie and Raymond Marble attempt to usurp her title.

The Marbles run a [black market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_market "Black market") baby ring: they kidnap young women, have them impregnated by their manservant Channing, and sell the babies to lesbian couples. The proceeds are used to finance pornography shops and a network of dealers selling heroin in inner-city elementary schools. Raymond also earns money by [exposing himself](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exhibitionism "Exhibitionism") —with a large [kielbasa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kielbasa "Kielbasa") sausage or turkey neck tied to his penis—to women and stealing their purses when they flee. Later in the film, one of Raymond's would-be targets, a [trans woman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trans_woman "Trans woman"), thwarts his scheme by exposing her breast, which excites him, and then showing her penis and scrotum, causing Raymond to flee in shock.

The Marbles enlist a spy, Cookie, to gather information about Divine by dating Crackers. Cookie is raped by Crackers while he tortures and [crushes a live chicken between them](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crush_fetish "Crush fetish") as Cotton [looks on through a window](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voyeurism "Voyeurism"). Cookie then informs the Marbles about Babs' identity, her whereabouts, and her family—as well as her upcoming birthday party.

The Marbles send a box of human feces to Divine as a birthday present with a card addressing her as "Fatso" and proclaiming themselves "the filthiest people alive". Worried that her title has been seized, Divine declares that whoever sent the package must die. While the Marbles are gone, Channing dresses in Connie's clothes and imitates his employers' overheard conversations. When the Marbles return home, they are outraged to find Channing mocking them while wearing Connie's clothes, so they fire him and lock him in his "room" (a closet) while they head out to do evil deeds until they can return and throw him out for good after searching his stuff.

The Egg Man, who delivers eggs to Edie daily, confesses his love for her and proposes marriage, which she accepts. The Marbles arrive at the trailer to spy on Divine's birthday party. The birthday gifts include [poppers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poppers "Poppers"), [fake vomit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fake_vomit "Fake vomit"), lice shampoo, a pig's head, and a meat cleaver. Entertainers feature a topless woman with a snake act and a contortionist who flexes his [prolapsed anus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rectal_prolapse "Rectal prolapse") in rhythm to the song " [Surfin' Bird](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surfin%27_Bird "Surfin' Bird") ". Disgusted by the outrageous party, the Marbles anonymously contact the police, but Divine and her guests ambush the officers, hack up their bodies with the meat cleaver, and [eat them](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_cannibalism "Human cannibalism"). Afterwards, Edie and The Egg Man are wed, and he carts her off in a wheel barrow.

Divine and Crackers head to the Marbles' house, where they lick and rub the furniture, which excites them so much that Divine [fellates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oral_sex "Oral sex") Crackers. They find Channing and he begs to be released, but they are not sympathetic and force him to show them the two pregnant women held captive in the basement. Divine and Crackers free the women and hand them a large knife to deal with the tied-up Channing, stating they can kill him and Divine will do so herself if they aren't interested. The women then use the knife to [emasculate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emasculation "Emasculation") Channing.

The Marbles burn Divine's beloved trailer to the ground; when they return home, their furniture—cursed by being licked by Divine and Crackers—"rejects" them, the cushions flying up and throwing them to the floor when they try to sit down. They also find that Channing has bled to death from his emasculation and the two women have escaped.

After finding the remains of their burned-out trailer, Divine, Crackers, and Cotton return to the Marbles' home, kidnap them at gunpoint, and bring them to the arson site. Divine calls the local tabloid media to witness the Marbles' trial and execution. Divine holds a [kangaroo court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kangaroo_court "Kangaroo court") and convicts the bound-and-gagged Marbles of "first-degree stupidity" and "assholism". Cotton and Crackers recommend a sentence of execution, so the Marbles are tied to a tree, coated in [tar and feathers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarring_and_feathering "Tarring and feathering"), and shot in the head by Divine.

Divine, Crackers, and Cotton enthusiastically decide to move to [Boise, Idaho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boise,_Idaho "Boise, Idaho"). Spotting a small dog [defecating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defecation "Defecation") on the sidewalk, Divine scoops up the feces with her hand and [puts them in her mouth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coprophagia "Coprophagia") —proving, as the [voice-over](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voice-over "Voice-over") narration by Waters states, that Divine is "not only the filthiest person in the world, but she is also the filthiest actress in the world".

## Production

### Development

Divine's friend Bob Adams described the trailer set as a "hippie commune" in Phoenix, Maryland, and noted that their living quarters were in a farmhouse without hot water. Adams added that ultimately Divine and Van Smith decided to sleep at Susan Lowe's home in Baltimore, and that they would awake before dawn to apply Divine's makeup before being driven to the set by Jack Walsh. "Sometimes Divy would have to wait out in full drag for Jack to pull the car around from back, and cars full of these blue-collar types on their way to work would practically mount the pavement from gawking at him," [^8] Adams said.

Divine's mother, Frances, later said she was surprised that her son was able to endure the "pitiful conditions" of the set, noting his "expensive taste in clothes and furniture and food".[^9]

John Waters told an interviewer "I was high when I wrote this movie. I was NOT high when I filmed it."

### Filming

[Principal photography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Principal_photography "Principal photography") began October 1, 1971 and ended January 12, 1972. Shot on a budget of only $12,000, *Pink Flamingos* is an example of Waters' style of [low-budget filmmaking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-budget_film "Low-budget film") inspired by New York [underground filmmakers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Underground_film "Underground film") like [Kenneth Anger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenneth_Anger "Kenneth Anger"), [Andy Warhol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andy_Warhol "Andy Warhol"), and brothers [Mike](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mike_Kuchar "Mike Kuchar") and [George Kuchar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Kuchar "George Kuchar").[^5] Stylistically, it takes its cues from "exaggerated seaport [ballroom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ball_culture "Ball culture") [drag-show pageantry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drag_pageantry "Drag pageantry") and antics" with "classic '50s rock-and-roll [kitsch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kitsch "Kitsch") classics".[^5] Waters' idiosyncratic style – also characterized by its "homemade [Technicolor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technicolor "Technicolor") " look, the result of high amounts of indoor paint and make-up – was dubbed the " [Baltimore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltimore "Baltimore") aesthetic" by art students at [Providence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island "Providence, Rhode Island").[^5]

### Post-production

Waters' rough editing added "random [Joel-Peter Witkin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joel-Peter_Witkin "Joel-Peter Witkin") -esque scratches and [Stan Brakhage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stan_Brakhage "Stan Brakhage") - [moth-wing-like](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mothlight "Mothlight") dust marks" to the film, apart from sound delays between [shots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shot_\(filmmaking\) "Shot (filmmaking)").[^5] Waters has stated that [Armando Bo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armando_Bo "Armando Bo") 's 1969 Argentine film *[Fuego](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuego_\(1969_film\) "Fuego (1969 film)")* influenced not only *Pink Flamingos*, but his other films: "If you watch some of my films, you can see what a huge influence *Fuego* was. I forgot how much I stole.... Look at Isabel's makeup and hairdo in *Fuego*. Dawn Davenport, Divine's character in *[Female Trouble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Female_Trouble "Female Trouble")*, could be her exact twin, only heavier. Isabel, you inspired us all to a life of cheap exhibitionism, exaggerated sexual desires and a love for all that is trash-ridden in cinema." [^10]

## Release

*Pink Flamingos* had its world premiere on March 17, 1972, at a screening sponsored by the Baltimore Film Festival. The event was held on the campus of the [University of Baltimore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Baltimore "University of Baltimore"), where it sold out for three successive shows. The film had aroused particular interest among fans of underground cinema following the success of *[Multiple Maniacs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_Maniacs "Multiple Maniacs")*, which had begun to be screened in cities such as New York, Philadelphia, and San Francisco.[^12]

Original [trailer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trailer_\(promotion\) "Trailer (promotion)") for *Pink Flamingos*, showing testimonials about the film from midnight moviegoers.

Being picked up by the then-small independent company [New Line Cinema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Line_Cinema "New Line Cinema"), *Pink Flamingos* was later distributed to [Ben Barenholtz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ben_Barenholtz "Ben Barenholtz"), the owner of the [Elgin Theater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elgin_Theater "Elgin Theater") in New York City. At the Elgin, Barenholtz had been promoting the [midnight movie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midnight_movie "Midnight movie") scene, primarily by screening [Alejandro Jodorowsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alejandro_Jodorowsky "Alejandro Jodorowsky") 's [acid western](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acid_western "Acid western") film *[El Topo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Topo "El Topo")* (1970), which had become a "very significant success" in "micro-independent terms". Barenholtz felt that being of an avant-garde nature, *Pink Flamingos* would fit in well with this crowd, subsequently screening it at midnight on Friday and Saturday nights.[^13] The original [trailer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trailer_\(promotion\) "Trailer (promotion)") used by New Line Cinema did not feature any footage from the actual film, and instead consisted almost entirely of interviews with filmgoers who had just seen the film. This trailer was included at the end the 25th anniversary re-release.[^14]

The film soon gained a cult following of filmgoers who came to the Elgin Theatre for repeat viewings, a group Barenholtz characterized as initially composed primarily of "downtown gay people, more of the hipper set", but after a while, Barenholtz noted that this group eventually broadened as the film also became popular with "working-class kids from New Jersey who would become a little rowdy". Many of these cult cinema fans learned all of the lines in the film, and recited them at the screenings, a phenomenon which later became associated with another popular midnight movie of the era, *[The Rocky Horror Picture Show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Rocky_Horror_Picture_Show "The Rocky Horror Picture Show")* (1975).[^15]

### Ban

The film was initially [banned](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_banned_films "List of banned films") in Switzerland and Australia, as well as in some provinces in Canada and Norway.[^16] It was eventually released uncut on VHS in Australia in 1984 with an X rating, but distribution of the video has since been discontinued. The 1997 version was cut by the distributor to achieve an R18+ after it was also refused classification. No submissions of the film have been made since, but it has been said that one of the reasons for which it was banned (as a film showing [actual sexual activity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unsimulated_sex "Unsimulated sex") cannot be rated X in Australia if it also features violence, so the highest a film such as *Pink Flamingos* could be rated is R18+) would now not apply, given that the depiction of actual sex was passed within the R18+ rating for *[Romance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romance_\(1999_film\) "Romance (1999 film)")* in 1999, two years following *Pink Flamingos* ' re-release.[^17] The film was originally rated as R18 by the [Classification Office](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classification_Office_\(New_Zealand\) "Classification Office (New Zealand)") in [New Zealand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Zealand "New Zealand") with no cuts, but was later refused classification on a re-submission in 2024, effectively banning the film.[^18]

### Home media

*Pink Flamingos* was released on [VHS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VHS "VHS") and [Betamax](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Betamax "Betamax") in 1981, and the re-release in 1997 from [New Line Home Video](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Line_Home_Entertainment "New Line Home Entertainment") became the second best-selling VHS for its week of release. The film was released in the John Waters Collection DVD box set along with the original NC-17 version of *[A Dirty Shame](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Dirty_Shame "A Dirty Shame")*, *[Desperate Living](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desperate_Living "Desperate Living")*, *[Female Trouble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Female_Trouble "Female Trouble")*, *[Hairspray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hairspray_\(1988_film\) "Hairspray (1988 film)")*, *[Pecker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pecker_\(film\) "Pecker (film)")*, and *[Polyester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polyester_\(film\) "Polyester (film)")*. The film was also released in a 2004 special edition with [audio commentaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audio_commentary "Audio commentary") and [deleted scenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deleted_scene "Deleted scene") as introduced by Waters in the 25th anniversary re-release ([*see below*](#Alternative_versions)). The film was released on [Blu-ray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blu-ray "Blu-ray") on June 28, 2022, by [the Criterion Collection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Criterion_Collection "The Criterion Collection"), featuring a new [4K](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/4K_resolution "4K resolution") restoration.[^19]

### Alternative versions

- The 25th anniversary re-release version contains a re-recorded music soundtrack, re-mixed for stereo, plus 15 minutes of [deleted scenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deleted_scene "Deleted scene") following the film,[^20] introduced by Waters. Certain musical excerpts used in the original version, including [Igor Stravinsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Igor_Stravinsky "Igor Stravinsky") 's *[The Rite of Spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Rite_of_Spring "The Rite of Spring")*, had to be removed and replaced in the re-release, since the [music rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_rights "Music rights") had never been cleared for the original release.[^11]
- Because of this film's explicit nature, it has been edited for content on many occasions throughout the world. In 1973, the U.S. screened version edited out most of the [fellatio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fellatio "Fellatio") scene, which was later restored on the 25th anniversary DVD. Canadian censors recently restored five of the seven scenes that were originally edited in that country. [Hicksville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hicksville,_New_York "Hicksville, New York") in [Long Island, New York](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_Island,_New_York "Long Island, New York"), banned the film altogether.[^16] The Japanese laserdisc version contains a blur superimposed over all displays of pubic hair. Prints also exist that were censored by the Maryland Censor Board.
- The first UK video release of *Pink Flamingos* in November 1981 (prior to [BBFC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Board_of_Film_Classification "British Board of Film Classification") video regulation requirements) was completely uncut. It was issued by Palace as part of a package of Waters films they had acquired from New Line Cinema. The package included *[Mondo Trasho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mondo_Trasho "Mondo Trasho")* (double-billed with *Sex Madness*), *[Multiple Maniacs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_Maniacs "Multiple Maniacs")* (double-billed with *Cocaine Fiends*), *[Desperate Living](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desperate_Living "Desperate Living")*, and *[Female Trouble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Female_Trouble "Female Trouble")*. The 1990 video re-release of *Pink Flamingos* (which required BBFC approval) was cut by three minutes and four seconds (3:04), the 1997 issue lost two minutes and forty-two seconds (2:42), and the pre-edited 1999 print lost two minutes and eight seconds (2:08).
- The 2009 Sydney Underground Film Festival screened the film in [Odorama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scratch_and_sniff#In_popular_culture "Scratch and sniff") for the first time, using scratch 'n' sniff cards similar to the ones used in Waters' later work *Polyester*.
- John Waters recast the film with children and rewrote the script to make it kid-friendly in a 2014 project, *Kiddie Flamingos*. The 74-minute video features children wearing wigs and costumes modeled on the originals and performing roles originated by Divine, Mink Stole, Edith Massey, and others. Waters has said the new version, filmed in one day with actors drawn mostly from friends' children, is in some ways more perverse than the original. The film was shown on a continuous loop in the Black Box gallery at the Baltimore Museum of Art from September 2016 through January 2017.[^21]

## Reception

On the [review aggregator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Review_aggregator "Review aggregator") website [Rotten Tomatoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes "Rotten Tomatoes"), 84% of 49 critics' reviews are positive, with an average rating of 7.4/10. The website's consensus reads: "Uproarious and appalling, *Pink Flamingos* is transgressive camp that proves as entertaining as it does shocking." [^22]

[Metacritic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metacritic "Metacritic"), which uses a [weighted average](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean "Weighted arithmetic mean"), assigned the film a score of 47 out of 100, based on 10 critics, indicating "mixed or average" reviews.[^23]

In the "Cult Movies" episode of the UK documentary series *[Mark Kermode's Secrets of Cinema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Kermode%27s_Secrets_of_Cinema "Mark Kermode's Secrets of Cinema")*, [Kermode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Kermode "Mark Kermode") admitted that *Pink Flamingos* is "one of the very few films that I have ever walked out of, when I first saw it as a teenager on a late-night double bill at the [Phoenix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenix_Cinema "Phoenix Cinema") in [East Finchley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Finchley "East Finchley") ".[^24]

[Roger Ebert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roger_Ebert "Roger Ebert") of *The Chicago Sun-Times* gave the film a rare no-stars rating when he reviewed it in 1997 for the 25-year anniversary re-release; he and [Gene Siskel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gene_Siskel "Gene Siskel") did not review the film on their TV show. Ebert described Waters as "a charming man" whose "talent had grown" over the years, and also speculated the controversial material of Waters's earliest low-budget films was an "understandable" method to "attract audiences". But Ebert also felt *Pink Flamingos* was perhaps best understood as "a weird kind of documentary" because the transgressive behaviors were not simulated, and added: "stars simply seem not to apply. \[*Pink Flamingos*\] should be considered not as a film but as a fact, or perhaps as an object." [^25]

Like the underground films from which Waters drew inspiration, which provided a source of community for pre- [Stonewall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stonewall_riots "Stonewall riots") [queer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queer "Queer") people, the film has been widely celebrated by many in the [LGBT community](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LGBT_community "LGBT community") [^26] and has been described as "early gay [agitprop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agitprop "Agitprop") filmmaking".[^5] [^27] *Pink Flamingos* is also considered an important precursor of [punk culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punk_subculture "Punk subculture").[^28]

Despite Waters having released similar films, such as *[Mondo Trasho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mondo_Trasho "Mondo Trasho")* and *[Multiple Maniacs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_Maniacs "Multiple Maniacs")*, it was *Pink Flamingos* that drew international attention.[^5] Like other underground films, it fed the rising popularity of [midnight movie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midnight_movie "Midnight movie") screenings, at which it generated a dedicated [cult following](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cult_following "Cult following") that carried the film for a 95-week run in New York City and ten consecutive years in Los Angeles.[^5] [^29] For its 25th anniversary, the film was re-released in 1997, featuring a post-film commentary by Waters in which he introduced and discussed deleted scenes,[^5] adding fifteen minutes of material.[^20] American " [New Queer Cinema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Queer_Cinema "New Queer Cinema") " director [Gus Van Sant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gus_Van_Sant "Gus Van Sant") has described the film as "an absolute classic piece of [American cinema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_cinema "American cinema"), right up there with *[The Birth of a Nation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Birth_of_a_Nation "The Birth of a Nation")*, *[Dr. Strangelove](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dr._Strangelove "Dr. Strangelove")*, and *[Boom!](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boom!_\(1968_film\) "Boom! (1968 film)")*" [^5] The [Library of Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_of_Congress "Library of Congress") inducted *Pink Flamingos* to the [National Film Registry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Film_Registry "National Film Registry") in December 2021.[^30]

## Influence

### Divine

The final scene in the film would prove particularly infamous, involving the character of Babs eating fresh dog feces; as Divine later told a reporter, "I followed that dog around for three hours just zooming in on its asshole" waiting for it to empty its bowels so that they could film the scene. In an interview not in character, Harris Milstead revealed that he soon called an emergency room nurse, pretending that his child had eaten dog feces, to inquire about possible harmful effects (there were none).[^31] The scene became one of the most notable moments of Divine's acting career, and he later complained of people thinking that "I run around doing it all the time. I've received boxes of dog shit – plastic dog shit. I have gone to parties where people just sit around and talk about dog shit because they think it's what I want to talk about". In reality, he remarked, he was not a [coprophile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coprophilia "Coprophilia") but only ate excrement that one time because "it was in the script".[^32]

Divine asked his mother, Frances Milstead, not to watch the film, a wish that she obliged. Several years before his death, Frances asked him if he had really eaten dog excrement in the film, to which he "just looked at me with that twinkle in his blue eyes, laughed, and said 'Mom, you wouldn't believe what they can do nowadays with trick photography.'" [^9]

### Cultural influence

The film has a reputation as a [midnight movie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midnight_movie "Midnight movie") classic [cult](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cult_following "Cult following") with audience participation similar to *[The Rocky Horror Picture Show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Rocky_Horror_Picture_Show "The Rocky Horror Picture Show")*.

- *The [Funday PawPet Show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funday_PawPet_Show "Funday PawPet Show")* holds what is called the "Pink Flamingo Challenge", in which the ending to the film is played to the audience while they eat a (preferably chocolate) confection. Videos of the show are forbidden from showing the clip, only the reaction of the audience.
- Theater patrons often received free "Pink Phlegmingo" vomit bags.

[Death metal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_metal "Death metal") band [Skinless](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skinless "Skinless") sampled portions of the Filth Politics speech for the songs "Merrie Melody" and "Pool of Stool", both on their second album, *[Foreshadowing Our Demise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreshadowing_Our_Demise "Foreshadowing Our Demise")*.

Joe Jeffreys, a drag historian, mentioned seeing in *Pink Flamingos* a poster for the documentary film *[The Queen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Queen_\(1968_film\) "The Queen (1968 film)")* (1968), featuring [Flawless Sabrina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flawless_Sabrina "Flawless Sabrina"), and stated that it influenced his career path to document the history of drag with the [Drag Show Video Verite](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Drag_Show_Video_Verite&action=edit&redlink=1 "Drag Show Video Verite (page does not exist)").[^33]

Modern zombie films like *[The Dead Don't Die](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Dead_Don%27t_Die_\(2019_film\) "The Dead Don't Die (2019 film)")* (2019) [^34] and *[Hemet, or the Landlady Don't Drink Tea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemet,_or_the_Landlady_Don%27t_Drink_Tea "Hemet, or the Landlady Don't Drink Tea")* (2023) [^35] were compared to *Pink Flamingos*.

### Cancelled sequel

Waters had plans for a sequel, titled *Flamingos Forever*. [Troma Entertainment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troma_Entertainment "Troma Entertainment") offered to finance the picture, but it was never made, as [Divine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divine_\(performer\) "Divine (performer)") refused to be involved, and [Edith Massey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edith_Massey_\(actress\) "Edith Massey (actress)") died in 1984.[^16]

After reading the script, Divine had refused to be involved as he believed that it would not be a suitable career move, for he had begun to focus on more serious, male roles in films like *[Trouble in Mind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trouble_in_Mind_\(film\) "Trouble in Mind (film)")*. According to his manager, Bernard Jay, "What was, in the early seventies, a mind-blowing exercise in Poor Taste, was now, we both believed, sheer Bad Taste. Divi\[ne\] felt the public would never accept such an infantile effort in shock tactics some fifteen years later and by people fast approaching middle age." [^36]
