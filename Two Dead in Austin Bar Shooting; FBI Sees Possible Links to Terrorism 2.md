Austin Police Chief Lisa Davis said a man armed with a pistol opened fire from his SUV and shot at people on a patio and in front of a bar around 2 a.m. local time.

The man then drove to another street, exited the vehicle with a rifle and started shooting at people walking by, Davis said at a news briefing Sunday. Police officers fatally shot him at an intersection, Davis said.

Alex Doran, the acting special agent in charge of the Federal Bureau of Investigation in San Antonio, said it was too early to know the shooter’s motive. Still, he said at the news briefing, “There were indicators on the subject and in his vehicle that indicate potential nexus to terrorism.”

Doran declined to say what those indicators were. The FBI didn’t respond to a request for further comment Sunday.

Austin Police Department later identified the suspect as 53-year-old Ndiaga Diagne.

Diagne entered the United States on a B-2 tourist visa in March 2000, federal officials said. In 2006, he became a lawful permanent resident through marriage to an American citizen. He was naturalized as a U.S. citizen on April 5, 2013.

Two victims were killed in Sunday’s shooting, said Robert Luckritz, the chief of emergency medical services in Austin and Travis County. He said 14 people were taken to hospitals where three remain in critical condition.

Davis said the shooter had driven around the block several times before putting on his flashers, rolling down his window and opening fire at the bar on West Sixth Street, a popular stretch of nightclubs several blocks west of the famed Sixth Street nightlife district. The area is usually packed with thousands of revelers and police on patrol.

Two people were killed and 14 were injured in a shooting in downtown Austin, Texas, early Sunday that authorities said might be connected to terrorism. The shooter was also killed.
