American Internet entrepreneur (born 1977)

**Sam Yagan** (born April 10, 1977) is an American [Internet entrepreneur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_entrepreneur "Internet entrepreneur") best known as the co-founder of [OkCupid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OkCupid "OkCupid").<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-time100-2">[2]</a></sup> [*Time*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_\(magazine\) "Time (magazine)") named Yagan [one of the 100 most influential people in the world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_100 "Time 100") in 2013.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-time100-2">[2]</a></sup> He is the co-founder and managing director of Corazon Capital.

## Family and education

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sam_Yagan&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Family and education")\]

Yagan is the son of Syrian immigrants, Al and Dr. Haifa Yagan,<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and grew up in [Bourbonnais, Illinois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bourbonnais,_Illinois "Bourbonnais, Illinois"); he studied at [Bradley-Bourbonnais Community High School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bradley-Bourbonnais_Community_High_School "Bradley-Bourbonnais Community High School"), [Illinois Math and Science Academy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illinois_Math_and_Science_Academy "Illinois Math and Science Academy"), [Harvard College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_College "Harvard College"), and eventually [Stanford Graduate School of Business](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_Graduate_School_of_Business "Stanford Graduate School of Business").<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Bourbonnais-5">[5]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Stanford-6">[6]</a></sup>

Yagan holds a bachelor's degree in Applied Mathematics and Economics from Harvard University and an MBA from [Stanford University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_University "Stanford University"), where he earned distinction as a Siebel Scholar, an Arjay Miller Scholar, and the Henry Ford Scholar, the award granted to each class's valedictorian.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> His brother Danny Yagan is an economics professor at the [University of California, Berkeley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley "University of California, Berkeley").<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> His wife Jessica Droste Yagan is the CEO of Impact Engine, an [impact investing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Impact_investing "Impact investing") fund.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>

In 1999, during his senior year at Harvard, Yagan and his classmates, Chris Coyne, Max Krohn, and [Christian Rudder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Rudder "Christian Rudder") started TheSpark.com,<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Ick-11">[11]</a></sup> which would later be known as [SparkNotes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SparkNotes "SparkNotes").<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2000, Yagan and partners sold SparkNotes to iTurf for $30 million,<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Nerd1-13">[13]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Matchmaking2-14">[14]</a></sup> the partners then helped iTurf sell the site again to [Barnes & Noble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barnes_%26_Noble "Barnes & Noble"). in 2001.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Matchmaking2-14">[14]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Ride-15">[15]</a></sup> After the sale, Yagan then stayed at Barnes & Noble for a year.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Lucky-16">[16]</a></sup>

[eDonkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EDonkey_network "EDonkey network") was a part of MetaMachine Inc and was a peer-to-peer (P2P) file-sharing network. Yagan was the CEO of MetaMachine Inc.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Kicks2-17">[17]</a></sup> As the developer of eDonkey, Yagan testified before the [Senate Judiciary Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Senate_Committee_on_the_Judiciary "United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary") expressing a need for balance between innovation and intellectual property.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>

In 2006, MetaMachine, Yagan, and founder Jed McCaleb agreed to pay $30 million to avoid potential copyright infringement lawsuits brought by the [RIAA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America "Recording Industry Association of America").<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Kicks2-17">[17]</a></sup> In accordance with the agreement, MetaMachine, Yagan and McCaleb agreed to discontinue distribution of its software as well as to deprecate previous copies of its software.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Settles-19">[19]</a></sup>

In 2003, Yagan again teamed up with his Harvard classmates Chris Coyne, Max Krohn, and Christian Rudder to found online dating website [OkCupid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OkCupid "OkCupid").<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Matchmaking2-14">[14]</a></sup> Yagan and partners designed OkCupid with a question-and-answer-based system.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Grandfather-20">[20]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Stats-21">[21]</a></sup> In 2011, Yagan led the sale of OkCupid to [Match Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Match_Group "Match Group"), a subsidiary of [IAC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IAC_\(company\) "IAC (company)"), for $50 million.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Board2-22">[22]</a></sup> Yagan was the CEO of Match Group for three years and left at the end of 2015 before joining the company's public board as vice chairman.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Board1-23">[23]</a></sup> While Yagan was CEO, Match Group started [Tinder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tinder_\(app\) "Tinder (app)").<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Nerd2-24">[24]</a></sup>

In 2009, Yagan, Kelli Rhee, and Kapil Chaudhary co-founded Excelerate Labs with the financial backing from [Sandbox Industries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandbox_Industries "Sandbox Industries").<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Excelerate-25">[25]</a></sup> Excelerate Labs merged with [Techstars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Techstars "Techstars") in 2013.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Incubator1-26">[26]</a></sup>

In 2014, Yagan co-founded Corazon Capital with Steve Farsht.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Corazon1-27">[27]</a></sup>

In 2016, Yagan became the CEO of ShopRunner, an e-commerce network that provides two-day shipping across multiple merchants that was founded by [Michael Rubin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Rubin_\(businessman\) "Michael Rubin (businessman)").<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-ShopRunner-28">[28]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Rubin-29">[29]</a></sup> He led the sale of the company to [FedEx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FedEx "FedEx") in December 2020.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Forbes_2019_Shoprunner2-30">[30]</a></sup> Yagan shut down ShopRunner's office in [San Mateo, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Mateo,_California "San Mateo, California"), and established its headquarters in Chicago.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Chicago1-31">[31]</a></sup> He attributed this move to the labor market and ability to recruit.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Chicago2-32">[32]</a></sup>

Yagan has participated on the board for several companies including [Grindr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grindr "Grindr"),<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-SEC-33">[33]</a></sup> Match Group,<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Board3-34">[34]</a></sup> [Rush University Medical Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rush_University_Medical_Center "Rush University Medical Center"),<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Hospital-35">[35]</a></sup> [Start Early](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Start_Early "Start Early"),<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Start_Early_Board_of_Directors-36">[36]</a></sup> [Shiftgig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shiftgig "Shiftgig"),<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Board3-34">[34]</a></sup> [SpotHero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SpotHero "SpotHero"),<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Board3-34">[34]</a></sup> [Tinder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tinder_\(app\) "Tinder (app)"),<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Board3-34">[34]</a></sup> and Techstars.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Corazon2-37">[37]</a></sup>

[*Time*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_\(magazine\) "Time (magazine)") named Yagan [one of the 100 most influential people in the world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_100 "Time 100") in 2013.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-time100-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2011, Yagan was named to *[Crain's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crain%27s_Chicago_Business "Crain's Chicago Business")* "40 under 40" in Chicago.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>

Sam Yagan is married to his high school sweetheart, Jessica Droste Yagan.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-kapos-39">[39]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> They have three children.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-cst2015-1">[1]</a></sup> <sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Yagan and his wife started the Yagan Family Foundation.<sup><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Yagan#cite_note-Foundation-42">[42]</a></sup>
