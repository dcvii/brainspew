## Bottom line

- By **hull count and shipbuilding throughput**, China now has the world’s largest navy and the largest shipbuilding base. By the Pentagon’s 2024 report, the PLA Navy already had **over 370 battle-force ships**, with projections of **395 by 2025** and **435 by 2030**; much of that growth is in major surface combatants. The U.S. Navy, by contrast, had **296 battle-force ships** as of January 27, 2025. [U.S. Department of War+1](https://media.defense.gov/2024/Dec/18/2003615520/-1/-1/0/MILITARY-AND-SECURITY-DEVELOPMENTS-INVOLVING-THE-PEOPLES-REPUBLIC-OF-CHINA-2024.PDF)
- But **ship count is not the same thing as naval power**. On the conventional measures that matter most in wartime—carrier aviation, nuclear submarines, logistics, overseas basing, integrated command-and-control, live coalition operations, and real combat experience—the **United States remains the strongest navy in the world by a large margin**. China is the nearest challenger and is becoming a very serious regional and increasingly blue-water force, but it still does not match the U.S. in global reach or wartime experience. ## On the claim about “military-grade” Chinese hulls

- The useful distinction is this: many Chinese commercial yards are tied into **military-civil fusion**, and China is clearly building a merchant and auxiliary base that could support military mobilization. CSIS notes that China’s shipyards are producing very large numbers of **ro-ro vessels**, with **upwards of 200** potentially delivered from 2023 to 2026, and that this has possible military implications. That does **not** mean those ships are equivalent to purpose-built warships in survivability, sensors, damage control, magazine protection, or combat-system integration. It means China is building a much deeper **mobilization and sealift reserve** than most rivals. [ChinaPower Project+1](https://chinapower.csis.org/analysis/china-construct-ro-ro-vessels-military-implications/)
- So the stronger claim is not “China is secretly turning merchant ships into destroyers.” The stronger claim is that China has built an industrial ecosystem in which commercial and naval construction reinforce one another, giving it a major advantage in **surge capacity, repair capacity, and wartime transport potential**. [CSIS+1](https://www.csis.org/analysis/china-dominates-shipbuilding-industry?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

## Conventional measures of sea power

### 1\. Fleet size and industrial depth

- **China** now leads the world in naval construction tempo. That matters because naval war is an industrial contest as much as a tactical one. China’s shipbuilding base is vastly larger than that of the U.S. and Europe, and the Pentagon expects continued expansion in major combatants and submarines. [U.S. Department of War+1](https://media.defense.gov/2024/Dec/18/2003615520/-1/-1/0/MILITARY-AND-SECURITY-DEVELOPMENTS-INVOLVING-THE-PEOPLES-REPUBLIC-OF-CHINA-2024.PDF)
- **The U.S.** still has a larger and more sophisticated high-end force in several categories, but its shipbuilding base is under strain. CRS and CBO both describe the difficulty of reaching planned U.S. fleet goals because of industrial-base constraints and delays. [Every CRS Report+2Congressional Budget Office+2](https://www.everycrsreport.com/files/2025-01-31_RL32665_2ce77f6ce199e71515cfecc46da2845e9cdd6705.html)
- **Europe** does not compete on volume. France, the UK, Italy, Spain, and Germany build high-quality ships, but not at Chinese scale. Their navies are best understood as **elite-but-limited** forces. [Royal Navy+2defense.gouv.fr+2](https://www.royalnavy.mod.uk/organisation/surface-fleet)

### 2\. Power projection

- **The U.S. Navy is still unmatched**. It maintains **11 carrier strike groups**, with globally deployable carrier air wings and integrated escorts, and it combines that with nuclear attack submarines, amphibious groups, replenishment ships, prepositioning, and a worldwide base network. That is still the gold standard for maritime power projection. [SurfPac+2Airpac+2](https://www.surfpac.navy.mil/Ships/Carrier-Strike-Group-COMCARSTRKGRU-9/About/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- **China is improving rapidly** in far-seas operations. In 2025 it conducted more ambitious blue-water carrier activity, including simultaneous carrier operations in the Pacific and extensive operations beyond the First Island Chain. Still, China’s real comparative advantage remains strongest in a **Western Pacific contingency close to home**, where land-based missiles, aircraft, and sensors reinforce the fleet. [ChinaPower Project+2Jamestown Foundation+2](https://chinapower.csis.org/china-increased-military-activities-indo-pacific-2025/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- **France and the UK** are the only European navies with recurring carrier-based high-end power projection, though on a much smaller scale than the U.S. France’s **Clemenceau 25** five-month Indo-Pacific deployment and the UK’s 2025 carrier strike deployment show genuine expeditionary capability. Italy has also demonstrated serious reach with its Indo-Pacific carrier deployment. ### 3\. Submarines

- This is where **the U.S. retains a decisive edge**. Its attack submarines are nuclear-powered and built for blue-water hunting, land attack, ISR, mine warfare, SOF support, and escort of carrier groups. These are among the most potent conventional and nuclear instruments in any navy. [U.S. Navy](https://www.navy.mil/Resources/Fact-Files/Display-FactFiles/article/2169558/attack-submarines-ssn/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
- **China’s submarine force is large and still growing**. The Pentagon reported **6 SSBNs, 6 SSNs, and 48 diesel/AIP attack submarines**, with the force projected to reach **65 boats by 2025** and **80 by 2035**. Numerically that is formidable. Qualitatively, however, U.S. SSNs still generally hold the advantage in quieting, habitability, training ecosystem, and long-range global operations. The balance is much closer in China’s near seas than on the open ocean. [U.S. Department of War+1](https://media.defense.gov/2024/Dec/18/2003615520/-1/-1/0/MILITARY-AND-SECURITY-DEVELOPMENTS-INVOLVING-THE-PEOPLES-REPUBLIC-OF-CHINA-2024.PDF)

- **France** is the strongest European submarine operator because it combines SSBNs and SSNs; the UK also remains formidable underwater, though your prompt did not ask for a full Anglo-French deterrent comparison. ### 5\. Amphibious warfare, sealift, and staying power

- **China has built a serious amphibious and sealift base**, and this matters enormously for any Taiwan scenario. The bigger point is not only the number of assault ships, but the combination of naval amphibs, coast guard, maritime militia, and potentially mobilizable ro-ro shipping. - **The U.S. still has the better global logistics machine**.
