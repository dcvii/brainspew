### We have not retvrned enough.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Oqxb!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F94eb2033-a197-429d-a690-888e548aff4e_562x358.jpeg)

Discourse surrounding the “trad wife”, a woman who eschews a career for a singular focus on the home and her children, is nowadays impossible to ignore. She is not only in the algorithm, grinding fresh flour on camera \[flour mill discount code available\], she is also intensely scrutinized by media and alternately celebrated, lambasted, or eye-rolled by political and social commentators.

For many women, she’s offering a “retvrn” to a more primordial purpose, after decades of spiritually hollow The Future is Female girl-bossery. Conservatives agree, and say this is an admirable revival of the nuclear family model, to which they suggest we all rewind. Progressives warn that these trends denote a slide back into a Dark Ages patriarchy, one which will strip women of recently acquired rights.

Everyone here has it wrong. The conservatives yearn for a Cracker Barrel-style simulacrum of tradition. The progressives don’t recognize their own immediate ancestor. The “trad wife” and her “nuclear family” are modern inventions. They were temporary way stations along the long March of Progress, which is still ongoing. Were we to return to trad-wifery, we’d soon find ourselves right where we are at present.

To understand this, we must look back to the actual traditional form of the family, which predates industrialization, as Mary Harrington explains in her insightful book *[Feminism Against Progress](https://www.amazon.com/Feminism-against-Progress-Mary-Harrington/dp/1684514878)*.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!EXlW!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F12faac17-d422-4677-8c8d-70f7ae101a0b_580x437.jpeg)

This is the rural village family, perhaps the storybook Cave Family, rather than mid 20th century suburbia. Here, families are set up as multi-generational “enterprises” run largely from the home (or, rock cavern). Men and women co-manage small plots of land or run businesses on their property, like an inn or blacksmithing forge. Women make all manner of essential household products, like candles, bread and fabric, which can be traded and sold. They also take in mending, offer childcare, or perform other services that could be done at home. These tasks allowed them to care for children and the elderly in the midst of work. Looms, for instance, are elevated from the ground, which allows babies to play beneath them.

In this setting, you’ll find that many of the modern tenets of trad-wifery fail the sniff test of true tradition. Women have always worked, they just worked within their own property. The notion that the care and instruction of children is largely the concern of women is also not traditional. Infancy might have been their domain (with assistance from extended family) but sons worked with their father as soon they might walk, going into the fields or apprenticing a trade. Even the idea that women were setting out lavish, multi-course, freshly-made meals each day seems recent. Large stew pots can go for days!

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mwg8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffb872b0b-ba02-4796-be96-17f9b6332a93_364x309.jpeg)

Such arrangements enabled people to be substantially independent from both the external marketplace and the state, freedoms which both capitalism and communism find inconvenient.

The Industrial Revolution and the rise of factory-made goods destroyed artisanal production and broke up these minute familial enterprises. Economic activity now occurred almost exclusively outside the home. Families were uprooted from rural villages and headed into cities, where they lived far less communally and much more at the mercy of their external employers. Women’s domestic contributions dwindled, in the public eye, to cooking, shopping, cleaning and childcare. As marketplace participation became intertwined with personal value, women’s efforts seemed increasingly petty.

“The ploughman’s wife could point to yarn spun, cheeses made, animals well-tended, and so on: material output every bit as indispensable as her husband’s muddy toil in the field. But under the new regime ‘women’s work’ was, as \[Ivan\] Illich puts it, ‘not only unreported but impenetrable by the economic searchlight,’” says Harrington.

Here we mark the birth of the “stay-at-home mom” or “trad wife” whose identity is entirely children and housework, the first unfortunate stageof progressive modernity and not its anecdote.

Many at the time defended women’s increasingly domestic role. They were pilloried by Communists for peddling in “false consciousness” and propagandizing “patriarchal submission.” These same tactics occur today, with feminists lambasting “trad wives” whose propaganda “misleads women.”

But, as Harrington points out, this is a fatal misread. Those who attempted to protect a feminine “cult of domesticity” actually “sought to defend an ‘embedded’ pre-industrial domain of relational human activity, centered around the care of vulnerable children, that was difficult to valorise in market terms…”

#### Valkyries Storm the High Rises

Of course, as we know today, hearth and home defenders lost the battle. Not merely because of the scolding of progressives, but also because of the inherent mental anguish associated with this unnatural domestic life, relegated largely to suburbs. Women now had few extended family members to assist with tasks, were saddled with childcare beyond what they’d done for millennium, and their contributions had been lowered substantially in the eyes of the community.

By the 1960s some upper and middle-class women, grew increasingly insane in this unhealthy petri dish, as Camille Paglia explains in her essay “No Law in the Arena”:

“The predominant fact of modern sexual history is not patriarchy but the collapse of the old extended family into the nuclear family, an isolated unit that, in its present form, is claustrophobic and psychologically unstable…Marooned in the suburbs or barricaded in urban apartments, upwardly mobile families are frantically overscheduled and geographically transient, with few ties to neighbors and little sustained contact with relatives.”

Such agitation powered the second wave feminist movement, with texts like *The Feminine Mystique* outlining their dissatisfaction. Women campaigned for the right to participate in structural and economic power by engaging with the exterior workplace, just as men did, and decided to try and foist childcare onto another woman, just as men did, by outsourcing their children to daycares and nannies. (That last part kicked off a still unresolved guilt within upper-class liberal women for having farmed childcare onto POC wammen, still the subject of many a self-flagellating New Yorker article.)

At the end of World War II, only 10% of married women with children under the age of 6 held jobs or were looking for work. But, by 1985, nearly 50% of these young mothers were participating in the workforce. The pace of change tripled after 1950.

The challenges of balancing family life with working life soon became acute, given that women’s individual “progress” was not accompanied by equally progressive adjustments to public life. The struggle was encapsulated in the 1981 movie, *9-5*, which exposed the difficulties of being working women (several of them moms) subject to brutish male supervisors.

When our three heroines imprison their boss (he’s jailed at home to watch Days of Our Lives) and take command of the office, they’re finally free to remake the workplace in their preferred fashion. Their most ambitious redesign? Build an in-office daycare for their staff. They’re then given permanent executive positions after upper management discover that the women’s restructuring has “increased profits.”

From this, we glimpse how “women’s liberation” gave the expanding financialization and externalization of family life a nice varnish of rebellion. The moldy old “patriarchs” of yesteryear were replaced by a far less caring cast of overlords: the gender-neutral CEOs of global economy, for which women became merely interchangeable economic units. First, work was displaced from the home. Now, the home was to be absorbed into these exterior workspaces.

#### Back to the Stoned Age

Some 40 years after the debut of *9-5*, American working moms are still in a similar position as the film’s leads. The U.S. doesn’t offer any sort of federal parental leave and daycare is [tremendously expensive](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/child-care-costs-rent-lendingtree/).

Progressives are not interested in any sort of misguided “retvrn” but in the final completion of their Progressive Family project which stalled out in America in the 1970s. (A bipartisan bill in 1971 [attempted to fund](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/mar/24/childcare-daycare-cost-absurdly-expensive) a federally subsidized daycare system, but it was vetoed by President Nixon.) Their end goal is a socialist state such as Norway, which offers generous parental leave and subsidizes nearly 90% of daycare costs, with “free” healthcare and college to boot. The state, rather than the office, absorbs the family.

Ironically, however, progressives are yearning for a vision of the family nearly as antique as the Nuclear Family – one that will soon also be replaced.

The warning bell is sounding loudly. Norway is currently at 1.4 births per citizen. This is even worse than the U.S., which has 1.6. Oddly, that robust state support for family formation doesn’t actually seem to help much with the actual formation of families. Progressives argue that this is because women in these countries have more freedom to choose. But then — if having a family isn’t fundamentally all that important — why sell Americans on family benefits to begin with?

Even if you’re satisfied with the argument of “well, we should give moar benefits because that’s just part of being a heckin’ *good nation*!” it’s become impossible to ignore the massive problems of sub-replacement level fertility. Namely: how does a country pay for generous socialized benefits when there are fewer and fewer incomes to tax and more elderly to support? European countries have already attempted ever larger wealth taxes, finding [mediocre to downright awful results](https://calmatters.org/commentary/2026/02/wealth-taxes-europe-countries-california/).

A quick and dirty solution advanced by policy experts and politicians over the past 25 years or so has been to [massively import immigrants](https://worldmigrationreport.iom.int/what-we-do/world-migration-report-2024-chapter-3/europe) from other countries (with increasing disregard for the legality of their arrival). These new workers provide welcome labor, they argue, and boost the birth rate. Unfortunately, nations are now discovering that mass migration is perhaps not a great solution. Too many migrants are [draining socialized benefit programs](https://www.wsj.com/politics/policy/california-cuts-back-on-costly-immigrant-healthcare-70f2794d?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqeYkPcwQJxUVHhe0tpx7sfgUw2GGxsrC0C4lBtViR1muw0o_p573xTE7vmF9ak%3D&gaa_ts=69c6af0d&gaa_sig=rB3kbTLCGnSem1kminF86zv4CyUyMXkc0Jj2__1qZNmBX33URy616s2Js-Q5N7PNYa3mGquIG_7Kp2CKugGoXg%3D%3D), not propping them up. [Most arrivals](https://cis.org/Report/Fertility-Immigrants-and-Natives-United-States-2023) just end up with a similar birth rate.

Those that *do* have many more babies are generally not assimilating into the host culture of Western nations. They’re building cultural and religious enclaves that make “trad-wives” look positively libtarded. Allow such behavior to continue long enough, and paying for “parental” leave becomes immaterial, as the retrograde culture subsumes the host. Women won’t be allowed in the public sphere to even necessitate parental leave. You think I jest. Afghanistan, which just banned women from attending schools, has a fertility rate of 4.5.

Even if mass migration were to stop, there’s a [large gap](https://ifstudies.org/blog/the-lefts-family-problem-marriage-and-kids-cratering-among-liberal-young-adults) now between native liberals and the seriously conservative as to fertility. The Future May Be Mormon. I wrote [more about that here.](https://linguaignota.substack.com/p/evocation-of-a-fertility-cult) Although, we should note [the calculated rise of shows](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Secret_Lives_of_Mormon_Wives) focusing on pockets of degeneracy within Mormon communities, since they resist the efforts of global progress. Suffice to say, when Progress ends, so ends the Progressive Family project.

#### Fully-Automated-Luxury-Procreation

The alternative is that progress, well, must keep progressing. To boost birth rates, the Progressive Family could be replaced in the not-so-distant-future by Progress sans Family, with experts manipulating sperm and egg in a lab to produce the necessary numbers of citizens. Already you see hints of what this odd future will look like.

A Russian billionaire [has fathered over 100 children](https://www.wsj.com/world/pavel-durov-children-fertility-sperm-donation-3d6d5231?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqepsEckKC_dLfQtg6hc01VAJG-tU-eDKm6SGnqYtrcB1v_Op05zmT97Uw9iuKU%3D&gaa_ts=69b97be1&gaa_sig=naQH5FL_6bCPsGV0B2uwxNKdIgbihOz869w4JsNruEMI3cIbkW3mIBMaurVH6zGwV6yGxP3RlpUiAZAjYXw4wQ%3D%3D) using IVF. Elon Musk has [14 children from 4 different women](https://people.com/all-about-elon-musk-children-11678749), using a mixture of surrogacy and IVF. Alyssa Lui, the American ice-skating champion of the Winter Olympics, was born to a single Chinese father who used anonymous egg donation from American women to [sire five children](https://people.com/all-about-alysa-liu-dad-11896584). Her biological mother likely watched Alyssa skate with no idea it was her own child.

Commentators often compare this strange new world with that of the film *Gattaca*, but it is far worst. In *Gattaca*, children were at least created out of the prime genetic material of their own parents. In our world, offspring are increasingly forged from donor parts, with only partial (at best) genetic attachment to the parent that raises them. Orphanhood, once considered a tragedy of fate, is increasingly a quasi-default state for many children. Their creation is increasingly advanced by the government.

Such Frankensteining is egged on as much by feminists as it is tech bros. Harrington argues that feminism has largely seen nature as a “stalking horse” for patriarchy: “physiological constraints that we didn’t choose and that limit our ability to self-realize.”

Thus, Shulasmith Firestone in *The Dialectic of Sex: The Case for Feminist Revolution*, calls for the emancipation of women from the drudgery of pregnancy through artificial wombs. “Transfeminist” Sophie Lewis demands “gestational communism” where babies “are not the particular obligation of family units” but “universally thought of as anybody and everybody’s responsibility,” says Harrington. Once again, feminism runs cover for the insertion of the state, marketplace and technology into the family unit.

Faced with the prospects of either a collapse of all progress or a future in which children are untethered from their ancestral line, it’s understandable to ask where, precisely, we went so wrong.

As outlined, we too often pin our hopes upon much later versions of the family. Harrington is right to suggest that we turn our gaze much farther back, to the pre-industrial arrangements. None of that is meant to paint a utopian idyll. Agrarian life was and is exceptionally harsh. Indeed, it is just such harshness that makes people reluctant to abandon progress. If we were all really so desperate to escape modernity’s ills, the Amish already offer an alternative!

#### Reject Gerontocracy, Embrace Tradition

The aim for many is not a full-fledged “retvrn” but a revival of certain values which help us maintain the good of modernity, while avoiding the disastrous futures we’ve already discussed. Perhaps the most obvious starting point is to bring work back into the home.

Ironically, the cosplaying version of some trad wives we see on the internet provides a decent example. Women who garner income from viral videos making Goldfish crackers from scratch are working from home, while also raising children (that they also use for their content.) Progressives try to use this as a gotcha that these women aren’t “trad” but they’ve inadvertently emphasized what’s actually traditional about them.

Of course, peddling your children and homemaking to the internet isn’t palatable or effective for most (and probably not good for families long term). There are other ways to encourage the re-fusing of home and work.

A new working paper from Stanford found that nearly 300,000 additional children were born during the pandemic in the United States thanks to a (temporary) pivot to working from home. If both parents move from full time in the office to working at home at least one day a week, they will average about 0.5 extra children, pushing toward the “replacement” level of 2.1, [said](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/09/opinion/fertility-rate-baby-work-from-home.html) Stanford economist and author of the study Nicholas Bloom. This comes down to both opportunity (one does not conceive “through email” as the New York Times coverage quips) as well as a perception of increased parenting time.

Merely pushing employers to offer hybrid work could possibly reverse our existential “replacement” level problem, with little to no state expenditure. Here, we see that correcting our ills can sometimes make use of technology, rather than reject it outright.

Unfortunately, presumably conservative voices such as Elon Musk have not caught on to such solutions. Musk may be rightly concerned about disruptive migration and the need for boosted native fertility, but he also demands that his employees [work in the office.](https://www.npr.org/2022/06/01/1102513281/elon-musk-tesla-return-to-work) These sentiments about in-office work are shared by many on his side of the political aisle, including the current presidential administration who [mandated](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/return-to-in-person-work/) a return to in-office.

Even if these employees were all men supporting their wives at home with children, this would (again) just be a return to the unfortunate and temporary nuclear family model. It is further evidence that even the conservatives of the modern age are chasing an ineffectual mirage.

Other solutions become more controversial. A recent piece outlined the problem of [Total Boomer Luxury Communism](https://americanmind.org/salvo/what-is-total-boomer-luxury-communism/) in America. Contrary to the idea we’re a parsimonious “late-stage capitalist” hellscape, we offer extensive socialized benefits. We redistribute more income than communist China, it’s just that we overwhelmingly redistribute money from young workers to retirees.

The national debt is at an absurd level: $39 trillion and counting. Simply offering moar benefits to moar people just doesn’t feel sustainable, no matter how much people yell about taxing teh billionaires (and remember, socialist Euro countries already tried it). But, surely *some* adjustment to our priorities can be done. One small example:

Currently, retirees can draw a max of $5,181 a month in Social Security, totaling $62,172 a year. To qualify for this maximum social security amount you’d need to have made over $170,000 a year, *for 35 years*. Surely, someone who made such an income would have other retirement assets. Indeed, dare I say that they **should**, at least in the form of a paid-off living situation. Even if they didn’t, a single adult could subsist upon $50,000 a year. We can then redirect the $12,000 to subsidize a mother staying at home with her infant.

Lest you think this is cruel, consider our utopian northern neighbor Canada, where the combined maximum from the Canada Pension Plan and Old Age Security (rough SS equivalents) appears to be around $2,200 or $26,000 annually. Seniors drawing the maximum there are making 1/3 of what maximum-drawing Americans draw.

Perhaps more than the financial bonus, this redirection could help our collective spirits. We live in what is basically a geriatric, managed decline. Reprioritizing babies with the movement of money might perk us up.

Of course, none of this matters if people are not having the sex required for babies. Harrington has an audacious additional suggestion: the re-wilding of sex, by rejecting contraceptives. Their use, she argues, has stripped sex of its erotic danger.

Heterosexual sex has become akin to homosexual sex, which similarly lacks the organic risk of creating life and therefore must engineer its thrills. To compensate, those who do have sex these days are pursuing debased or ludicrous sexual behavior, from choking to puppy play.

Birth control has also cleaved women from her body and her sex. Rejecting the technology actually brings empowerment, since it renews our awareness of fertility cycles and the great sexual differences between man and woman.

Sex “with the real danger in it” allows us to “reclaim our sexual cycles, our capacity for eroticism, our atunement to our own bodies, and our right to refuse exploitative, loveless and degrading approaches. And in refusing this degrading parody…we can open ourselves up to better ones — not with The One, but with a one: someone who is willing to step up. Not to Big Romance, or the fantasy of perfect communion, but as far solidarity, intimacy, family and building a life together.”
