## Iran, U.S. Move Closer on Proposal to Restart Talks

## Mediators are pushing a short-term memo to guide monthlong talks amid key disputes over enrichment and sanctions relief, said people familiar with the matter.

![President Trump in the Oval Office.](https://images.wsj.net/im-40669315?width=620&height=413)

President Trump in the Oval Office. Evan Vucci/Reuters

Iran and the U.S. [are working with mediators](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/iran-u-s-move-closer-to-one-page-memo-to-restart-talks-LlqZMxUIRpVHj1inG8YI) on a one-page framework to restart negotiations aimed at ending the conflict and opening the Strait of Hormuz, with talks potentially beginning next week in Islamabad. The proposed monthlong process would address Iran’s nuclear program and sanctions relief, but major disagreements remain over uranium-enrichment limits, inspections and sequencing.

President Trump warned the [U.S. would resume bombing](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/trump-says-iran-deal-would-end-strikes-warns-of-return-to-bombing-if-talks-fail-Pf1m2Qk82v3W49jBe5wE) of Iran “at a much higher level and intensity” if Tehran doesn’t agree to a deal. [Oil prices, which had plunged](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/oil-prices-slide-on-optimism-for-diplomacy-NYQMAVDYgqDUAi7qsdup) more than 10% on hopes for a diplomatic breakthrough, recovered slightly after Trump said he was pausing a U.S. operation to guide commercial ships through the strait that had begun Monday.

The push to wrap up an agreement to end the war is coming from Trump's mid-May visit to China, [said a former U.S. diplomat](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/trump-wants-iran-war-done-before-china-visit-former-u-s-diplomat-says-6Py8VV4E5cRbEdWxok4W).

Trump said Wednesday that Iranian negotiators ["want to make a deal."](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/trump-says-iran-wants-to-make-a-deal-V21ymWt3KeKTZRiNJeSp)

Tehran [reiterated a warning](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/iran-warns-ships-against-crossing-strait-without-its-permission-5KpAZqicdXouC5axCJm3) to ships not to cross the strait without its permission.

### [Israel Strikes Beirut, Targeting Hezbollah Commander](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/israel-struck-beirut-targeting-hezbollah-commander-Tu5sNGH17RGn4hnbq1Vr)

![The aftermath of an airstrike in Beirut.](https://images.wsj.net/im-16778095?width=620&height=413)

The aftermath of an airstrike in Beirut. Marwan Naamani/ZUMA Press

The Israel Defense Forces carried out a strike in Beirut on Wednesday that targeted the commander of Hezbollah’s Radwan Force, according to a joint statement from Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and Defense Minister Israel Katz. “Israel’s long arm will reach every enemy and murderer,” the statement said.

Netanyahu and Katz also said that they are committed to bringing security to residents of northern Israel, saying, “This is how it’s done.” Shortly before the strike, Netanyahu said Israel is in continuous contact with the U.S., noting there is full coordination between the two countries because they share common goals, including the removal of Iran’s enriched uranium.

### [Iran Top Negotiator Warns of Economic Pressure as Primary Threat](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/iran-top-negotiator-warns-of-economic-pressure-as-primary-threat-URpS9wD6IdCMGoqHlJsx)

![Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf](https://images.wsj.net/im-78917581?width=620&height=413)

Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf Icana/Zuma Press

Iran’s parliament speaker and top negotiator Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf warned Wednesday that while further military attacks against the country remain possible, the more immediate threat is a campaign of economic pressure aimed at forcing Iran to yield. In an audio message to the public, cited by Iranian state media IRNA, Ghalibaf said the country had entered a “new phase” of war, accusing adversaries of using a maritime blockade and media pressure to weaken national unity.

“The enemy has pinned great hope on economic pressure,” he said, adding that Iranians “may give their lives, but will not surrender.” Ghalibaf also urged citizens to conserve resources as a form of resistance and called for broader public cooperation to withstand economic strain, framing endurance as central to Iran’s ability to weather the conflict.

### [Trump Wants Iran War Done Before China Visit, Former U.S. Diplomat Says](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/trump-wants-iran-war-done-before-china-visit-former-u-s-diplomat-says-6Py8VV4E5cRbEdWxok4W)

![President Trump and Chinese leader Xi Jinping at last year's summit in South Korea.](https://images.wsj.net/im-34932942?width=620&height=413)

President Trump and Chinese leader Xi Jinping at last year's summit in South Korea. Evelyn Hockstein/Reuters

The push to swiftly wrap up a deal to end the war is coming from President Trump's mid-May visit to Beijing, said Daniel Shapiro, a former deputy assistant secretary of defense for the Middle East.

Shapiro said a continuing war in the Middle East will improve China's leverage in talks on other issues with Washington, including the issue of Taiwan’s independence.

"Trump has a big incentive to get the war wrapped up before he visits Beijing next week. If the war is ongoing, he arrives in the position of a supplicant, seeking Xi's help to get Iran to accept his terms," Shapiro said, referring to Chinese leader Xi Jinping.

"An ongoing war in the Gulf also undercuts U.S. deterrence of China in the IndoPacific, and reinforces Xi's effort to portray the United States as a destabilizing force and China the responsible adult,” he added.

“If the war is over, Trump can focus on the economic deals he is most interested in. Of course, Iran understands these dynamics, so it gives the leverage to achieve a very modest deal,” said Shapiro, a former U.S. ambassador to Israel.

In a separate post on X, Shapiro said Trump has been trying to tamp down tensions with China by showing deference in delaying approval of a Taiwan arms sale, not backing up the Japanese prime minister in a verbal spat with China, and with a limited response to renewed Chinese aggression in the South China Sea.

Trump visits China on May 14-15.

### [Macron Condemns Strikes Against U.A.E., Ships in Call With Iranian President](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/macron-condemns-strikes-against-u-a-e-ships-in-call-with-iranian-president-cQL1tyDI10ZsvZPa0V8H)

![French President Emmanuel Macron](https://images.wsj.net/im-57397434?width=620&height=413)

French President Emmanuel Macron Vahram Baghdasaryan/PHOTOLURE/EPA/Shutterstock

French President Emmanuel Macron condemned Iran’s strikes against civilian infrastructure in the United Arab Emirates and several ships, and he called for full freedom of navigation in the Strait of Hormuz during a phone call on Wednesday with Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian.

“All parties must lift the blockade of the Strait immediately and unconditionally,” Macron said in a post on X about his conversation. “We must return permanently to the regime of full freedom of navigation that prevailed before the conflict.”

Macron also said the French-U.K. led international mission aimed at restoring freedom of navigation in the strait could help restore confidence among shipowners and insurers. French officials said the mission could start escorting ships if the U.S. lifts its blockade and Iran agrees to reopen the strait and engage in negotiations with Washington.

“Recent events clearly demonstrate the value of such a mission. I have urged the Iranian president to seize this opportunity, and I intend to discuss this matter with President Trump,” Macron said.

### [U.S. Forces Disabled Iran-Flagged Oil Tanker Trying to Violate Blockade, Centcom Says](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-s-forces-disabled-iran-flagged-oil-tanker-trying-to-violate-blockade-centcom-says-m4tTJOijdjdtGlIiHLeH)

U.S. forces disabled an Iranian-flagged oil tanker attempting to sail toward one of the country’s ports on Wednesday morning in violation of the U.S. blockade, according to U.S. Central Command.

After issuing several warnings to the empty tanker, which was moving through international waters toward a port on the Gulf of Oman, the U.S. fired several rounds at the vessel’s rudder, Centcom said. The rounds came from a naval fighter aircraft launched from the USS Abraham Lincoln. The tanker, the M/T Hasna, is no longer transiting to Iran, Centcom said.

“The U.S. blockade against ships attempting to enter or depart Iranian ports remains in full effect,” Centcom said. “CENTCOM forces continue to act deliberately and professionally to ensure compliance.”

### [Trump Says Iran Wants to Make a Deal](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/trump-says-iran-wants-to-make-a-deal-V21ymWt3KeKTZRiNJeSp)

Photo: Kent Nishimura/AFP/Getty Images

President Trump told military mothers gathered at the White House on Wednesday that the conflict in Iran was moving along “smoothly,” calling it a “skirmish.” He said he thought military mothers would want to know. Trump said Tehran negotiators "want to make a deal."

“We’ll see whether or not they can make a deal that’s satisfactory to us, we have it very much under control,” he said.

Trump compared it to the Venezuela operation in which the U.S. captured President Nicolás Maduro, noting that it was a quick operation. Trump has previously said conflicts the U.S. previously engaged in, including Vietnam, took a long time.

### [U.S. Airlines Spent 56% More on Fuel in March, Data Shows](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-s-airlines-spent-56-more-on-fuel-in-march-data-shows-yJCzoMfYlKyPyrdLhzK0)

U.S. airlines spent more than $5 billion on jet fuel in March, up 56% from the prior month, according to [data released Wednesday](https://www.bts.gov/newsroom/us-airlines-march-2026-aviation-fuel-cost-564-consumption-195-and-fuel-cost-gallon-309) by the Bureau of Transportation Statistics.

The hefty bill came as airlines used 19.5% more fuel in March than they did in February, the data showed. But the cost per gallon of fuel also rose substantially, jumping nearly 31%. Rising fuel costs have threatened to add [billions of dollars of unexpected expenses](https://www.wsj.com/business/airlines/iran-war-airline-impact-flight-costs-310c8cc7) for airlines this year, prompting carriers to pare back routes and raise fares to fight mounting costs.

### [Gas Prices Top $4.50 a Gallon, Highest Since July 2022](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/gas-prices-top-4-50-a-gallon-highest-since-july-2022-yEIoIt2Xt6H0J45vjgeM)

![Gas Prices Top $4.50 a Gallon, Highest Since July 2022](https://images.wsj.net/im-64939643?width=620&height=413)

David Ryder/Bloomberg News

Gas prices in the U.S. have topped more than $4.50 a gallon, the highest they have been since July 2022, according to data from AAA. The national average for regular gasoline is now nearly $4.54, AAA said Wednesday—a sharp rise from $4.30 a week ago. It is roughly 50 cents away from the July 2022 high of $5.01 a gallon.

Overall, the price for regular gasoline in the U.S. is up 50% since the Iran war began in late February. While the country is more insulated than many others from the oil and fuel supply squeeze in the Persian Gulf, the conflict has still led to [substantial pain at the pump](https://www.wsj.com/economy/consumers/americans-spent-125-million-more-on-gas-friday-than-they-did-a-week-ago-0038690c) for many Americans.

### [Iran Urges Rejection of U.S.-Backed U.N. Resolution on Hormuz](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/iran-urges-rejection-of-u-s-backed-u-n-resolution-on-hormuz-i2Qt4PT80oie7dxEgepe)

Iran’s mission to the United Nations in New York said Wednesday that “the only viable solution in the Strait of Hormuz is clear: a permanent end to the war, the lifting of the maritime blockade, and the restoration of normal passage.”

The statement, posted on X, accused Washington of using the pretext of “freedom of navigation” to legitimize unlawful actions and urged member states to reject a draft resolution proposed by the U.S.

The resolution calls on Iran to cease attacks and the mining of the strait, as well as to end charging tolls. It also demands that Tehran disclose the number and locations of sea mines it has laid and support the establishment of a humanitarian corridor.

On Tuesday, Secretary of State Marco Rubio described the draft as a test of the U.N.’s credibility and called on China and Russia not to veto the measure.
