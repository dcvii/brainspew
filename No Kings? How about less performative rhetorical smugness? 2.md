### Despairing of the way leftishists behave, even when they have a point

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mtD8!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0a6bf4bb-199b-45f4-87e5-5a4c2473b7f9_1024x1024.png)

For want of a better header image

Those No Kings protests. You see, Donald Trump is behaving like a quasi monarch with his executive orders and fondness for seemingly impulsive acts of war.

There’s obviously a *there* there. As a so-called lame duck president he’s using personal fiat to act in performatively vulgar ways involving things like wars and militia-style swoops on cities to remove immigrants. It’s clear why many people in America do not approve of this.

It’s not my business to litigate the rights and wrongs of what Trump does. In fact, I find reacting to Donald Trump exhausting. Plus I’ve no skin in the game, as a British national living in France, so my opinion on how America is run is of no consequence.

I’m reacting to an internal despair with the way the leftish conducts itself in general.

Even supporters of the No Kings parties (because that’s how they appear from where I’m sitting) [acknowledge the unserious theatrics and the unintended irony of celebrating that they are free to mercilessly lampoon their would-be ‘King’](https://www.theradicalist.com/p/no-kings-is-silly-but-i-love-it).

I suppose that when you’re losing you need something to smile about. So that’s what the people who organise and join in with these things are probably chasing. It certainly looks like fun. Much more fun than the dreary anti-Brexit marches I went on in Britain.

The rout of the Democrats in 2024, due to a senile president and a useless would-be successor, must have been awful for them. And it must drive them nuts that such a cartoonishly ridiculous president continues to lie and mislead almost every time he speaks and how they’ve got another two years of this to endure. I have sympathy in an abstract sense.

So I get the need to party hard. Eat, drink and be merry for tomorrow your own movers and shakers might well appoint yet another terrible candidate for the White House.

What unsettles me about what I see of No Kings is the in-group smugness of it all.

There’s a reasonable point to be making, on the street if necessary, but it’s diluted by subcultural signalling. ‘No Kings’ has intelligible meaning and is even quite clever in the way that it invokes a traditional republican principle that political power should not be personal or absolute. That is presumably what the organisers intend.

But I’ve got a thing about slogans and especially ones that come across as rhetorically self-satisfied. It feels like a branding exercise designed to delight people who already get the reference. But where does it leave non-activist types who don’t like Trump’s authoritarian-adjacent turn but aren’t fluent in activist idiom. I’m remembering the ludicrous sight of women thronging the streets in ‘pussy hats’ nearly a decade ago.

It’s all just very in-group and it seems to me that the crazy ways of Donald Trump could now inspire the formation of a much broader coalition than just people who are versed in irony.

When the ‘vibe shift’ happened it seemed to be inspired as much by an attitude of the leftish as much as enthusiasm for - or opposition to - concrete policies. We all know that attitude. The *right side of history* smugness and the sneering denial of reasonable principles held by people of a more conservative disposition.

The problem with ‘No Kings’ isn’t the debatability of the principle behind it. There’s obviously a small-d democratic value to be upholding by making your voice heard. But it’s for people who are already in the gang. Going heavy on costumes, ironic signs and self-consciously performative wit makes these protests look more like a vibrant social scene rather than a political movement.

I don’t doubt the sincerity of many who take part in these things but when a party who (rightly or wrongly) already suffers from the appearance of being especially hot for cross-dressing it looks from the outside like a festival of identity.

So a reasonable constitutional point is kept in the shadows with a gaudy display of in-group recognition. It looks like another scene in the culture war that I’m willing to bet now bores most people who have a vote in America.

Maybe this is inevitable when your core ranks now represent a particular class of educated, highly fluent people who are able to stage displays of aestheticised dissent.

This isn’t about it being wrong per se to use spectacle, costumes, humour and wit. But the vibe I’ve been getting is that it changes the register of these protests. And I’m willing to bet that a lot of ordinary Americans are glancing askance rather than feeling inspired to join in.
