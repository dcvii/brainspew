[Ford](https://carbuzz.com/cars/ford/) has been obsessed with the Nürburgring lately, sending its Mustang GTD around Germany's "Green Hell" racetrack to achieve a quicker time after [losing its American production car record](https://carbuzz.com/2025-corvette-zr1-nurburgring-lap-time/) to the Corvette ZR1. While it may not be the ZR1-smashing GTD rematch we've been waiting for, Ford just clocked a different Nürburgring record that was much quicker. In fact, with a time of 6:15.97 seconds, no American car has ever gone faster.

This incredible lap was set using a [Ford GT Mk IV](https://carbuzz.com/cars/ford/gt/), a limited run, [track-only version of the Ford GT](https://carbuzz.com/ford-gt-mk-iv-final-production/) supercar. To put that time into perspective, the Mercedes-AMG One, which uses an F1-derived engine and currently holds the Ring record for production cars, took 6:29.090 to lap the German track.

This is certainly an impressive time, but it's important to note the AMG One can be driven on public roads, while the record-setting GT Mk IV can not. If you count race cars and prototypes, the GT is the third-quickest vehicle to ever lap the Nürburgring.

"Driving the Ford GT Mk IV at the Nürburgring is an experience unlike any other. The car is an absolute weapon, a true extension of your will. Every input is met with an immediate, precise response. To set these records is a dream come true, a testament to what's possible when passion meets precision."

– Frédéric Vervisch, Ford Racing factory driver

## How The GT Is So Quick

![Ford GT Mk IV Nurburgring2](https://static0.carbuzzimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ford-gt-mk-iv-nurburgring2.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)

Ford Performance

You may question how Ford was able to dethrone so many world-class vehicles, but if you dive deeper into the GT Mk IV, it all makes sense. This car isn't road-legal, so it doesn't have to abide by safety and emissions regulations. It's also not a race car, so it's not limited by any specific engine and aero specifications.

That allowed Ford to go all-out with a 3.8-liter twin-turbo EcoBoost V6, developed by Roush-Yates Engines, producing over 820 horsepower. Like the Mustang GTD, the GT Mk IV also rides on adjustable Multimatic Adaptive Spool Valve dampers. The GT Mk IV was limited to only 67 examples, priced at around $1.7 million.

Ford says the weather conditions at the Nürburgring were colder than average, meaning the car could potentially have a quicker time in it. The car was also forced to run with a 310 kph (192.9 mph) top speed limit, when it will go up to 205 mph with no restrictions.

## How The GT's Time Stacks Up

![Ford GT Mk IV Nurburgring8](https://static0.carbuzzimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/ford-gt-mk-iv-nurburgring8.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)

Ford Performance

Since you can't put a license plate on the GT Mk IV, there are some asterisks and caveats attached to its impressive time. Ford says the GT Mk IV is now the quickest American car, exclusive internal combustion engine car (no hybrid), and purchasable car to ever lap the Nürburgring Nordschleife. As a track-only vehicle, the GT is put into the prototype category, meaning it is only the third-fastest lap time ever recorded.

- **Porsche 919 Hybrid EVO: 5:19.546**
- **Volkswagen ID.R: 6:05.336**
- **Ford GT Mk IV: 6:15.59**
- **Xiaomi SU7 Ultra Prototype: 6:22.091**
- **Lotus Evija X: 6:24.047**
- **Mercedes-AMG One: 6:29.090**

Two vehicles have recorded quicker times, though one uses hybrid assist and the other is fully electric. The Volkswagen ID.R set a 6:05.336 time, but unlike the GT Mk IV, it was never available to buy. The all-time record is held by the Porsche 919 Hybrid Evo, an LMP1-h LeMans prototype that set a blistering 5:19.546 time. If those are the only cars that have ever beaten the Ford GT, we can still count this 6:15.59 lap as a major achievement.
