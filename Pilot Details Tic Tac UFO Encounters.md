Mooch sits down with retired Commander Dave "Sex" Fravor — the Navy F/A-18 pilot at the center of the most credible UAP encounter in modern American military history — for a deep dive into the 2004 Tic Tac incident, the long road from carrier punchline to congressional testimony, and where the disclosure movement is headed.

---

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=764DtInvj4M)

---

Fravor was CO of VFA-41 aboard USS Nimitz when the USS Princeton vectored him out on a real-world intercept off San Diego. Controllers told him they’d been tracking these objects for two weeks — descending from space, hovering at 20,000 feet for hours, then going back up. What he found was a white, oblong object with no wings, no tail, no exhaust plume, hovering just above a churning patch of white water on an otherwise glassy sea. He descended to investigate. It immediately began mirroring his movements through a two-circle engagement, then disappeared instantaneously as he cut across the circle to close — and reappeared sixty miles away at his assigned cap point. Four sets of eyeballs from two aircraft watched it for about five minutes against a clear blue sky. An hour and a half later, Chad “Nuts” Underwood grabbed the now-famous FLIR footage from a follow-on sortie before it zoomed off frame — a uniform thermal signature, no hotspots, no ranging source because the object had already jammed the radar. The immediate aftermath on Nimitz was mostly jokes. No one in the chain of command ever came to ask Fravor about it. No investigation, no NDA — just air wing comics featuring his jet chasing a UFO and a ship-wide movie rotation of Signs, Men in Black, and Independence Day.

What followed was a fifteen-year slow burn: a 2009 AATIP investigation led by Jay Stratton, a Fighter Sweep blog post, Lou Elizondo and Tom DeLonge showing up in Boston, and Fravor finally saying yes to the 2017 New York Times story after refusing six times. The 60 Minutes piece reportedly became the most-watched episode in the show’s history. Joe Rogan hit nearly 10 million views. The 2023 open congressional hearing with Fravor, Dave Grusch, and Ryan Graves packed Capitol hallways. War.gov/ufo has now released two tranches of declassified records — including Apollo 17 footage of unidentified objects hovering above the lunar surface during the moonwalk, hidden for over fifty years. Fravor’s ask hasn’t changed: he’s not asking for classified programs to be burned. Just say out loud that we’ve known we’re not alone for eighty years, and that something crashed at Roswell. That’s it. With a new Spielberg film publicly credited to the 2017 NYT article and the 2023 hearings — Spielberg has named Fravor specifically — he thinks the cultural moment is about to broaden again in ways that can’t be walked back.
