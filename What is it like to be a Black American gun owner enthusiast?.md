I started in with guns seriously about four years ago. Seems like an eternity. It’s like the difference between graduating from high school and graduating from college. After you graduate from college, you can’t believe how dumb you were just four years ago. Since I’m a blogger, I’ve put together a lot of my experiences and thoughts in a particular section I call ‘Martial Ed 2 ’ short for martial education. The idea basically started when I listened to some NPR show about how many violent actions people see on TV and movies, vs how many they actually encounter in real life. Most Americans have seen hundreds of thousands of fictional gunshots and tens of thousands of fictional murders, but most have never seen more than one or two dead bodies in their lives. I asked myself one day, “What do I really know about fighting and what cops and soldiers do?” The answer was ‘I know what people say on the interwebz and what Hollywood says.” Unacceptable. Now I’m becoming more advanced in my martial education. Guns are becoming a smaller part of that education. There’s a lot I can say, but I’ll keep it brief. One day several years ago my son and I went to the Army Surplus store and R. Lee Ermey was there. He signed my ballcap and so I let my son wear the NRA hat that I was wearing. Everywhere we went that day people seemed to jump out of the woodwork to be friendly. It was completely unexpected. I mean at least 4 or 5 times people would wave and express pleasant greetings. It was totally unexpected. Pretty much like everything else in American life, when you’re genuinely interested in actually knowing and doing something, you’ll soon find yourself surrounded by good people who are into the same thing. The only surprise with shooters was that it happened so fast. BTW. 12% of all police officers in the US are black Americans. You don’t have to go far to find answers. Footnotes 1 Cobb
