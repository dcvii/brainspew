Hours before hundreds of Jewish students were due to celebrate Purim at the Chabad House of Stanford University, Rabbi Dov Greenberg received an email.

“The coming Holocaust 2.0?” said the subject line of the email that landed in the Chabad chapter’s general inbox on Monday.

To Greenberg, the message to Jews was clear.

“Don’t get together. We’re on to you. We know about you, and we’re coming for you,” the rabbi summarized.

The email, exclusively obtained by *The Free Press*, comes on the heels of a number of others directed toward Jewish students at Stanford since Sunday. The Federal Bureau of Investigation and other law enforcement are now investigating those messages, [according to](https://stanforddaily.com/2026/03/02/university-to-pursue-investigation-increase-security-following-antisemitic-emails/) *The Stanford Daily*. All the emails came from the same anonymous address: [ExposingStanfordJews@proton.me](https://www.thefp.com/p/). The contents included threats of “horrible physical attacks and abuse,” according to Greenberg.

“They were really horrible—as dark as the imagination can go,” said the rabbi.

The message sent to Chabad levied highly specific accusations against internal events organized by Stanford’s Jews, before accusing them of outsized wealth and a “persecution complex.” “Here’s the thing about comfortable people,” it continued. “They get sloppy. And the wind is shifting in a direction your great-grandparents would recognize before you do.”

The email ended with a veiled threat: “Just kidding, if you all died of the flu tomorrow, hypothetically speaking, it would be a major win for the world and for the cleansing of human genetics.”

In recent months, Chabad, an Orthodox organization with emissaries across the world, has become a target for antisemitic violence. In late January, a man was charged with several hate crimes after [repeatedly ramming his car](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/29/us/chabad-lubavitch-headquarters-car-ramming-hnk) into the Chabad Lubavitch Headquarters in Brooklyn. In December, a father-son pair of Islamic terrorists killed 15 attendees at a “ [Chanukah by the Sea](https://www.chabad.org/news/article_cdo/aid/7257328/jewish/At-Bondi-Beach-Chabads-Australian-Family-Shows-Up.htm) ” event hosted by Chabad in Sydney, Australia.

Meanwhile, like most elite campuses in the years after the Hamas attacks on Israel on October 7, 2023, Stanford has been a hotbed of antisemitism. Shortly after the terrorist attacks, the university suspended a professor who singled out Jewish students in a class and removed their belongings to simulate “what Jews were doing to Palestinians,” [according to a student quoted in](https://stanforddaily.com/2023/10/18/stanford-suspended-a-lecturer-for-identity-based-targeting-heres-what-students-say-happened/) *[The Stanford Daily](https://stanforddaily.com/2023/10/18/stanford-suspended-a-lecturer-for-identity-based-targeting-heres-what-students-say-happened/)*. Jewish students have reported mezuzahs being ripped from their door frames, while messages on an anonymous campus chat platform frequently targeted Jewish and Israeli students for “dissenting from the prevailing orthodoxy of virulent condemnation of Israel,” according to a [report on antisemitism and anti-Israel bias](https://news.stanford.edu/__data/assets/pdf_file/0033/156588/ASAIB-final-report.pdf) issued by the school in May 2024.

The email ended with a veiled threat: “Just kidding, if you all died of the flu tomorrow, hypothetically speaking, it would be a major win for the world and for the cleansing of human genetics.”

Much of the hostility has come from the left, said Dylan Rem, a 21-year-old student from Los Angeles. Not anymore. On Sunday, his non-Jewish peers at *The Stanford Review*, a student-run publication mostly staffed by conservatives and independents, received an email from the same address with a link to a 2020 blog post titled “ [Vampires vs Crosses: Why Jews and Leftists Hate White Men, Christianity and Western Civilization](https://www.theoccidentalobserver.net/2020/09/20/vampires-vs-crosses-why-jews-and-leftists-hate-white-men-christianity-and-western-civilization/).” He and the two other Jews who write for the paper were some of the only staffers not copied on the email, he said.

“It was a bit jarring,” Rem said. “But it’s pretty tame in comparison to what other people got.”

Both Rem and Greenberg noted that despite everything they have seen on Stanford’s campus over the past few years, they have never seen something like this.

“It’s unprecedented for me to receive that kind of hatred and death threats,” said the rabbi.

On Monday night, a few hours after the email was received by Chabad, about 200 Jewish students celebrated Purim at the Chabad House, located just beyond Stanford’s campus in Palo Alto, California. Greenberg told me that the event continued, but with three times as many security staff as usual. According to Olivia Raykhman, a master’s student at Stanford, some of her friends “almost didn’t come because they were scared to gather so publicly, given the events of the day.” In the end, they did so anyway. “The house was completely packed,” she said.

“The effect it has on me is absolutely no fear, but it reminds us to stand taller, with more conviction, with more joy, with more pride,” said Greenberg. “That is the single best response to hatred and antisemitism, and that’s what Jewish people have done across history, and ever more so than today.”
