## Open Street Events in LA County are at Risk: We Need Your Voice by 5pm Wednesday July 22

Metro is about to quietly end future funding for open street events. You can stop it, but only if you act by Wednesday, July 22, at 5pm.

### Why it matters:

Open street events are for everyone. Cutting funding doesn’t just put organizations like CicLAvia and open streets programs like ActiveSGV’s 626 Golden Streets/Active Streets at real risk. It erases opportunities for communities that don’t otherwise get this kind of social infrastructure investment.

### What happened:

Last week, Metro’s Planning and Programming Committee voted 4-1 to approve a report that guts open street events. Supervisor Horvath cast the lone dissenting vote.

What the report does:

- Leaves large-scale open street events unfunded through the start of the 2028 Olympics (leaving all of 2027 and the first half of 2028 unfunded)
- Delays any decision on a permanent program until after the 2028 Olympics
- Puts open street organizations like CicLAvia and open streets programs like ActiveSGV’s 626 Golden Streets/Active Streets at real risk

The report is now headed to the full Metro Board meeting for a **final vote** on Thursday, July 23, at 10am. It is on the [consent agenda as item #13](https://boardagendas.metro.net/event/regular-board-meeting-c6587f927a29/) on page 14. This means it gets approved automatically, with zero discussion—unless a Board member pulls it. **We need to make them pull it.**

### The bigger picture:

Metro spent over a decade building this movement; turning a bold experiment into a proven regional model that’s gotten hundreds of thousands of people onto transit, bikes, and their own two feet.

Walking away now doesn’t just cut a program. It walks back one of Metro’s best tools for building a transit-riding, multi-modal region, right as the world’s eyes turn to LA for the Olympics.

### TAKE ACTION NOW

Submit written comments via email by 5pm Wednesday, July 22, and / or give public testimony in-person or via phone at 10am Thursday, July 23.

Tell the Board:

- Fund open street events through 2027 and into 2028, don’t push the decision down the road.
- Find a creative solution now, don’t shut down the conversation for 2+ years until after the Olympics.
- Protect the 4+ mile, multi-neighborhood format that makes these events impactful.
- Make the open street events program permanent, not a grant line fighting for survival every cycle.

We’ve written an example for you to easily copy and paste.
