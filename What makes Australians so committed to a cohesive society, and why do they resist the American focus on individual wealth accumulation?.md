I’ve been to Sydney about 4 or 5 times and have lived there probably a total of 3 months. I would say that Australians live in the shadow of China in ways that most Americans don’t understand. In Sydney there is a fancy mall that’s like Beverly Hills and the proprietors and customers are rich Chinese to an extent you don’t see anywhere outside of China, pretty much. So in the same way that Japanese bought a lot of American real estate and properties in the 80s, China did so in Australia. It was dramatic.

Australians for the most part are much more isolated in their upscale associations than Americans are. Maybe you could think about it this way. If you are in the Deep South, the capital is Atlanta. And there are a lot of poorer people who get to live in Atlanta and feel they have made it. In the same way, the capital of the Midwest is Chicago. There are thousands of small towns where your dream is to get to Chicago. And that is completely outside of LA and NYC. But in Australia pretty much everything is Sydney and there is nowhere else comparable to go. You can’t just say, oh well, LA sucks I’m moving to Austin. Very few Aussies have that kind of money.

I say this in the best way, that Aussies are like Californians but without the attitude or the money. They love outdoors and animals and hanging with their buds and being casual. But it’s not full of power couples and celebrities. It’s just a low-key kind of cool place without harsh vibes. But they also know that relatively speaking, they are a small country. They depend a lot on imports from countries like the US that don’t pay them any mind. And their neighborhood is the South Pacific and Southeast Asia.

Nobody gets that rich is Australia. They can live comfortably there’s just not a lot of big fancy business. Their biggest employer is a mining company and then three of four banks. It’s not a place where you can run out and get an MBA and buy a Mercedes.

Australia is cool. It just doesn’t swagger.
