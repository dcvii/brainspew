## U.S. Blockade of Strait of Hormuz Takes Effect

## The U.S. military blockade was officially under way on Monday after peace talks ended without an agreement, and President Trump threatened drug-boat-style strikes on any Iranian ships coming near the blockade.

<iframe title="" frameborder="0" src="https://www.wsj.com/visuals/embed/inset-renderer/fader/4c241a69-7f89-430b-b171-8cb616fc64a8" allow="" allowfullscreen=""><p>Your browser does not support iframes</p></iframe>

A U.S. military blockade of the Strait of Hormuz was [under way](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/u-s-blockade-of-iran-officially-under-way-Zs0R6YhmAjmOGljdRf5W) on Monday after peace talks broke down, and President Trump warned that any Iranian fast-attack ships that came near the blockade would be destroyed.

More than 15 U.S. warships are in place to support the operation, according to a senior official. An advisory to mariners from U.K. Maritime Trade Operations, which is affiliated with Britain’s Royal Navy, said [maritime-access restriction](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/restrictions-placed-on-maritime-access-along-iranian-coastline-Ypsfn8JVoK0OzsC9tK1J) s were being enforced for Iranian ports and coastal areas along the Persian Gulf, Gulf of Oman and parts of the Arabian Sea.

Trump threatened [drug-boat-style strikes](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/trump-threatens-iranian-ships-with-drug-boat-style-strikes-amH2Xp6TKrmqVi1IOeEQ) on Iranian ships, saying in a social-media post that they would be targeted “using the same system of kill that we use against the drug dealers on boats at Sea.” Speaking to the press later, Trump said that [the “right people” in Iran](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/trump-says-iran-still-wants-to-make-a-deal-Ih7ccDTiXNSsPoG27WBb) still want to make a deal and that Iran wouldn’t have a nuclear weapon.

Iran earlier said no port in the Persian Gulf or the Sea of Oman would be safe [if its ports are threatened](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/iran-says-no-port-in-persian-gulf-or-sea-of-oman-will-be-safe-if-its-ports-are-threatened-fHuUhIm8miZiz6RAcjqr).

During talks in Pakistan over the weekend, the U.S. asked Iran to agree not to enrich uranium [for 20 years](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/in-a-shift-u-s-asked-iran-to-suspend-nuclear-enrichment-for-20-years-rlwtJa1iApyA67KQ7GO2), according to people familiar with the matter, softening previous demands concerning Tehran’s nuclear program.

Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif said that the two-week cease-fire was still in place and that efforts were under way to resolve [the outstanding issues](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/pakistan-says-full-effort-under-way-to-resolve-issues-between-u-s-and-iran-F4EnupCsd2dMgBWDDjsf) between the U.S. and Iran.

The U.K. and France said they [wouldn’t participate](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/u-k-won-t-help-u-s-enforce-strait-blockade-starmer-says-fMme3hnipksExvgxBD4e) in the blockade.

Trump lashed out at [Pope Leo](https://www.wsj.com/politics/elections/trump-criticizes-pope-leo-accuses-him-of-catering-to-radical-left-2cfb5509), while the pontiff vowed to continue to speak out against the war.

Oil prices jumped [back above $100](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/stock-market-today-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-04-13-2026).

### [Trump Says Iran Still Wants to Make a Deal](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/trump-says-iran-still-wants-to-make-a-deal-Ih7ccDTiXNSsPoG27WBb)

![Trump spoke to the press in Washington on Monday.](https://images.wsj.net/im-67126698?width=620&height=413)

Trump spoke to the press in Washington on Monday. Brendan Smialowski/AFP/Getty Images

President Trump said the “right people” in Iran still want a U.S.-Iran deal after talks in Pakistan over the weekend ended without an agreement. “We’ve been called by the other side and they would like to make a deal very badly,” Trump said. “We’ve been called this morning by the right people, the appropriate people, and they want to work a deal.”

Trump went on to say that Iran won’t have a nuclear weapon—a key focus of talks. “We agreed to a lot of things, but we did not agree to that, but I think they will agree to it,” Trump said.

### [Lebanese Leader Says He Hopes Talks Will Lead to Cease-Fire](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/lebanese-leader-says-he-hopes-talks-will-lead-to-cease-fire-MOnJq3mghmZAH74Jit4o)

![Lebanese first responders searched for survivors on Monday after an airstrike targeted the village of Maaroub.](https://images.wsj.net/im-17240947?width=620&height=413)

Lebanese first responders searched for survivors on Monday after an airstrike targeted the village of Maaroub. Kawnat Haju/AFP/Getty Images

Lebanese President Joseph Aoun said he hopes the upcoming meeting between the Arab state, U.S. and Israel in Washington will lead to further direct negotiations and a cease-fire deal.

“There is an opportunity now to reach a sustainable solution, which is what Lebanon desires. However, this can’t be achieved unilaterally. Israel must respond to Lebanese, Arab and international calls to cease its aggression against Lebanon and begin negotiations,” he said in a statement Monday.

The negotiations between Israel and Lebanon, which are currently planned to take place Tuesday in Washington, will be preparatory talks at the level of ambassadors rather than at a more senior level, The Wall Street Journal reported last week.

Lebanese officials have said they want a temporary cease-fire before talks begin, according to mediators. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu said Thursday that there was no cease-fire in Lebanon and that Israel was continuing to hit Hezbollah forcefully. But a person familiar with Israel’s thinking said Israel would limit its attacks in accordance with President Trump’s request.

Lebanese officials have told the mediators that they wanted their cease-fire talks to be separate from U.S.-Iran talks as Tehran could use the Israel-Lebanon fight as a cudgel to seek greater concessions from Washington.

“Negotiations with Israel are the responsibility of the Lebanese state, not any other party, as this is a sovereign matter in which Lebanon has no partners,” Aoun said.

### [In a Shift, U.S. Asked Iran to Suspend Nuclear Enrichment for 20 Years](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/in-a-shift-u-s-asked-iran-to-suspend-nuclear-enrichment-for-20-years-rlwtJa1iApyA67KQ7GO2)

![Vice President JD Vance speaking in Islamabad, Pakistan, on Sunday.](https://images.wsj.net/im-73663744?width=620&height=413)

Vice President JD Vance speaking in Islamabad, Pakistan, on Sunday. Jacquelyn Martin/Press Pool

During talks in Pakistan, the U.S. asked Iran to agree not to enrich uranium for 20 years, according to people familiar with the matter, softening previous demands concerning Tehran’s nuclear program. The U.S. worries nuclear enrichment gives Tehran a path toward a nuclear weapon.

U.S. Vice President JD Vance said Sunday he had shown “flexibility” with Iran, who rejected his offer of a nuclear deal to end the war. The U.S. has previously demanded that Iran permanently give up the right to enrich uranium domestically and rely on foreign imports of the material. The 20-year moratorium would have come with sanctions relief for Iran, said one of the people.

A critical question raised by the offer: Would Iran be free to enrich as much as it wanted after the 20-year window or would some constraints remain? Iran instead proposed a pause for only a few years, say Iranian officials, who added that Iran also rejected a U.S. demand that it transfer its stockpiles of highly enriched uranium abroad.

### [Iran War’s Economic Shock Wave Is Expected to Get Even Bigger](https://www.wsj.com/world/europe/iran-wars-economic-shock-wave-is-expected-to-get-even-bigger-3aadf870?mod=livecoverage_web)

![Iran War’s Economic Shock Wave Is Expected to Get Even Bigger](https://images.wsj.net/im-28974873?width=620&height=413)

Giuseppe Cacace/AFP/Getty Images

President Trump’s naval blockade of Iran risks further upending a global economy already battered by weeks of war, escalating a regional clash into a worldwide financial shock that could prove more devastating than the fighting itself.

The U.S. blockade on ships entering or exiting Iranian ports is set to drain more oil from a tight market, prolong the squeeze on other key commodities flowing through the Strait of Hormuz and inject significant uncertainty into the global economy.

Oil prices jumped on Monday. Aluminum prices surged to a four-year high amid fears of prolonged supply squeeze from a region producing nearly a 10th of the world’s supply of the key industrial metal.

### [China and Iran Can Wait Out a Blockade](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/china-and-iran-can-wait-out-a-blockade-CkgynAUc99nauwzYw5z5)

Iran has built a stockpile of oil outside the Strait of Hormuz that could help it—and Chinese crude importers—withstand a blockade for weeks or even months.

Iran has been exporting more oil than usual during the war. It shipped 1.84 million barrels a day last month, data from Vortexa shows. February was even busier, at 2.15 million barrels a day. This puts Iran’s average exports over the last two months 26% above 2025 levels.

The amount of Iranian oil at sea was already high heading into the crisis. Tehran began ramping up its exports ahead of military action by the U.S. and Israel last June.

Iran really has only one customer for its crude: independent Chinese “teapot” refiners who soak up over 90% of its exports. As Beijing sets annual quotas on how much the teapot refineries can buy, they couldn’t take all of the excess oil immediately.

This leaves Iran with around 160 million barrels of oil already loaded onto ships floating on the ocean outside the Persian Gulf today. Some of the crude is already spoken for and on its way to Chinese buyers. But in theory, Tehran can keep its customers supplied until mid-July, based on the 1.8 million barrels a day of Iranian oil that China imports.

President Trump hopes that choking off oil revenues will force Iran to the negotiating table. Tehran is betting it can withstand the disruption longer than the global economy. Based on its oil stockpile at sea, that is more than mere bluster.

### [250 Hezbollah Militants Were Killed in Single Wave of Israeli Strikes, Military Says](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/250-hezbollah-militants-killed-in-single-wave-of-israeli-strikes-military-says-cgoHakDlIJFWZKdwsVQE)

![People standing amid the graves of Hezbollah fighters killed in Israeli strikes at a cemetery in Choueifat, Lebanon, on Monday.](https://images.wsj.net/im-96036983?width=620&height=413)

People standing amid the graves of Hezbollah fighters killed in Israeli strikes at a cemetery in Choueifat, Lebanon, on Monday. Anwar Amro/AFP/Getty Images

Israel’s military said it killed more than 250 Hezbollah militants and commanders last Wednesday in its largest single wave of attacks in Lebanon since the start of operations, hitting targets throughout Beirut, the Beqaa valley region, and southern Lebanon almost simultaneously.

The commanders killed include: Hassan Mustafa Nasser, commander of Hezbollah’s Logistics Support Headquarters, who helped rebuild Hezbollah’s capabilities in southern Lebanon and was responsible for smuggling and storing weapons; Ali Qassem, Abu Ali Abbas, and Ali Hijazi, senior commanders in Hezbollah’s intelligence unit; and Abu Muhammad Habib, deputy commander of Hezbollah’s Missile Unit, the military said.

As of April 9, Lebanon’s Ministry of Health reported 303 dead in those attacks and 1,150 injured, with figures expected to rise as missing persons are identified. Lebanon’s Ministry of Health reported Sunday that 2,089 people have been killed and 6,588 injured since March 2.

### [U.S. Blockade of Iran Officially Under Way](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/u-s-blockade-of-iran-officially-under-way-Zs0R6YhmAjmOGljdRf5W)

![Warships on the move in the Persian Gulf near the Strait of Hormuz on Sunday.](https://images.wsj.net/im-72023272?width=620&height=413)

Warships on the move in the Persian Gulf near the Strait of Hormuz on Sunday. WSJ

The U.S. blockade has officially gone into effect, and there are more than 15 U.S. warships in place to support the operation, according to a senior U.S. official.

The U.S. has an aircraft carrier, multiple guided-missile destroyers, an amphibious assault ship and several other warships in the Middle East, according to Navy and Central Command officials. These ships have the ability to launch helicopters that support boarding operations, and some are capable of marshalling commercial vessels to specific areas to hold them in place.

The warships would likely operate outside the Strait of Hormuz to avoid threats fired by Iran, according to retired Navy Vice Adm. Kevin Donegan. “There are lots of ways you can construct this, and there are a lot of boarding forces in the region now,” Donegan said. “Don’t expect it all to be started at once, this will build. Blockades take time to have an impact.”

### [Bahrain Summons Iraqi Ambassador Over Continued Drone Attacks](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-us-cease-fire-talks-stalled-2026/card/bahrain-summons-iraqi-ambassador-over-continued-drone-attacks-Z0yiUpig7nY8vJ07lAmg)

Bahrain’s Foreign Ministry summoned Iraq’s ambassador in protest of what it said were “continued hostile drone attacks launched from Iraqi territory” against the kingdom and other Gulf Arab countries. A Bahrain government spokesperson said the attacks came after the cease-fire the Trump administration announced last week.

Bahrain’s demarche of the Iraqi ambassador follows similar moves by Saudi Arabia and the U.S., which accused an Iraqi militia of attacking a diplomatic convoy last week that was carrying a released American hostage.
