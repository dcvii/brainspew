## Attacks Strike U.A.E., Ships as Fighting Escalates in Mideast

## Drone hit sparks fire at Emirati oil hub of Fujairah hours after Trump announced initiative to get ships through the strait

![Ships and boats in the Strait of Hormuz.](https://images.wsj.net/im-02463048?width=620&height=413)

Ships and boats in the Strait of Hormuz. Reuters

Fighting in the Middle East flared, as Iran struck a crucial United Arab Emirates oil port and several ships, testing a shaky cease-fire. The U.A.E., which has backed tougher [action against Tehran](https://www.wsj.com/world/middle-east/u-a-e-opec-new-middle-east-32ceda56), said it had intercepted [missiles and drones](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-a-e-says-it-intercepted-15-missiles-four-drones-from-iran-f9Xj3494zm5z9y3v8Mwx) launched from Iran. It also confirmed a drone hit sparked a fire at the crucial [oil hub of Fujairah](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/why-iran-s-attack-on-fujairah-matters-for-energy-markets-dWt5E0zPyCzNDCCx9quJ), which the country has used to pump much of its crude output around Iran’s chokehold on the Strait of Hormuz. “We reserve the right to a full and legitimate response to attacks,” [the U.A.E. foreign ministry said](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-a-e-calls-iranian-attacks-dangerous-escalation-says-has-full-right-to-respond-QWDY6m2UaYMrALEeOFJi).

Iran fired cruise missiles and other projectiles [at U.S. warships and commercial vessels](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/iran-fired-cruise-missiles-at-u-s-warships-commercial-vessels-Q88T6TMuvYZrG1J7dwkM) as the U.S. launched an effort to help vessels through the strait, according to Adm. Brad Cooper, head of U.S. Central Command. Additional ships are “en route” to transit the strait after [U.S. military forces successfully guided](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-s-military-forces-clears-safe-path-through-strait-despite-mines-centcom-chief-says-7zHh6i3LfjvGoWqJe2ty) two U.S.-flagged commercial vessels through the vital waterway, he added. President Trump [warned Iran](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/trump-threatens-iran-if-it-targets-u-s-ships-6kDQ2MbRMG73wcf1AJYl) against attacking U.S. ships near the strait.

Trump [called on South Korea](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/trump-calls-on-south-korea-to-join-fight-in-strait-of-hormuz-NIv5IUQK2cPOYYmmMctI) to join the fight in the strait, after Iran fired on a South Korean cargo ship.

[Oil prices rose](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/brent-crude-hits-2026-closing-high-yzoqwMDqyp6VhdebIwpp) amid heightened tensions, with Brent crude climbing 5.8% to its highest close for the year.

A unit of Abu Dhabi’s state-owned oil and gas giant said one of its crude oil tankers was [struck by Iranian drones](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/adnoc-tanker-struck-by-drones-off-oman-crew-unharmed-e0Abf48ZjJSUvOzNWHyQ).

The Israeli military is [on high alert](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/israel-s-military-on-high-alert-makes-no-changes-to-civilian-guidelines-Hr01JgcEY4WC7VPFhGos) following the Iranian attack on the U.A.E., an Israeli military official said.

### [Why Did the U.A.E. Leave OPEC?](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/why-did-the-u-a-e-leave-opec--CQbYrXfyHaYa512QKW6F)

Iran's new attacks on the United Arab Emirates came about a week after the wealthy federation of Persian Gulf monarchies shocked the oil markets by saying it would withdraw from the OPEC cartel. Here are some of the reasons behind that dramatic step:

**The move gives the U.A.E. more freedom to decide how much oil to pump**. The country had sought in recent years to produce more oil than the cartel's production quotas allowed, and was one of the few members that had the spare capacity to do so. The U.A.E. could seek to derive greater global influence from the resulting ability to influence oil prices, particularly in a moment of crisis.

The U.A.E. could use any added revenue–at a time when oil prices are elevated–to offset war-related pressure on its earnings from tourism and business. It could also then direct capital toward securing its export routes by investing more in pipelines designed to bypass the bottleneck in the Strait of Hormuz.

**Leaving OPEC also makes a statement about the U.A.E.'s aspirations for regional power and its view of its security interests**. The move brings it out of the shadow of its larger neighbor, Saudi Arabia, the cartel's main powerhouse, and casts a spotlight on the U.A.E.'s growing security cooperation with Israel.

The U.A.E. is also a significant military power, and the Iran war has highlighted the threats that it faces. Iran has fired more missiles and drones at the U.A.E. than any other target, including Israel. [Monday's strikes](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-a-e-says-it-intercepted-15-missiles-four-drones-from-iran-f9Xj3494zm5z9y3v8Mwx) included a [drone attack on the U.A.E. port](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/why-iran-s-attack-on-fujairah-matters-for-energy-markets-dWt5E0zPyCzNDCCx9quJ) of Fujairah, which is critical in part because it sits on the Gulf of Oman, outside the Strait of Hormuz, which means Abu Dhabi can move some crude pumped from its fields directly to the coast without sending tankers through the strait.

**The U.A.E. also has been growing closer to the U.S. in recent years.** It was the first Trump administration that orchestrated the Abraham Accords in which the U.A.E. and Bahrain normalized relations with Israel in 2020.

In the wake of the U.A.E.'s decision to withdraw from OPEC, President Trump said, “I think ultimately it’s a good thing for getting the price of gas down, getting oil down, getting everything down.”

### [The U.S. Has Entered a Tanker War Before. Here’s How It Went.](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/the-u-s-has-entered-a-tanker-war-before-here-s-how-it-went--byQJJSVeJMOUaZE8aUlO)

![In 1987, the U.S. launched a ship-escort operation in the Persian Gulf.](https://images.wsj.net/im-50691339?width=620&height=413)

In 1987, the U.S. launched a ship-escort operation in the Persian Gulf. Mark Duncan/AP

Investors hoping the U.S. military can end Iran’s effective blockade of the Strait of Hormuz should know: It didn’t take long after America’s entry into the last tanker war for something to go awry.

The U.S. in 1987 began a ship-escort operation in the Persian Gulf that echoes what President Trump has launched [to help unlock energy supplies](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/what-is-project-freedom--9QZKx9XbAr8rvrzpIup5) from the embattled region today.

In a show of force aimed to discourage attacks by Iran, several U.S. warships joined commercial vessels through the Strait of Hormuz in their first escort mission. As the convoy motored toward Kuwait, one of its skyscraper-sized tankers struck a mine that blasted a hole in its hull.

“That was a turning point,” said retired Rear Adm. Harold Bernsen, who commanded the U.S. Navy’s Middle East Force at the time. “All of a sudden, we recognized that Iran was willing to attack us even though we had the hammer.”

Washington is now launching a similarly high-stakes operation to reopen the strategic waterway, through which hundreds of tankers and cargo ships need to sail to leave the Persian Gulf. The bottleneck has created the largest supply disruption in the history of the oil market and the resulting shock has nearly doubled [global oil prices](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/brent-crude-hits-2026-closing-high-yzoqwMDqyp6VhdebIwpp) since the start of the year.

Several vessels and [a crucial United Arab Emirates oil port](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/why-iran-s-attack-on-fujairah-matters-for-energy-markets-dWt5E0zPyCzNDCCx9quJ) have been struck in recent hours. The skirmish marks the latest flare-up in hostilities that has throttled tanker traffic from dozens a day before the war to just a handful in recent weeks.

The Trump administration's new plan—which includes nearby ships and aircraft but not tanker escorts—comes after weeks of limited interest from other countries to help reopen the strait. Retired officers and analysts who studied efforts to police the region in the 1980s warn that the conflict today is more expansive, endangering crews and widening the margin for error in the energy market.

Even as Iraq and Iran traded deadly blows on commercial ships as part of a broader war in the 1980s, “the Iranians never actually attempted to close the strait,” said Anthony Gurnee, a former Navy intelligence analyst who covered threats to commercial shipping at the time. Gurnee, also the former chief executive of tanker operator Ardmore, called the current conflict “much more kinetic.”

About three-dozen commercial vessels in the region have experienced incidents since March 1 ranging from missile strikes to small-arms fire to Iranian threats, according to the Joint Maritime Information Center. Some tanker operators may deem making the trip through the narrow strait too risky even while the U.S. military steps in.

America’s 1980s intervention in the Persian Gulf showcased its ability to protect some commercial ships, historians and former officials say. Bookended by tragedy, including an incident in which Iraqi friendly fire killed 37 U.S. crew members, the intervention also came with grave consequences.

Operation Earnest Will began in mid-1987, when forces under Bernsen’s command began escorting 11 Kuwaiti-owned tankers reflagged with the U.S. banner. Despite several weeks of preparation, he said, the operation didn’t have minesweeping assets in place before its initial journey. The effort eventually drew minesweeping and intelligence support from countries, including the U.K. and France.

After a Navy frigate struck a mine in 1988, the U.S. destroyed Iranian oil platforms and much of the country’s conventional navy in response. A Harpoon missile from an Iranian warship buzzed about 100 feet past a U.S. vessel at one point, leading an American frigate to return fire with four of its own missiles, helping destroy what was then the largest warship sunk by the U.S. Navy since World War II.

The escort operation ended around the time of the conclusion of the Iran-Iraq conflict, after a U.S. warship mistakenly shot down an Iranian passenger plane, killing 290 people.

### [Brent Crude Hits 2026 Closing High](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/brent-crude-hits-2026-closing-high-yzoqwMDqyp6VhdebIwpp)

Oil futures ended Monday higher, rising to some of their highest prices of the war after Iran struck a United Arab Emirates oil post and several ships. Brent crude, a global price gauge, rose 5.8% to end at $114.44 a barrel, the highest close for the most-traded futures contract since June 2022.

The flare in fighting threatens a precarious cease-fire and comes as global oil supplies are showing strains from two months without normal flows from the Persian Gulf. Benchmark U.S. crude futures added $4.48 a barrel to settle at $106.42.

### [U.A.E. Says It Intercepted 15 Missiles, Four Drones From Iran](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-a-e-says-it-intercepted-15-missiles-four-drones-from-iran-f9Xj3494zm5z9y3v8Mwx)

The United Arab Emirates’ Defense Ministry said late Monday it intercepted 12 ballistic missiles, three cruise missiles and four drones launched from Iran, the first [attacks from Tehran](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-a-e-intercepts-iranian-missiles-in-first-wave-of-attacks-in-weeks-WDDSEOs87685rIG6NDpC) since the U.S.-Iran cease-fire went into effect last month. Since the start of the war, U.A.E. air defenses have intercepted a total of 549 ballistic missiles, 29 cruise missiles, and 2,260 drones, resulting in 227 injuries, the ministry said.

### [How Tensions Have Reignited Between Iran and the U.S.](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/how-tensions-have-reignited-between-iran-and-the-u-s--UZ6CT2IpzdXiR0RciY0q)

![A state-organized rally in Tehran late last month.](https://images.wsj.net/im-38988748?width=620&height=413)

A state-organized rally in Tehran late last month. Vahid Salemi/AP

After weeks of a fragile cease-fire with Iran, President Trump on Sunday announced a new effort to guide trapped commercial ships out of the Strait of Hormuz. But tensions rose sharply in the hours that followed, threatening to plunge the region into a new round of fighting. Here’s how it has unfolded so far.

Trump announced [“Project Freedom”](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/what-is-project-freedom--9QZKx9XbAr8rvrzpIup5) on Sunday night, describing the effort as a humanitarian gesture to free up trapped commercial ships. Any interference would be “dealt with forcefully,” he said.

Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps responded sharply, saying ships transiting the Strait without its permission faced serious risks.

U.S. military forces including warships and aircraft actively protected several commercial vessels from Iranian attacks Monday, a senior U.S. official said. Adm. Brad Cooper, head of U.S. Central Command, said [Iran fired cruise missiles](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/iran-fired-cruise-missiles-at-u-s-warships-commercial-vessels-Q88T6TMuvYZrG1J7dwkM) and other projectiles at U.S. warships and commercial vessels. Cooper said the U.S. defeated the threats and destroyed six [Iranian small attack boats](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-s-military-destroys-six-iranian-small-attack-boats-QdvbqEV4XSC8dBUMcJwv).

The American military said it successfully guided two [U.S.-flagged commercial ships](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/how-the-u-s-cleared-a-path-through-the-strait-of-hormuz-uIT1k1q31Oi6b6zIpEzY) through the strait.

Several vessels were reported to have been hit, including [a crude oil tanker](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/adnoc-tanker-struck-by-drones-off-oman-crew-unharmed-e0Abf48ZjJSUvOzNWHyQ) used by Abu Dhabi and the Marshall Islands-flagged JV Innovation. The HMM Namu, operated by [a South Korean shipping company](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/trump-calls-on-south-korea-to-join-fight-in-strait-of-hormuz-NIv5IUQK2cPOYYmmMctI), was also struck by an explosive Monday, according to maritime-security company Vanguard.

On Monday, authorities ‌in the United Arab Emirates said they dealt with four cruise missiles launched by Iran, the first airstrikes targeting the Gulf states in weeks. Separately, authorities said a fire erupted at the Fujairah oil industry zone after a drone attack from Iran. "We reserve the right to a full and legitimate response to attacks," [the U.A.E. foreign ministry](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/iran-hormuz/card/u-a-e-calls-iranian-attacks-dangerous-escalation-says-has-full-right-to-respond-QWDY6m2UaYMrALEeOFJi) said.
