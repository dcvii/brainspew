### GMP is a system built on traceability, documentation and control, and without that system, the claim can't exist.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qIPz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff4144b88-1ec6-402f-9e2f-63f5637de8f5_1320x1963.jpeg)

I’ve noticed that a number of grey market vendors and manufacturers claiming to be “GMP compliant”.

So I decided to investigate those claims.

In this article, I’ll explain what GMP compliance actually means, what is required to achieve it, and why it’s simply not feasible for any grey market manufacturer or vendor to be genuinely GMP compliant.

But to understand why, we first need to understand what GMP actually is.

**GMP vs cGMP**

**GMP defines the framework.**

**cGMP means that framework is being followed using current, up to date methods, systems, and controls.**

If GMP itself is not present, claims of cGMP are even less credible, because they imply not just a system, but a modern, validated, and continuously maintained one.

**Where GMP comes from**

GMP didn’t originate in pharmaceuticals. It evolved from **industrial quality systems**, particularly:

military manufacturing

aerospace

high reliability engineering

These were industries where failure was not an option. They relied on:

**traceability**

**documentation**

**controlled processes**

**qualified suppliers**

**repeatability**

Pharmaceutical manufacturing adopted these same principles because it carries the same requirement:

**Zero tolerance for failure**

**What GMP actually is**

GMP is an industrial level quality system designed for **traceable, controlled high-risk production**

GMP does not mean a manufacturer produces the “best” possible product. It means the product is produced consistently, within strictly defined limits, in a controlled, documented, and auditable environment. That distinction matters, because a product may appear good at the end and still not be GMP. GMP is not a claim about outcome alone, it is a claim about the **system behind the outcome**

**Why traceability is everything**

At the centre of GMP is one requirement

**Traceability**

Every raw material, component, supplier, process step, must be qualified, documented and auditable

If you don’t know where something came from, GMP fails immediately. GMP cannot be partially applied and if one part of the system breaks, the entire system fails.

**What GMP actually requires**

For a product to be manufactured under GMP, all of the following must exist:

**1.Full supply chain traceability**

known origin of all materials

qualified and audited suppliers

traceability of every component

**2.Complete documentation system**

full batch records

every step recorded

every parameter logged

every deviation investigated

**If it isn’t written down, it didn’t happen**

**3.Qualified and audited facilities**

approved manufacturing sites

regulatory inspection

controlled cleanroom environments

**A cleanroom without oversight is not GMP, it’s just a clean room**

**4.Validated processes**

proven, repeatable methods

no improvisation

**GMP doesn’t allow “it worked once” it requires “it always works.”**

**5.Trained personnel**

documented training

competency verification

**6.Equipment qualification**

installation, operational, and performance qualification

calibration and maintenance logs

**7.Sterility assurance (for injectables)**

WFI systems

depyrogenation

sterile filtration

environmental monitoring

**Sterility is not assumed, it is continuously proven**

**8.In process controls**

continuous monitoring during production

verification of critical parameters

**9.Inspection and testing**

automated visual inspection

sterility, endotoxin, and potency testing

**10.QA release system**

independent quality review

full data verification

**If QA doesn’t sign off, the product does not exist**

**11.Post production traceability**

Serialisation

supply chain tracking

**GMP is not one of these things, it’s all of them, working together**

**API GMP vs Finished Product GMP**

Pharmaceutical GMP is divided into two levels:

**API GMP**

synthesis and purification

chemical quality and consistency

**This is the highest level grey market products could theoretically approach**

**Finished Product GMP**

sterile filling and injectables

aseptic processing

environmental control

full batch documentation

**This is the standard required for hospitals, pharmacies and patient use**

The critical misunderstanding when vendors claim, “GMP product”, buyers hear, “Safe injectable pharmaceutical”

But the reality is at best, what might exist is API-level practices (maybe) but not finished sterile GMP

**Where GMP fails in the grey market**

Once the GMP requirements are understood, it doesn’t take a genius to realise that in the grey market

**traceability** is missing

**documentation** is missing

**supplier qualification** is missing

**GMP is not a spectrum, it’s all or nothing**

Even if a grey market manufacturer attempts to operate “as if GMP”, the system fails the moment any part of the supply chain lacks proper qualification or documentation.

**Why this system does not exist in the grey market**

Real GMP offers advantages in legal market circulation and export, but it also subjects a facility to inspection, accountability, and continued regulatory scrutiny. That is precisely why claims of genuine pharmaceutical GMP in the grey market are so difficult to take seriously: the very system that makes GMP meaningful is the same system grey market operators would have strong incentives to avoid.

**GMP requires scrutiny. The grey market works by staying outside it**
