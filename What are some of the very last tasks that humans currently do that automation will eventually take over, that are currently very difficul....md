What’s interesting is how little people prepare their own food. If you’ve ever read Michael Pollan you have a feel for what I’m saying. Back in the immediate post-war period, author George Orwell was amazed by how many people ate food out of ‘tins’. Canned vegetables was a new thing.

Today machines make lots of ‘food’ that people eat, and people don’t miss the skill of making their own salad dressing, or selecting their own cuts of meat from a butcher. Pollan says that if your grandmother wouldn’t recognize it in the supermarket, it’s not real food, it is an engineered food product. Health nuts would probably be shocked to see the factories that manufacture granola bars. And if that bread is not using wheat, in order to be gluten-free, what exactly is going into it?

My point here is that people often suffer because of the convenience that machines provide. They lose manual skills and they don’t know about, or how to operate or fix the machines. And yet they don’t complain and they socially disparage the job that once people took pride in.

I’ve said it before. When it comes to a number of things, I am a Neo-Victorian. I believe people are losing the ability to manage simple labor and take skilled labor for granted. (and yet they go overboard for craft beer). I would prefer a world where the affluent could give gainful employment to servants who work with dignity, rather than build AIs and pay Basic to the ‘unemployable’.
