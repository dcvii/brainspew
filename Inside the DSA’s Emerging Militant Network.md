As its national influence has risen, the Democratic Socialists of America (DSA) has simultaneously [grown](https://www.city-journal.org/article/democratic-socialists-of-america-public-office) more extreme. Nowhere is this more apparent than in the group’s “Red Rabbits” initiative. The Red Rabbits Security Commission, a subgroup within the DSA focused on “community defense” efforts, is, according to its authorizing resolution, preparing for a “national uprising against federal agents and police brutality.” In practice, that means training cadres in tactics like armed and unarmed self-defense, blocking intersections, and fighting “fascists” with umbrellas.

A recent [panel](https://actionnetwork.org/events/red-rabbits-security-commission-panel) offered an unprecedented window into what the project looks like. Organizers from Minnesota, Oklahoma City, Philadelphia, Tucson, Austin, and Portland compared notes. As the discussion made clear, the DSA is trying to construct a nationwide security apparatus to support its expanding role in street protests and direct-action organizing. And in so doing, it fears drawing the attention of the Internal Revenue Service—likely with good reason.

The DSA launched the Red Rabbits Security Commission at its 2025 national convention. Organizers chose the deliberately innocuous name as a [nod to](https://www.dsausa.org/blog/walk-rabbit-walk-protecting-the-left-nonviolently) the novel *Watership Down*, in which anthropomorphized rabbits are outnumbered and beset by enemies. Earlier branding proposals, including “National Vigilance Committee,” were deemed too politically stark, with some members concerned that they could be interpreted as an endorsement of vigilantism.

The Red Rabbits [claim](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=f8oDv7XHnI0HW9Cj&t=7480) that their focus is on five core security skills: de-escalation, Stop the Bleed (a first aid training on bleeding control), firearm safety, unarmed self-defense, and protest marshalling (crowd management during demonstrations). The commission has set a goal of having at least five members in [40 percent](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=kQImsBNEkEivfYFn&t=7506) of DSA chapters complete these trainings.

Since its inauguration, the committee has been a source of internal controversy. That includes an unsuccessful effort by members of the DSA’s governing National Political Committee to [remove](https://www.city-journal.org/article/democratic-socialists-of-america-national-political-committee-maoist) a Maoist organizer whose past public comments included praise for [revolutionary violence](https://x.com/thestustustudio/status/2026722732428111878). The committee ultimately voted to retain him.

Still, the Red Rabbits have mostly kept a low profile. The [recent panel](https://actionnetwork.org/events/red-rabbits-security-commission-panel), an introduction to the Red Rabbits’ nationally approved training, marked the commission’s first major public-facing appearance. The panel was organized by Hazel Williams, a National Political Committee member and [former co-chair of California DSA](https://democraticleft.dsausa.org/2025/03/07/reflections-on-california-dsa), and was meant to [provide](https://redstarcaucus.org/red-star-newsletter-may-2026) “lessons learned, best practices, and practical guidance for building security committees in local chapters.”

During the event, local chapters [described](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IyhQk8tDbok&t=5886s) a range of security preparations broader than that envisioned by the national commission. These included martial-arts sparring, evacuation planning, wound-packing, radio communications, the use of umbrellas and signs to shield participants from and block “fascists,” and even chemical-exposure training, in which participants practiced being pepper-sprayed.

Some chapters already conduct in-house trainings or are working to expand them, such as the Philadelphia chapter’s [plan](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=QynJo1BPnsjnfyA3&t=3650) to develop what one member called “sick” firearm-safety trainings. The DSA’s Oklahoma City Queer Fight Club has evidently become a [local training hub](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=DzLeTc64v1ZEJu4M&t=1366), teaching self-defense and broader “community defense” skills. Portland DSA has organized trainings on blocking intersections with bicycles, a practice [known as](https://www.instagram.com/p/C7UHMHwxpRg) “corking.”

Panelists also explained that, as the Red Rabbits initiative has grown, other activist groups have come to depend on it for “self-defense” purposes. In New Jersey, DSA’s [immigrant justice working group](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=vriPyHYC1JpxCgGc&t=2590) has become a go-to security resource for immigrant organizations and Palestine affinity groups. In Philadelphia, the Red Rabbits [started](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IyhQk8tDbok&t=3215s) with abortion clinic “defense” efforts, then expanded to helping groups focused on everything from “immigrant justice” to “Palestinian solidarity.” Philly DSA’s Red Rabbits team apparently [functions as](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=aYeGUrFfaJEqQs0G&t=3371) a “movement incubator” due to its many community relationships. Those include the Philly Palestine Coalition, where the Red Rabbits team [led](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=rQ__OzoJnrX7C5NL&t=3332) the “direct action contingent.”

That work has also drawn the DSA into closer alliance with even more radical groups. Tucson’s Red Rabbits, for example, [work with](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=A3lU764_AMk4CWbz&t=1230) the Party for Socialism and Liberation (PSL)—a would-be revolutionary political party with [close ties](https://www.city-journal.org/article/jim-fergie-chambers-neville-roy-singham-protests) to the Communist Party of China. Portland DSA [cited its work](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=TY5f2DzhUr2O7BeQ&t=5910) with the National Lawyers Guild—a left-wing legal group with [historic ties](https://www.influencewatch.org/organization/international-association-of-democratic-lawyers-iadl) to the Soviet Union—to provide know-your-rights trainings.

Because they may be outside DSA’s mandate as a 501(c)(4) “social welfare” organization, the Red Rabbits trainings could invite legal scrutiny and jeopardize the tax-exempt status of its sister 501(c)(3), [the DSA Fund](https://dsafund.org/). The panelists seemed aware of this risk.

For example, Cliff Connolly, a National Political Committee member who has spearheaded the Red Rabbits commission, [emphasized](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=1ToA10THU_cFcmiZ&t=1303) that the commission was working “very, very closely with \[DSA\] staff” to ensure upcoming standardized trainings were “cleared and good to go” with “no question marks or gray area” about what chapters are permitted to do. The Tucson chapter likewise [stressed the importance](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=ocLuNJmohlLlEjeG&t=1127) of ensuring that its trainings “match the reality of what a 501(c)(4) can do.”

But not everyone in attendance followed that playbook. Oklahoma City’s Queer Fight Club revealed that its project was [designed](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=zS_3YK29plyUo9O-&t=1442) to be “DSA-sponsored and community-supported,” but not entirely DSA-operated. It cited “Red Rabbits’ existence as a separate 501(c) from the DSA structure”—the existence of which could not be confirmed—as a model. But the group also acknowledged that it had “not fully separated from the DSA,” framing the arrangement as a way to manage “liability and tax purposes,” so problems would not “come back to the DSA.”

Panelists also raised concerns about the legal risks introduced by the DSA’s new allies. Portland DSA representative “C” [pointed to](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=L7Bv5EFyq6qwaxcU&t=7232) the Sunrise Movement, an activist group [increasingly focused](https://www.city-journal.org/article/sunrise-movement-climate-ice-protests) on disruptive protests. “Sunrise nationally has been doing a lot of more high-risk stuff for official orgs, like the No Sleep for ICE campaign,” C said. “Their partners in immigration organizations might not be as ready to engage in that work because of their immigration status.”

As a 501(c)(4), the DSA must, [according to](https://www.irs.gov/charities-non-profits/other-non-profits/social-welfare-purposes) the IRS, “operate primarily to further the common good and general welfare of the people of the community.” Some Red Rabbits activities, like know-your-rights education and de-escalation training, probably meet that threshold. But it’s hard to argue that a street-level security force geared toward disruption, confrontation, and resistance to law enforcement exists primarily to “further the common good.” Tactics such as blocking traffic with bicycles, training activists to [escape physical holds](https://youtu.be/IyhQk8tDbok?si=8ELz4ogcGpiY-CFW&t=1533), forming umbrella phalanxes to confront “fascists,” and [conducting](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IyhQk8tDbok&t=3335s) “takedowns on intersections” bear little resemblance to traditional social-welfare activities. Instead, they suggest preparation for a broader “national uprising”—one of the organization’s stated directives.

The DSA has largely avoided scrutiny, despite its increasingly extreme rhetoric and practices. As the organization grows in influence and increasingly aligns itself with [radical activists](https://www.city-journal.org/article/pro-iran-protests-answer-coalition-foreign-funding) and [hostile regimes abroad](https://www.city-journal.org/article/democratic-socialists-of-america-cuba), elected officials and government institutions have continued to look the other way. If Republicans, Democrats, and state and federal authorities keep ignoring these red flags, they may eventually find themselves living under one.
