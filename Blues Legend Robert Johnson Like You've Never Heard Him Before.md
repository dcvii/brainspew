### This is blues legend Robert Johnson like you’ve never heard him before.

It showed up on YouTube a few days ago, and almost nobody noticed. Sound restorer Nick Dellow got his hands on a a shellac master test pressing of blues legend Robert Johnson. This disk had been made in 1940 from the original metal parts, which had been acquired by Columbia Records, and left forgotten in the company’s storage facility in Bridgeport, Connecticut.

The sound quality is stunning.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gmnNi8oPsrQ)

### Pay a visit to a man who has lived alone in the Oregon woods for 25 years.

You can feel the peace and quiet just by watching this video. Lloyd Hammons is 81 years old—and for the last quarter of a century he has lived in solitude and close contact with the natural world.

I watch him with his pipe and wood stove, sharing his ecosystem with cougars, bears, bobcats—it’s like taking a time machine back to the 19th century.

“I’ve had a bear chase me,” he recalls. “That’s no fun.” But Hammons also draws on solar energy and other modern technologies, so he can enjoy a few conveniences (radio, hot shower, etc.) in a totally pristine natural environment.

“It’s the freedom—I can do what I want. I spend a lot of time watching birds and animals. That’s my entertainment. And reading of course.”

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gzRkyNFcUaQ)

### The rise and fall of a great American piano company.

The story of the Baldwin business is a strange as it gets. Somehow a great piano company morphed into a huge financial empire which collapsed under $9 billion in debt—the largest bankruptcy in history at the time. The piano business survived (and is now owned by Gibson. But all manufacturing now happens in China. The company’s impressive 8-story Cincinnati headquarters still stands, but survives only as an apartment building.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9x67jm-hdaE)

### “I photographed boring things for a year. Here’s what happened.”

I wrote a chapter on boredom for [my first book](https://www.honest-broker.com/p/my-warning-about-ai-music-from-1988), and I’ve kept returning to the concept over the years. I view boredom as the secular equivalent of original sin, an inability to connect with the world that serves as a pathway to so many other self-inflicted problems.

In a deeply engaged life, there’s no room for boredom. Its pervasiveness in society today is thus a commentary on our misplaced priorities.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!LBqn!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b33468c-4ad5-4fb3-88a6-a8e8bd7700d6_1920x1353.jpeg)

Maybe that’s why I resonated with this video about a photographer who decided to take photos of boring things for a full year. He soon found that nothing is really boring—you just need to learn how to look at things with fresh eyes.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ymjiI5SuK80)

### The woman who build Pat Metheny’s 42-string guitar.

Linda Manzer is a guitar maker who lives in Almonte, Ontario. She discovered her vocation after seeing Joni Mitchell play the dulcimer. In the aftermath, she wanted a dulcimer of her own, but couldn’t afford one—so she built her own.

Her encounter with Pat Metheny was decisive: “It was like my universe had shifted.” She has now made more than two dozen guitars for him—but the most mind-blowing one is her [42-string Pikasso guitar,](https://guitar.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/Pikasso-Guitar-Chinery-Credit-Brian-Pickell@1400x1050.jpg) based on a sketch Metheny drew on a napkin.

This video tells the behind-the-scenes details, and also contains commentary from Metheny himself.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LkDXr2_2qOo)

### “I kept a journal for 42 years and this is what happened.”

The single best habit for a writer is keeping a journal. But for Amanda Close, her diary collection is part of an ongoing family legacy. Her grandmother and mother also kept journals, and she still has them in her possession. What an extraordinary thing to pass on the next generation.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QQyZWxRwKD4)

### The oldest surviving blues star Buddy Guy, age 89, performs a Tiny Desk Concert.

Other music genres celebrate the young. Blues fans, however, like their musicians as old as possible—because this is *roots* music, and the oldest roots dig the deepest. So Buddy Guy, the son of cotton sharecroppers in Louisiana, is absolutely in his prime. And—dang!—he looks so young, even as he approaches his 90th birthday.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m5XxOLdMSS8)

### The decline of the British pub.

I don’t hang out in bars—it’s just not my style. But when I was a student in Britain, I went to the pub every day. It was a social event, completely unlike anything I’d experienced back home. By the way, I also played occasional jazz gigs at pubs, and this added to my love affair with this beloved institution. Since then, I’ve wondered why the US has never had much of a pub culture.

As it turns out, I was visiting the pub at the absolute peak period of its influence, and the number of public houses has been declining since that time. On average, one pub shuts down every day in the UK. So I now fear that Britain may also be losing this convivial institution—a victim to phone scrolling, abstinence, and home meal delivery, among other things.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4cT7EzPND40)

### Lucas Debargue updates Mozart’s “Alla Turca” music for a concert in Istanbul.

Lucas Debargue infuriates many people in the classical music world—most recently for a Facebook post ardently defending the role of improvisation in the concert hall. I love him all the more for his boldness and irreverence.

But now he is at it again.

Yesterday he posted this video from a recent concert in Istanbul. Debargue decided to use the occasion to create new piano variation inspired by the “Alla Turca” movement from Mozart’s Piano Sonata in A Major. He says he wrote it in a couple of hours with the sole goal of “having fun on stage.”

Fun? That’s a dangerous notion in the world of *serious music*. Imagine what would happen if it started spreading?

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bhEHRrsx_qE)
