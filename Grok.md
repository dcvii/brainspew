The largest natural gas storage facility in California is the Aliso Canyon Natural Gas Storage Facility (operated by Southern California Gas Company in Los Angeles County). It has a capacity of up to 86 billion cubic feet, making it the state's largest (and one of the largest in the U.S.).California did not permanently shut down the entire facility. Following the massive leak that began on October 23, 2015 (the largest methane release in U.S. history), authorities took significant actions:
- In January 2016, Governor Jerry Brown declared a state of emergency and prohibited further gas injections into the facility.
- The facility was effectively shut down for normal operations (no injections, limited or no withdrawals in phases) starting late 2015/early 2016 to address the leak and safety concerns. The leaking well (SS-25) was permanently plugged on February 18, 2016.
- It remained offline or heavily restricted for over a year while inspections, repairs, and regulatory reviews occurred.
Partial operations resumed in 2017 (limited injections allowed starting around July 2017 after CPUC approval), and it has continued operating since then despite ongoing calls for permanent closure.Governors Jerry Brown and Gavin Newsom both pledged to permanently close it (Brown targeted by 2027; Newsom aimed sooner), but as of 2025–2026:
- Regulators (including the California Public Utilities Commission) have repeatedly extended its life due to energy reliability needs in Southern California.
- Recent decisions (e.g., 2024 votes) allow it to continue into the 2030s, with periodic assessments and some capacity adjustments, but no full shutdown has occurred.
If the question refers to the initial major shutdown amid the 2015–2016 crisis (when the facility was taken offline for safety and the leak was addressed), that effectively began in early 2016 (with injections banned January 6, 2016, and the leak sealed February 18, 2016). However, it was not a permanent closure of the site. No other facility matches as California's "largest" with a shutdown event.
