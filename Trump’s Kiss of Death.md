Donald Trump is the black-thumb president.

Let’s start small. He persuaded FIFA to quash the one-game suspension of the U.S. striker Folarin Balogun, after which the American men’s soccer team suffered a humiliating 4-1 loss to Belgium.

When Trump first announced plans to renovate the Lincoln Memorial Reflecting Pool, he said it would [cost $1.5 million](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/2026/07/13/dc-reflecting-pool-renovation-timeline/90900332007/). On July 15, The Associated Press [reported](https://abcnews.com/US/wireStory/drained-reflecting-pool-reveals-trumps-american-flag-blue-134796731) that despite this initial promise, “the bill ballooned to more than $16 million by June. Trump had said the repairs would last a century, but within days of the project’s initial completion last month, the water was beset by an algae bloom and pieces of the new coating appeared to be peeling off the bottom.”

These setbacks are just two examples of Trump’s kiss of death, the defeats and economic setbacks that have come to characterize his second term in office, including the job losses following Trump’s imposition of tariffs, the surge in the national debt after passage of Trump’s One Big Beautiful Bill Act and the economic havoc resulting from his war against Iran.

Trump is one of the most — if not the most — ham-handed presidents in the nation’s history.

I asked Matthew Dallek, a political historian at George Washington University, for his take on Trump in 2026. He replied by email:

Trump’s infliction of harm is not a result of bad luck or malign interference by foreign and domestic adversaries — the common fate of American presidents. In the overwhelming majority of cases, Trump himself initiated the policies and actions that turned belly-up.

One of the most egregious examples, perhaps, is his decision to join Israel’s bombing of nuclear facilities in Iran on June 21, 2025.

“The strikes were a spectacular military success,” Trump declared in an [address to the nation](https://abcnews.com/Politics/transcript-donald-trump-addresses-nation-after-iran-strikes/story?id=123084288) that day. “Iran’s key nuclear enrichment facilities have been completely and totally obliterated.”

Despite these claims of success 13 months ago, the United States and Israel struck again this year, beginning on Feb. 28, and the war on Iran is in its fifth month, with the conflict intensifying and Americans beginning, once again, to die.

On his Substack, [Popular Information](https://popular.info/p/straight-talk-about-hormuz), Judd Legum documented 24 instances between April 17 and July 13 of Trump making some variation of the claim “that the Strait of Hormuz was ‘open’ or controlled by the United States.”

Trump also let the Iranians know that “ [You got beat to hell](https://apnews.com/article/trump-khamenei-beat-to-hell-ecbd1cf140ddf28b73d802f7a8ba70ea) ” and boasted that it was a “ [Total and complete victory. 100 percent](https://www.wbaltv.com/article/did-us-win-iran-war-fact-check-trump/70977123?utm_source=chatgpt.com).”

More important than Trump’s false claims of victory, the war on Iran — a war of choice — has inflicted severe damage on the United States, [including](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/07/18/world/middleeast/us-troops-killed-iran-war-jordan.html) the death of at least 17 American military personnel and the wounding of more than 400 others.

According to Brown University’s [Iran War Energy Cost Tracker](https://iranwarcost.watson.brown.edu/), Americans [are paying](https://iranwarcost.watson.brown.edu/) $71.3 billion more for gasoline and diesel alone. The increases in fuel costs translate into average household costs of $543.94 as I write. It will be more when you read this.

Let’s take a closer look at some of Trump’s proudest achievements.

On April 2, 2025, he announced “Liberation Day,” his plan to impose tariffs on America’s trading partners.

“Jobs and factories will come roaring back into our country,” the president asserted, adding, “We will supercharge our domestic industrial base.”

So what actually happened?

When Trump took office for the second time in January 2025, the total number of men and women employed in manufacturing was 12,673,000, according to the [Federal Reserve](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/manemp#:~:text=Graph%20and%20download%20economic%20data%20for%20All%20Employees%2C,headline%20figure%2C%20establishment%20survey%2C%20manufacturing%2C%20employment%2C%20and%20USA.). By June 2026, the number fell to 12,598,000, a loss of 75,000 jobs.

How about Trump’s One Big Beautiful Bill Act?

On June 30, 2025, the title of a White House news release claimed “The One Big Beautiful Bill Slashes Deficits, National Debt While Unleashing Economic Growth.”

Just before Trump took office, the nation’s debt was $36.2 trillion, according to the U.S. Treasury Department. The debt is now more than [$39.5 trillion](https://fiscaldata.treasury.gov/datasets/debt-to-the-penny/debt-to-the-penny), an increase of $3.3 trillion.
