Some years ago, I did a memoir for a fellow who had directed many films, including a couple of sports movies. He talked about how difficult it was to choreograph key scenes, even with multiple takes and special effects and careful editing.

He told me something after the 2024 Butler shooting that I think is relevant to last night: "Anyone who thinks it's easy to fake an assassination attempt (or rig the end of a football game) has never worked in Hollywood. Once you get many people moving, and you get things flying in the air like bullets or balls (or exploding like squibs), the chances of having it all work out on the first take? ZERO."

Those of you who think this stuff is all "smoke and mirrors" do not, in fact, understand "smoke and mirrors." I've been in L.A. long enough -- and ghostwritten enough for retired stunt people and F/X gurus -- to know you can't actually fake what you think you can fake.

I'd bet my family ranch what happened last night was real.

Your suspicion is a vice to be conquered, not an instinct to be indulged.
