Meet NYC’s New Socialist Leaders

Darializa Avila Chevalier, Brad Lander, and Claire Valdez—the three Zohran Mamdani–backed candidates who swept Tuesday’s New York Democratic congressional primaries. ( Illustration by The Free Press; images via Getty)

Abolish ICE. Free Palestine. Organize your union.

On Tuesday, the Democratic establishment suffered a crushing defeat when [nine out of 10 candidates](https://jacobin.com/2026/06/dsa-valdez-chevalier-mamdani-nyc) endorsed by the local chapter of the Democratic Socialists of America (DSA) coasted to victory in their primaries. In some instances, long-serving progressives were caught in the crosshairs, including Representative Adriano Espaillat, a five-term House member who lost the Democratic nomination to a 32-year-old socialist. A House staffer for a New York Democrat told me that in progressive cities like New York, the DSA is “the new establishment.”

Hasan Piker, the far-left Twitch streamer, squealed with excitement at an East Williamsburg watch party when he saw yet another Democratic Socialist clinch the Democratic nomination.

“Brother,” [he said](https://www.twitch.tv/videos/2803627514), “the Democratic Party machine in the blue stronghold of New York City was thoroughly defeated tonight.”

The night was a major test for Mayor Zohran Mamdani, who pulled off his own upset almost a year ago against Democratic Party giant Andrew Cuomo. The mayor, a long-standing DSA member, worked overtime to appear at countless campaign events for a trio of candidates he dubbed “the Team”: Claire Valdez, Brad Lander, and Darializa Avila Chevalier. Meet the Mamdani-backed candidates who soared to victory last night.

#### Claire Valdez

#### Age: 36

#### Seat: New York, District 7

Claire Valdez is an artist turned union organizer.

With about [92 percent of the results](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2026-primary-elections/new-york-us-house-district-7-results) in, Valdez, a first-time state legislator, trounced Representative Nydia Velázquez’s preferred successor, Brooklyn Borough President Antonio Reynoso, by a margin of more than 20 points. When the news broke at Valdez’s watch party in East Williamsburg, “DSA!” [chants erupted](https://www.cityandstateny.com/politics/2026/06/mamdani-ally-claire-valdez-captures-congressional-seat/414369/).

“Solidarity forever,” she [told her supporters](https://youtu.be/fIF3nCfz4nw?si=fw5MroZnLp2QUc3d&t=576) last night. “Abolish ICE. Free Palestine. Organize your union, and join DSA.”

Like Mamdani, Valdez made affordability a centerpiece of her insurgent campaign. In interviews, she [often stressed](https://jacobin.com/2026/01/claire-valdez-union-organizing-congress) her past employment at fast-food joints like Taco Bell and Pizza Hut. As a young adult, she had dreams of becoming an artist and [received her BFA](https://thevisualist.org/2014/12/pineapple-ribbon-new-works-by-erol-scott-harris-claire-valdez/) from the Art Institute of Chicago. In 2015, she moved to New York to pursue a career as a painter, but soon landed a job at Columbia University, where she worked as a program assistant in the visual arts department. At Columbia, she joined a chapter of the United Automobile, Aerospace, and Agricultural Implement Workers of America, which [represents support staff](https://www.instagram.com/columbia2110union/) at Columbia.

“My union changed my life,” Valdez [told](https://jacobin.com/2026/01/claire-valdez-union-organizing-congress) *[Jacobin](https://jacobin.com/2026/01/claire-valdez-union-organizing-congress)*, a socialist magazine, shortly after [launching her bid](https://qns.com/2026/01/valdez-campaign-congress-nydia-velazquez/) in January. “It was the first time in my working life that I didn’t feel alone.”

Valdez’s personal Facebook page shows how deeply her radicalism goes. In 2016, she shared [an article](https://web.archive.org/web/20170618222502/https://www.newyorker.com/news/news-desk/a-police-killing-in-baton-rouge) after Alton Sterling was [fatally shot](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2016/jul/06/alton-sterling-baton-rouge-investigation-department-of-justice) by police officers. She wrote that “racism is the largest, most coherent, and most consistent aspect of American society.”

[She added](https://www.facebook.com/claire.valdez.12/posts/pfbid0XqZR2CQz2fvBKr5NoHwhGGpDK9uu9eCpYNtcHtRyN2dNeoMsqq6GTkCADEFqUcpsl?rdid=dVXfNgsWcaevshDK#): “no thx to body cams. let’s abolish the police instead.”

Other posts from that time include a link to a *New York Times* article titled “ [White Terrorism Is as Old as America](https://www.nytimes.com/2015/06/19/magazine/white-terrorism-is-as-old-as-america.html),” and a note calling a new fund in Chicago for victims of police abuse a “precedent for reparations nationwide.”

In 2019, she [joined the DSA](https://democraticleft.dsausa.org/2026/06/16/claire-valdez-on-taking-the-labor-movement-to-congress/) after seeing the rise of Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez and state senator Julia Salazar, both of whom were elected with the DSA’s help.

Unlike Mamdani, who often [deflected questions](https://www.tiktok.com/@theviewabc/video/7556289767193840910) on Israel by stressing that his focus would be on New York, Valdez leaned into her anti-Israel activism as a key part of her campaign. At events, her staff handed out signs that said “Free Palestine.” She [appeared at her campaign launch party](https://www.cityandstateny.com/politics/2026/03/pro-palestinian-pal-pac-endorses-claire-valdez-congress/412325/) alongside Mahmoud Khalil, a key anti-Israel leader at Columbia University that the Donald Trump administration has tried to deport.

Valdez [referred to Israel’s war](https://x.com/claireforny/status/1712969888921194813?s=20) against Hamas as a “ [genocide](https://www.thefp.com/p/what-anti-zionism-really-is) ” as early as October 13, 2023. She has repeatedly criticized the American Israel Public Affairs Committee (AIPAC), the pro-Israel lobbying group run by Americans. At her watch party Tuesday evening, chants of “fuck AIPAC” [reportedly broke out](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/06/24/israel-democratic-party-new-york-primaries-00973287). Valdez is now favored to win the solidly blue House seat, which straddles Brooklyn and Queens.

#### Brad Lander

#### Age: 56

#### Seat: New York, District 10

Brad Lander is the quintessential anti-Zionist Jew. At his son Marek’s bris, Lander gave a speech lambasting Israel.

“We pray fervently that by the time you read this, the Israeli occupation of the West Bank and Gaza, the settlements, the house demolitions, the violence will be history,” he said in the speech, which was later reprinted in a 2003 book titled *Wrestling with Zion*. “But even then, we hope you will appreciate this absence of nationalist privilege in your inscribed identity.”

Lander, the city’s former comptroller, snagged the Democratic nomination out from under incumbent Dan Goldman.

Goldman is a Jew who [has identified](https://goldman.house.gov/media/press-releases/statement-congressman-dan-goldman-hr-5179-anti-bds-labeling-act) as a “proud Zionist and steadfast supporter of Israel.” In the run-up to Election Day, a chain of Brooklyn coffee shops called Poetica posted that it would have [barred Goldman entry](https://www.timesofisrael.com/nyc-coffee-shop-bars-jewish-congressman-we-dont-serve-genocide-enablers/) had they recognized him during a recent visit to their storefront.

“We don’t serve racists, fascists, homophobes, genocide enablers,” Poetica declared. “Too bad we didn’t recognize you right away, or we would have turned you away.”

Although many New York City races on Tuesday centered around Israel, none became more of a de facto referendum on the Jewish nation than the showdown between Goldman and Lander, a self-described “ [progressive Zionist](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/08/18/nyregion/israel-gaza-lander-adams.html) ” who has [accused Israel of genocide](https://www.thefp.com/p/what-anti-zionism-really-is), ethnic cleansing, and “ [forced starvation](https://www.timesofisrael.com/nyc-comptroller-calls-for-coalition-of-anti-zionists-and-liberal-zionists-at-mamdani-event/).” Only a few years ago, Goldman would’ve been considered a safe seat as a progressive center-left legislator in a deep-blue district—and yet, with about 90 percent of the results in, Lander is poised to win the Democratic nomination by more than 30 points.

At his election night party at a Gowanus brewery, Lander seemed to register the sea change within his party.

“We cannot keep paying for \[Benjamin\] Netanyahu’s wars with our tax dollars,” he said. “Democratic voters across the country are saying this loud and clear.”

A key faction of his coalition were anti-Israel activists, including members of [Jews for Racial & Economic Justice](https://www.jfrej.org/news/2026/06/jews-for-racial-economic-justice-celebrates-sweeping-primary-victories-brad-lander-eli-northrup), a radical organization that [released a statement](https://x.com/JFREJNYC/status/1710800706456768823) on October 7, 2023, calling the Hamas attack on Israel “ *neither* justifiable *nor* unprovoked.”

#### Darializa Avila Chevalier

#### Age: 32

#### Seat: New York, District 13

Darializa Avila Chevalier, a 32-year-old PhD candidate with no prior experience in office, toppled Representative Adriano Espaillat, a five-term incumbent.

On Tuesday night, Avila Chevalier told the crowd at her watch party at a Puerto Rican restaurant about her fight against the “Democratic machine.”

“I see our future,” [she said](https://youtu.be/4ecgtsm2yVE?si=DtZut1SQhsBbbHHT&t=684), standing next to Mamdani. “I see a New York that we can all afford. I see a New York that truly invests in its babies, not bombs. And it is such a beautiful future.”

Avila Chevalier’s social media history is chock-full of offensive content. In 2020, she called Joe Biden a “rapist” and “war criminal” and referred to the U.S. as a “ [fucking disgrace](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/new-york-playbook/2026/05/29/espaillats-challenger-really-doesnt-like-establishment-democrats-00942098).” According to CNN, in 2021, she also retweeted posts declaring that the border [should be abolished](https://web.archive.org/web/20210922095338/https://twitter.com/haymarketbooks/status/1440409849947250690) and that “all deportation [is wrong](https://web.archive.org/web/20210924024202/https://twitter.com/darializabonet/status/1441153430299373569).”

Perhaps no other candidate in American history has been so brazenly hostile toward Israel and made it this far. As a student at Columbia University, Avila Chevalier was involved in Students for Justice in Palestine. In 2024, she returned to her alma mater to help organize an anti-Israel encampment that took over the [South Lawn](https://columbianewsservice.com/2024/05/03/protest/) and was ultimately disbanded by the police. The day after the October 7 attack, she attended an anti-Israel demonstration in Times Square—a decision she has since defended.

“I can only say I have been advocating for the human rights of Palestinians for my adult life,” she [told](https://www.cityandstateny.com/politics/2026/06/avila-chevalier-attended-oct-8-pro-palestinian-rally-lander-condemned/413981/) *[City & State](https://www.cityandstateny.com/politics/2026/06/avila-chevalier-attended-oct-8-pro-palestinian-rally-lander-condemned/413981/)* when asked about her attendance at the rally.

Back in March, Avila Chevalier met with the Broadway Democrats, a left-wing political club, which asked if she condemned Hamas’s attack against Israel.

“The premise of that question, to me, ignores the 75 years of occupation that the Palestinian people have been subjected to and the conditions that folks were living under before this genocide began,” [she responded](https://freebeacon.com/democrats/not-a-close-call-left-wing-group-declines-to-endorse-socialist-in-tight-house-race-over-point-blank-refusal-to-condemn-hamas/).

The club later declined to endorse her, writing that she “ [point-blank refused](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1cb1fJXazH5ilXmY9lHeTa-Rh3PexGMxBC-Btpac9MA0/edit?tab=t.0) ” to condemn Hamas, instead “turning the question into yet another attack on Israel.”

Avila Chevalier considers herself a “revert” [to Islam](https://www.thefp.com/p/why-western-women-are-reverting), a term used to convey the belief that all people are born with an innate path toward Islam. In March, she spoke at the Al-Khoei Islamic Center in Queens—which has [received funding](https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/display_990/237345978/2014_02_PF/23-7345978_990PF_201303) from a charity U.S. officials later linked to [Iranian-sponsored terrorism](https://www.justice.gov/usao-sdny/pr/sdny-announces-recovery-hundreds-millions-dollars-victims-iran-sponsored-terrorism-0) —and shared that she had converted three years earlier.

The politician said she was [inspired to adopt Islam](https://youtu.be/05ZQDYG8T2k?si=QXlOY63vQRBxgP-6&t=171) after seeing how her Muslim friends had so much “grace and love and passion” in “spaces of social justice” that ultimately pushed her toward the religion. She added that she’s the only Muslim in her family.

Her race against Espaillat, a staunch supporter of Israel and recipient of [AIPAC-linked funds](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/23/nyregion/new-york-primary-campaign-spending.html), became a proxy war on the Middle East. Avila Chevalier [accused her opponent](https://youtu.be/05ZQDYG8T2k?si=AMz-qHSSZchpmzUQ&t=254) of being “bought by the Israeli lobby to stay silent.” Despite her criticism of AIPAC, Avila Chevalier herself was [helped by](https://docquery.fec.gov/cgi-bin/forms/C00932723/1984157/se) more than $1 million in spending on advertisements by American Priorities, an anti-Israel PAC [launched earlier](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2026-election/new-super-pac-launches-counter-aipac-spending-democratic-primaries-rcna259448) this year.
