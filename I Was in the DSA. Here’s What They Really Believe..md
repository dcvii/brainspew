The socialist candidates being swept into power want to dismantle American life. It’s all right there in their new platform.

Candidates backed by the Democratic Socialists of America (DSA) keep racking up primary victories, and skeptics and fans alike are struggling to determine what the organization actually believes. President Donald Trump says they are “communists.” One prominent DSA member, *Jacobin* publisher Bhaskar Sunkara, says they are a “milquetoast organization” seeking Scandinavian social democracy, with no interest in violent revolution.

I was a member of the DSA from the 2010s until 2022. During that time, I witnessed a rise in extremism, ranging from an eagerness to collaborate with Leninist groups to a growing animosity toward the U.S. and the West. My final departure was prompted by a DSA statement that attributed Russia’s aggression against Ukraine to American “imperialist expansionism.”

So what does the DSA stand for? An answer to that question arrived last week in the form of a new platform. Among the DSA’s core aims are ending the presidency and Supreme Court. Abolishing prisons. Universal free housing. The elimination of all debt. Closing all U.S. military bases overseas. Lifting all sanctions from Cuba, Venezuela, and Iran. And so much more.

“ [Workers Deserve More](https://program.dsausa.org/#we-fight-for)!”—the title of the new platform—is meant to present a friendlier face to the world and overcome the radical reputation the organization has developed in many corners of the public. That makes it even more remarkable that the document is still so radical on its face. It advocates the elimination of capitalism, not a thick layer of redistribution on top of the current economy. It prompts readers to imagine a world where the profit motive has been squelched out of existence. “You have no debt. You don’t need health insurance. You don’t pay a mortgage or have a landlord, because comfortable housing is a human right. Your retirement is publicly funded.” It goes on to promise open borders, the abolition of the police, “free” everything, and guaranteed jobs, all with less effort from the individual. This is the stuff of fairy tales, but instead of a fairy godmother, the reader is greeted by an agent of the secret police.

---

The platform offers a utopian vision that erases the complexities of human life. It includes vague demands for a massive expansion of public resources alongside specific proposals like “paid family leave for all workers, a 32-hour work week with full pay and benefits, and a living minimum wage.” They also call for free college and the cancellation of all student debt.It paints a world devoid of anxieties and limits—no debt, no mortgages, no landlords, and no bills coming due.

The platform makes no mention of homeownership, likely because many in the DSA genuinely imagine a world without private property. Instead, the proposal promises that “you work as much as you are able and no more than needed,” a near restatement of Marx’s famous dictum: “From each according to his ability, to each according to his need.” By suggesting that you could “control your own labor and see it going to good use,” the document makes a complex reorganization of society sound suspiciously easy. As history has shown, such experiments yield [catastrophic results](https://x.com/FoxNews/status/2077750629527605720?s=20).

At times, the authors try to make the revolution they’re seeking appear limited, saying they’ll target and expropriate only “the largest corporations and essential industries.” But the candid remarks of DSA leadership reveal a distrust of all private enterprise. New York City DSA co-chair Gustavo Gordillo [told Fox News](https://youtu.be/VjKMwl5vElA?si=xyeuzSwPneDmcRh3&t=335) that he did not care if government-owned grocery stores put small bodegas out of business: “If one publicly-owned grocery store that brings prices down in the neighborhood is enough to put someone out of business, then maybe they shouldn’t have been in that business in the first place.”

The DSA platform offers a utopian vision that erases the complexities of human life.

The plan also makes clear that the DSA would strictly regulate investment properties and impose universal rent control—a strategy [currently playing out](https://www.cityandstateny.com/policy/2026/06/new-york-city-freezing-rent/414448/) on a smaller scale in New York City. The goal of these policies is [not to solve a housing shortage](https://www.law.georgetown.edu/poverty-journal/blog/when-good-intentions-backfire-how-new-yorks-rent-laws-harm-the-most-vulnerable/), but to render private rental properties unprofitable, clearing a path for long-term expropriation. Remember, the DSA’s Housing Justice Commission has been clear that its [long-term goal](https://housing.dsausa.org/socialhousing/) is “the end of commodified housing.”

Many DSA activists are committed true believers armed with the arrogance of youth and plenty of free time to organize around their vision. Many really do believe a revolution will liberate the world from capitalism, all while enjoying chic coffee shops.

How will they pay for it? On July 16, Sunkara [posted to X](https://x.com/sunraysunray/status/2077883020678205664?s=20) perhaps the most honest answer: “Expropriate the expropriators.”

By targeting the most productive parts of the American economy and undermining small businesses, the DSA would gut the engines of America’s success. The result would mirror the [city of Chicago](https://rfs.dsausa.org/case-studies/chicago-teachers/), which has operated under [DSA-aligned leadership](https://y.dsausa.org/the-activist/the-activist-left-made-brandon-johnson-victory-possible/) since 2023. Chicago isnow borrowing to [pay its daily operating costs](https://www.compactmag.com/article/america-isnt-ready-for-blue-city-defaults/), a clear indication of a fiscal cliff. At a certain point, there is nothing left to expropriate; when economic reality finally hits, immiseration ensues. And Chicago has implemented only a fraction of what a full-blown DSA government would do. The spiral under their control would be immediate, deep, and irreversible.

The DSA’s revolution against American institutions is also designed to strip rights and eliminate checks on power. Instead of a balanced government, the DSA calls for replacing “the President and Supreme Court with an executive and judiciary chosen by and subordinate to Congress.” The only way the DSA could conceivably accomplish its total program is through the fundamental elimination of the checks and balances that safeguard America’s pluralism and stability. The changes DSA proposes would conveniently remove any potential brake on their consolidation of state power.

Beyond its domestic agenda, the DSA’s foreign policy signals a clear intent to pursue retribution against DSA’s political opponents while ignoring America’s adversaries. The proposal to “defund the Department of War” includes a demand to end “immunity for U.S. war criminals,” a clear call for political prosecution built around a far-left [campaign of lies](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5SKrFsb3g1c). The platform advocates for the closure of all overseas military bases, effectively seeking an end to U.S. military power.

By targeting the most productive parts of the American economy and undermining small businesses, the DSA would gut the engines of America’s success.

Crucially, DSA’s foreign policy positions ignore America’s enemies: While DSA targets U.S. and Israeli leadership for prosecution, the platform is silent on the actual atrocities committed by the Russian, Iranian, Venezuelan, and Chinese regimes. In early 2019, when I argued that the DSA should denounce the Nicolàs Maduro regime for mass persecution, abuse, torture, and murder, a local leader told me that to advocate against the Maduro administration would be to play into U.S. war propaganda.

It is always the U.S.—so-called American imperialism—that comes in for the harshest criticism while America’s authoritarian enemies [get apologia](https://international.dsausa.org/statements/no-war-with-russia/). For the DSA, the world’s most [authoritarian regimes](https://www.dsausa.org/statements/dsa-stands-against-imperialist-war-and-with-the-iranian-people/) deserve political cover. DSA blamed NATO and Western “imperialist expansionism” for the Russian invasion of Ukraine. October 7, 2023, the DSA first expressed its solidarity with Palestine and then laid the blame for Hamas’s terror attacks on Israel. After the Maduro regime brutalized and murdered its opponents, DSA members were happy to visit him and celebrate his regime. Meanwhile, the West is expected to dismantle its military capabilities.

Part of the DSA program is an attack on the forces of law and order. The DSA continues to push for police and ICE abolition while simultaneously establishing what sounds like a paramilitary wing. The DSA has formed a group called the [Red Rabbits](https://www.city-journal.org/article/democratic-socialists-of-america-red-rabbits) to prepare for a “national uprising against federal agents and police brutality.” So it’s a paramilitary force for the DSA, but no protection for the rest of us.

---

Do not be fooled by defenders who claim the utopianism of DSA’s platform is merely a far-flung aspiration—a vision planned for some ersatz new Jerusalem far over the horizon. It takes concrete shape in policy today. When [DSA demanded](https://x.com/nycDSA/status/2065529338305384845) that Mayor Zohran Mamdani, who they endorsed, not increase the number of police in New York, [the mayor caved](https://www.foxnews.com/us/mamdani-walks-back-plan-increase-nypd-headcount-following-dsa-pressure). The DSA’s utopianism, its flawed belief in human perfectibility, will have real-world consequences now.

Not everyone inside the DSA is busy with a whitewash brush. The [Marxist Unity Group](https://www.marxistunity.com/) —a caucus with three members on the organization’s governing board of 27, operating as part of the explicitly communist coalition that [governs the DSA](https://manhattan.institute/article/the-dsas-communist-turn) —is clear. The demand is communism. A February 2025 statement on their own draft program for DSA, which influenced “Workers Deserve More!” is much clearer about the organization’s ultimate goal: “The maximum program, on the other hand, elaborates the history of capitalist development and the role of the working class within it. It demonstrates our commitment to the long-term struggle for a fully liberated, classless society—in a word, communism.”
