#MeToo Is Back. This Woman Is Behind It.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!hKRs!,w_750,h_440,c_fill,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep,g_auto/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0b75227c-d814-4fdf-8365-31dee919c928_1024x740.jpeg)

Cheyenne Hunt, whose work taking down Graham Platner and Eric Swalwell has made her the face of a new #MeToo movement. (Paul Bersebach/ Orange County Register via Getty Images)

Cheyenne Hunt helped take down Graham Platner and Eric Swalwell. Now, with her nonprofit Reckoning Action, she has her sights on other men in power and says she is ‘just getting started.’

On July 8, within minutes of Graham Platner abandoning his campaign for U.S. Senate, a 29-year-old Democratic activist named Cheyenne Hunt took to X to declare that his downfall would not be the last.

“What happened in Maine should serve as a clear message: The days of women being sacrificed at the altar of our elections are over,” [she wrote](https://x.com/CheyenneHuntCA/status/2075022719087685824?s=20) of Platner, who was accused of sexual assault.

You may not have heard of Hunt, but you are almost certainly familiar with her work. A lawyer and progressive content creator, Hunt worked with Platner’s accusers to help orchestrate the media rollout that ultimately drove him to call off his campaign.

And he wasn’t Hunt’s first political knockout. In April, the lawyer-turned-influencer played an instrumental role in surfacing sexual misconduct allegations against Eric Swalwell, the Democratic congressman and leading candidate for California governor. The resulting backlash drove Swalwell, a seven-term lawmaker who has denied all wrongdoing, to step down from Congress and suspend his gubernatorial campaign.

In a matter of months, Hunt has become the face of a new #MeToo movement. And she has a name for it: the “ [reckoning](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/annika-albrecht-ally-sammarco-cheyenne-hunt-eric-swalwell-interview/).” Through her newly created nonprofit, [Reckoning Action](https://www.reckoningaction.org/), Hunt has her sights on three more targets: Republican members of Congress [Chuck Edwards](https://x.com/reckoningaction/status/2063404467920544131?s=20), [Cory Mills](https://x.com/reckoningaction/status/2063404470265061701?s=20), and [Max Miller](https://x.com/reckoningaction/status/2063404470265061701?s=20).

“The party of ‘law and order’ keeps protecting predators,” her group Reckoning Action posted in an [X thread](https://x.com/reckoningaction/status/2063404459338940469?s=20) calling for their removal. (The lawmakers did not respond to requests for comment.)

Then there are the new allegations that she’s not even involved with, according to Hunt’s spokesperson, including those against friend of Swalwell and Arizona Democratic senator Ruben Gallego, who is [facing claims](https://nypost.com/2026/07/16/us-news/sen-ruben-gallego-had-sexual-relationships-with-two-house-aides-sources-reveal-to-the-post/) that he had consensual sex with two House staffers who worked for other lawmakers.

Cheyenne Hunt speaks at a Democratic Women’s Caucus news conference outside the U.S. Capitol on May 20, 2026. (Gent Shkullaku/Zuma Press Wire)

Emma Davidson Tribbs, co-founder of the National Women’s Defense League, which researches and advocates against sexual harassment, said she’s been “really inspired” by the crusade Hunt has kicked off.

“To bring about this moment of reckoning for Congress has been really remarkable,” she said.

“The introduction of influencers and these women who utilize their own platforms to share stories,” Tribbs added, “is revolutionary.”

But Gloria Allred, the veteran women’s rights attorney who has represented multiple Harvey Weinstein victims, cautioned that this brave new world, where people can fire off accusations with a simple social media post, may come back to bite some women.

“I’m not against shaming the accused,” Allred told me. “But I’m in favor of protecting the victim—and sometimes just shaming the accused is going to hurt her.” She warns that all victims should understand their options—including litigation and confidential settlements—before going public.

“For every option, there is a consequence,” Allred said. “Some of them are positive and some of them are negative.”

Not all Democrats are thrilled with the resurgence of #MeToo, the 2017 movement that ended the careers of [hundreds of men](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2018/10/23/us/metoo-replacements.html) over allegations that spanned from rape to a [bad date](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/01/15/opinion/aziz-ansari-babe-sexual-harassment.html). Democratic heavyweight James Carville told me he had never heard of Hunt but was taken aback by the mounting number of allegations against lawmakers.

“I think there’s everything right about standing up for women who have been victimized by nonconsensual advances,” he said. If that is Hunt’s mission, he added, “more power to them. I’ll send them a check.”

But, Carville added, there are some “extreme” members of the left who “think every act of intercourse is an act of sexual violence.” The takedown of Senator Al Franken, the Minnesota Democrat who [resigned in 2018](https://time.com/5053958/al-franken-resigns/) after eight women accused him of groping or trying to forcibly kiss them, was “one of the greatest miscarriages” of justice, Carville said.

“I may not want someone like that to marry one of my daughters,” he said, “but I might vote for him.”

Hunt has a combined audience of about 443,000 followers on [TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@cheyennehuntca) and [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/cheyenne.l.hunt/?hl=en). While she’ll occasionally post a [bikini photo](https://www.instagram.com/p/Da6lk0jmnxu/?img_index=1) or [a joke](https://www.instagram.com/p/DZx_UAhy8vE/) (like a [June video](https://www.instagram.com/p/DZbUpALK5el/) of her running off into the sunset beneath the declaration: “There is no ‘man of my dreams.’ I never dreamed of a man in my life. As a little girl, I dreamed of traveling the world and vanquishing all those who dare to cross me”), she’s made a name for herself excoriating powerful men accused of sexual misconduct.

Last month, she went viral for [her commentary](https://www.instagram.com/p/DZqJIdOvLcC/) on the annual Turning Point USA summit for women, where Hunt claimed attendees were “suddenly jumping at the chance to give up their right to vote.” In the final days of Platner’s campaign, she posted a video offering to help staffers find new jobs if they were considering resigning over the allegations against their boss.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!vtG5!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ffc881af6-d3b4-4fef-b5be-26765d68b69c_1024x682.jpeg)

Hunt says she is “just getting started.” (Paul Bersebach/ Orange County Register via Getty Images)

“No one should feel stuck working for a predator, and we want to help,” she said in [the video](https://www.instagram.com/p/Dag19ISxdX2/) posted on July 7, three days before Platner filed paperwork to withdraw from the Senate race.

Hunt, who declined an interview for this story, seems to be casting a wider net with Reckoning Action. Its mission, according to [its website](https://www.reckoningaction.org/mission/), is to “confront misogyny and gender bias in American public life.” In an [April interview](https://www.instagram.com/p/DXUx6dyEgfu/?hl=en) with CNN, Hunt said she felt President Donald Trump, who in 2023 was [found liable](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2023/05/09/nyregion/trump-carroll-rape-trial-verdict) for sexually abusing E. Jean Carroll, had ushered in a “resurgence of misogyny” in America.

“This is the next generation putting our stake in the ground and saying that we’re not going to stand for it,” she told CNN. “Not in any place of power, and not in the society, this next generation of women is not going to take it.”

Rachel Jacobs, a Los Angeles–based lawyer who [co-hosted a podcast](https://www.youtube.com/@baddiesforthepeople) with Hunt for about six months until May 2025, said her friend is a “voice” for women who feel like they cannot speak up.

---

“That’s the ultimate act of service, because she’s put herself and her body on the line for this,” Jacobs, 28, told me. “She provides a shield.”

Hunt isn’t shy about the spotlight. She’s maintained a professional website since at least 2013, when she was roughly 16 years old, where she described herself as “an International Speaker, Model, Internet Personality, and Author.”She’s spent her time since largely advancing progressive causes—from Big Tech reforms to [climate change](https://abcnews.com/Politics/cheyenne-hunt-hopes-become-member-genz-congress/story?id=105559458). As a student at the University of Denver, she served as the policy chair for the school’s [Social Justice Living and Learning Community](https://socialjusticellc.tumblr.com/about), a residential program dedicated to exploring “causes of oppression by examining various forms of privilege and power.” She later earned a law degree from UC Irvine, where she helped represent survivors of domestic violence through a law school clinic. After [an unsuccessful bid](https://ballotpedia.org/Cheyenne_Hunt) to represent her native Orange County in the U.S. House, Hunt landed at Gen-Z for Change, a progressive advocacy organization, where she served as its executive director until just last month.

On March 31, she [uploaded a video](https://www.instagram.com/p/DWjgJEAETWI/) that supercharged a conversation about Swalwell’s sexual conduct that had been brewing since at least 2020, when he is alleged to have [cozied up](https://www.businessinsider.com/eric-swalwell-christine-fang-suspected-chinese-spy-2020-12) to a [suspected Chinese spy](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/trump-administration/eric-swalwell-cease-desist-letter-fbi-kash-patel-suspected-chinese-spy-rcna266005) known as “Fang Fang.” In the video, Hunt appears in front of a screenshot of an anonymous text message that reads: “You know Eric Swalwell has slept with many of his interns and makes them all sign NDAs so they don’t speak up, right?”

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!qlFa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F39d60a07-1a50-4926-9a6a-7212045e996f_1024x683.jpeg)

Eric Swalwell, who resigned from Congress after sexual misconduct allegations. (Tom Williams via Getty Images)

She stared directly into the camera and stated that her party had a moral obligation to grapple with the allegations.

“While it may be seen as politically expedient to sweep this under the rug, it is the wrong thing to do—and we know it,” she said.

One accuser, Lonna Drewes, [alleges](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h2Rr_jzKWE8) that Swalwell [drugged and raped her](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/14/us/lonna-drewes-eric-swalwell-sexual-assault.html) her at a West Hollywood hotel in 2018. The Los Angeles County Sheriff’s Department told me the investigation remains ongoing.

The other is an unnamed former staffer [who told CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/10/us/eric-swalwell-sexual-misconduct-allegations-invs) that, after a 2024 gala—at which point she no longer worked for Swalwell—he raped her. Their sexual history dated back to 2019, when she was his college intern. She said that over the years, the two had exchanged nude photographs, drank heavily, and on one occasion, she performed oral sex on Swalwell—then her boss—in a parking lot at his request.

Sara Azari, a lawyer for Swalwell, told me the claims he’s facing are “either fabricated or gross distortions of routine, professional, or consensual adult interactions.”

Azari described Hunt as someone who had “carefully cultivated the image of an advocate for women and ethical leadership.”

Hunt’s father, Chris Majer, a 75-year-old self-help author, said his daughter had been “on a roll” since helping drive the online campaign against Swalwell. She’s been “overwhelmed with support and requests,” he added.

“It was like a bomb went off,” Majer told me. “She said, ‘I’m going to just play with this a little bit and see if there’s enough support and enough traction, and it isn’t just flash-in-the-pan stuff. I think we’re going to give it a shot and take a crack at building a new organization.’ ”

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oYYK!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F107a8cf7-c707-49b0-a1e8-d09210e28c43_1320x30.png)

In the run-up to her post calling out Swalwell, Hunt received an Instagram message from another progressive content creator. According to *[The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/01/us/eric-swalwell-social-media.html)*, Arielle Fodor—a former teacher who goes by the username “Mrs. Frazzled” online—reached out to Hunt in March after hearing through the influencer grapevine that they were both considering airing allegations against Swalwell. The two quickly formed a group chat, looping in Annika Albrecht, a friend of Hunt’s who would later go on the record [accusing Swalwell](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/annika-albrecht-ally-sammarco-cheyenne-hunt-eric-swalwell-interview/) of sending her “sexually inappropriate” messages as a college student.

“We were just pounding the pavement on it every day,” [Fodor told NBC](https://youtu.be/VApdCMre0Yc?si=Vvjo5QRtsXiEnEjP&t=799) in April. “We just were using everything at our disposal to make sure that we could get a critical mass of women so that it could be investigated thoroughly and reported on and in a major publication.”

The women used social media to locate women with similar stories, but wanted trained journalists at a reputable outlet to independently verify the allegations. Fodor, who now sits on the board of Reckoning Action, has said in multiple podcast interviews that she helped connect several accusers with a CNN reporter. On April 10, the network [published its investigation](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/10/us/eric-swalwell-sexual-misconduct-allegations-invs): “Exclusive: Four women describe sexual misconduct by Rep. Eric Swalwell, including a former staffer who says he raped her.”

By then, the story had become a bona fide scandal, with nearly every major outlet chasing the allegations and their mounting fallout.

Eleven days after Hunt’s initial video, Swalwell [suspended his campaign](https://x.com/ericswalwell/status/2043488502327972096?s=20) for governor. The next day, he announced that he would resign from Congress.

Hunt’s involvement with the downfall of Platner began in a similar fashion: with a video that encouraged followers to connect her with accusers.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Y8Y_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe992361e-037e-4182-bc0e-58d8ea85a240_1024x683.jpeg)

Graham Platner, who dropped out of the Maine Senate race after accusations of sexual assault. (Brianna Soukup via Getty Images)

After [new reporting](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/06/04/us/politics/platner-maine-senate-girlfriends-relationships.html) from *The New York Times* on June 4 detailed an alleged pattern of volatile and intimidating behavior by Platner, Hunt [posted a video](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DZLl-YPBPuh/) revoking her endorsement. She soon received messages from organizers in Maine asking if they could put Hunt in touch with women looking to share their stories of Platner’s sexual misconduct, according to Hunt’s spokesperson.

Lyndsey Fifield and Jenny Racicot, two of the women featured in the *New York Times* story, felt that the publication hadn’t done justice to their experiences (Fifield said as much [in an interview](https://www.thefp.com/p/graham-platners-ex-girlfriend-wants) with *The Free Press*).

Fifield, a [41-year-old](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2026/07/07/ex-girlfriend-graham-platner-says-he-removed-condoms-without-consent/) Virginia conservative, told me she was devastated that the *Times* wrote they “could not independently corroborate Ms. Fifield’s account of the altercations,” despite [her saying](https://x.com/lyndseyfifield/status/2074458074983596378) she gave the reporters numerous contacts who could support her account—including a former landlord, her priest, and her former fiancé. In her June interview with *The Free Press*, Fifield was shocked that the *Times* didn’t include her most serious allegations of physical mistreatment until nearly halfway through the story.

The experience left her feeling “burned,” she told me. But a call from Hunt persuaded her to consider speaking publicly once more.

“She was so kind and sensitive,” Fifield said of Hunt. “She was like, ‘I know that something was terribly wrong with that piece.’ ”

Meanwhile, Hunt was also in contact with Racicot, another former Platner girlfriend, whose allegation—that Platner had sexually assaulted her—had not appeared in the initial story.

According to Hunt’s spokesperson, Hunt connected both women with resources, including pro bono legal services and mental health support as well as with journalists they could entrust with the more detailed versions of their experiences.

---

Fifield told me Hunt helped secure the interview with CNN’s Jake Tapper and remained on speakerphone while it was being recorded, coaching her in real time. “She gave yoga breathing exercises,” Fifield said.

And this time, Racicot shared the full story of an intoxicated Platner entering her home uninvited in late 2021. In an account [published by](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/07/06/graham-platner-sexual-assault-allegation-00987737) *[Politico](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/07/06/graham-platner-sexual-assault-allegation-00987737)* that Hunt coordinated, Racicot described Platner sexually assaulting her and ejaculating inside of her despite her protests and not being on birth control.

“I remember the specific moment where I thought to myself, like, ‘This is no longer my choice,’ ” Racicot told *Politico*.

Within days, Platner, who has consistently denied the allegations, dropped out of the race.

Hunt doesn’t appear to be slowing down any time soon. She’s eager to staff up and help introduce federal legislation barring politicians accused of misconduct from using campaign funds to pay their legal bills, she [recently told](https://sfstandard.com/2026/07/07/cheyenne-hunt-platner-swalwell/) *The San Francisco Standard*.Also on her list? Going after the Republicans who voted against making the Epstein files public, she told the paper.In a statement sent to me, Hunt said, “The fight against misogyny is far bigger than any single candidate or lawmaker, and our organization is just getting started.”

The last thing conservative activist Christopher Rufo wants is for Hunt’s movement to grow. He said he was happy to see Platner go, but said his party has made a mistake by embracing the #MeToo vigor that brought the Maine politician down. Unlike the original #MeToo wave, [Republicans have emerged](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/05/02/us/politics/boebert-mace-luna-republican-women.html) as prominent voices in the latest push for reform, with Reps. Lauren Boebert and Anna Paulina Luna helping lead the call to strengthen protections against sexual misconduct in Congress.

“This to me is just insanity,” Rufo told me, expressing shock that some Republican members of Congress have now joined the cause. “People have developed amnesia about the recent past.”
