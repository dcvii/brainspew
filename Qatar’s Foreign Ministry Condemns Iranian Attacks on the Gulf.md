Attacks by Iran on any Gulf Arab country are an attack on all of them, Qatari Foreign Ministry spokesman Majed al-Ansari said.

The comments, in an interview with Al Jazeera recorded last week, reflected the Gulf’s frustration with having been targeted by thousands of Iranian missiles and drones over the five week war, with disproportionate damage done to some.

Despite public displays of unity, the attacks have widened some fault lines in the Gulf, where countries including Qatar have been seen as more inclined to accommodate Iran than others that have taken a more confrontational approach.

Al-Ansari said Iranian strikes on Qatar had declined after the cease-fire but hadn’t ended as of the time of the interview. Qatar’s Ministry of Defense last reported an Iranian attack on April 8.
