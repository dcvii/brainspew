## L.A Clubs from Sunset Strip

## Gazzarri's

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/8832054_orig.jpg)

PHOTO BY GEORGE VREELAND HILL

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/5395271_orig.png)

**Gazzarri's** was a [nightclub](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nightclub) on the [Sunset Strip](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunset_Strip) in [West Hollywood](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California), [California](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California), [United States](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States). It is most notable as the location where [The Doors](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Doors) and [Van Halen](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_Halen) were featured house bands for long stretches before being discovered. It was also the LA club featured in [Huey Lewis and the News](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huey_Lewis_and_the_News) MTV video for their hit "The Heart of Rock and Roll."  
  
Along with The Roxy Theatre, The [Whisky a Go Go](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whisky_a_Go_Go), [The Troubadour](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Troubadour_\(Los_Angeles\)), The Starwood, and other nearby nightclubs, it was a staple of the Los Angeles music scene in the 1960s, 1970s and 1980s and early 90's. Some other bands that played at Gazzarri's either prior to or during their mainstream success include Johnny Rivers, The Go-Go's, [Tina Turner](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tina_Turner), Southgang, Sonny and Cher, [Ratt](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ratt), [Cinderella](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinderella_\(band\)), [Chicano rock](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicano_rock) band [Renegade](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renegade_\(band\)), punk band X, Victor Flamingo, [Quiet Riot](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quiet_Riot), [Stryper](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stryper), [Mötley Crüe](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe), [Poison](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poison_\(American_band\)), [Guns N' Roses](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses), [Warrant](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warrant_\(American_band\)), [Faster Pussycat](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faster_Pussycat), and Canadian rockers [Hollywood Trash](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hollywood_Trash&action=edit&redlink=1). Other notable local Los Angeles area bands to play there included [Redd Kross](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redd_Kross), [Brunette](http://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brunette), [Shark Island](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shark_Island), [Hurricane (band)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurricane_\(band\)), Page 3, Taz, Tuff, Foxx, Storyteller, Reinkus Tide, D'Molls, [Cold Shot](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cold_Shot&action=edit&redlink=1), LEGACY,TRAMP and Pretty Boy Floyd, and bands that never made it like Odin, managed by Bill Gazzari himself, and featured in [The Decline of Western Civilization Part II: The Metal Years](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Decline_of_Western_Civilization_Part_II:_The_Metal_Years), bands such as Salute, Shocktop, and Broken Cherry. Many giant hand-painted pictures of these bands adorned the side of the club's outside wall for many years.  
  

## Rainbow BAr and Grill

The **Rainbow Bar and Grill** is a [bar](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bar_\(establishment\)) and [restaurant](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Restaurant) on the [Sunset Strip](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunset_Strip) in [West Hollywood, California](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California), [United States](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States) adjacent to the border of [Beverly Hills, California](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beverly_Hills). Its address is 9015 [Sunset Boulevard](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunset_Boulevard).The bottom level of the building is the restaurant, The Rainbow Bar and Grill. Upstairs is an exclusive club called "Over the Rainbow", which consists of a full bar, dance floor, and a DJ booth. The restaurant is next to [The Roxy Theatre](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Roxy_Theatre) and [The Key Club](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gazzarri%27s).  
  
The restaurant was founded in early 1972 by [Elmer Valentine](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elmer_Valentine), [Lou Adler](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lou_Adler), [Mario Maglieri](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Mario_Maglieri&action=edit&redlink=1) and others, opening on April 16, 1972 with a party for [Elton John](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elton_John). At the time, the word "rainbow" signified peace and freedom. It quickly became known as a hangout for celebrities of all types. [John Belushi](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Belushi) ate his last meal (lentil soup) at table #16. For many years, the owner was Mario Maglieri.  
  
Before becoming the Rainbow, the restaurant was the Villa Nova restaurant, which was originally owned by film director [Vincente Minnelli](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vincente_Minnelli), at the time married to [Judy Garland](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judy_Garland). [Joe DiMaggio](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_DiMaggio) and [Marilyn Monroe](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marilyn_Monroe) met at the restaurant on a blind date in 1952.  
  
The Rainbow became known as a hangout for rock musicians and their [groupies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Groupies). Notable regulars at the Rainbow in this period include [Lemmy Kilmister](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lemmy), [John Lennon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Lennon), [Keith Moon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keith_Moon), [Micky Dolenz](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micky_Dolenz), [Grace Slick](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grace_Slick), [Ringo Starr](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ringo_Starr), [Neil Diamond](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neil_Diamond), [Robert Plant](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Plant), [Tony Iommi](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Iommi), [Ronnie James Dio](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ronnie_James_Dio), [Alice Cooper](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alice_Cooper), [Lizzy Valentine](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lizzy_Valentine) and many others. [Elvis Presley](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elvis_Presley) was known to have occasionally visited the Rainbow. The famous group of musicians called [The Hollywood Vampires](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hollywood_Vampires) made the Rainbow their home away from home in the early 1970s.  
  
Los Angeles songwriter [Warren Zevon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warren_Zevon) referenced the scene at the Rainbow in the last verse of his 1976 song " [Poor Poor Pitiful Me](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poor_Poor_Pitiful_Me)."  
  
The musical group [Rainbow](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainbow_\(English_band\)) was named after this club.  
  
The track "Rainbow Bar & Grill" from the [Cheech & Chong](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cheech_%26_Chong) album [Let's Make a New Dope Deal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Let%27s_Make_a_New_Dope_Deal) takes place in the bar and restaurant.  
  
Producer [Kim Fowley](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kim_Fowley) used to hangout at the Rainbow, especially in 1975, when he formed the all-girl group [The Runaways](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Runaways).  
  
As musical trends on the Strip changed towards [heavy metal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heavy_metal_music) in the 1980s, the Rainbow followed suit. Members of [Mötley Crüe](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe), [Poison](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poison_\(American_band\)), and [Guns N' Roses](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses) frequented the bar. It was mentioned in a number of songs, such as "Sunset and Babylon" by [W.A.S.P.](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/W.A.S.P.), "Vampire" by [L.A. Guns](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L.A._Guns) and "Peach Kelli Pop" by [Redd Kross](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redd_Kross), and featured in the videos of " [November Rain](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/November_Rain) ", " [Estranged](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Estranged_\(single\)) " and " [Don't Cry](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don%27t_Cry) " by [Guns N' Roses](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses) and also, although briefly, "Rock Out" by [Motörhead](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mot%C3%B6rhead).  
  
[Anthony Kiedis](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthony_Kiedis) of the [Red Hot Chili Peppers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers) noted in his book *[Scar Tissue](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scar_Tissue_\(book\))* that he often sat with his father, [Blackie Dammett](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackie_Dammett), at the club along with various members of bands such as Led Zeppelin. Often the waitresses and bartenders were groupies as well as those who frequented the establishment. In [Pamela Des Barres](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pamela_Des_Barres) ' book, *Let's Spend the Night Together*, the author commented that as an employee in the early 1980s she met a number of celebrities including [Billy Idol](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billy_Idol).  
  
In the early nineties, a tradition of hanging out at The Rainbow after a show was carried on by [Glam Metal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glam_Metal) bands like [Peppermint Creeps](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peppermint_Creeps), [The Zeros](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Zeros), [Alley Cat Scratch](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Alley_Cat_Scratch&action=edit&redlink=1), [Slamhound](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Slamhound&action=edit&redlink=1), [No Sympathy](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=No_Sympathy&action=edit&redlink=1), and [Big Bang Babies](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Bang_Babies).  
  

## Whisky a go-go

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/2637393.jpg?601)

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/7783378_orig.jpg)

**Whisky a Go Go** is a [nightclub](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nightclub) in [West Hollywood, California](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California), [United States](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States). It is located at 8901 [Sunset Boulevard](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunset_Boulevard), on the [Sunset Strip](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunset_Strip).  
  
In 1958, the first Whisky a Go-Go in [North America](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America) opened in [Chicago](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago), [Illinois](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illinois), on the corner of Rush Street and Chestnut Streets. It has been called the first real [American](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States) [discothèque](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discoth%C3%A8que). A franchise was opened in 1966 on M Street in the [Georgetown](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgetown_\(Washington,_D.C.\)) section of Washington, D.C., by restaurateur Jacques Vivien.  
  
It owes its name to the first discothèque, the 'Whisky à Go-Go', established in [Paris](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris) in 1947 by Paul Pacine.  
  
The Sunset Strip Whisky was founded by [Elmer Valentine](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elmer_Valentine), Phil Tanzini, Shelly Davis, and attorney Theodore Flier and opened on January 16, 1964. In 1972, Valentine, [Lou Adler](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lou_Adler), Mario Maglieri and others started the [Rainbow Bar & Grill](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainbow_Bar_%26_Grill) on the Sunset Strip. In 1966, Valentine, Adler and others founded [The Roxy Theatre](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Roxy_Theatre). Lou Adler bought into the Whisky in the late 1970s. Valentine sold his interest in the Whisky a Go Go in the 1990s but retained an ownership in the Rainbow Bar & Grill and the Roxy Theatre until his death in December 2008.  
  
Though the club was billed as a [discothèque](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discoth%C3%A8que), suggesting that it offered only recorded music, the Whisky a Go Go opened with a live band led by [Johnny Rivers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johnny_Rivers) and a short-skirted female [DJ](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disc_jockey) Rhonda Lane, spinning records between sets from a suspended cage at the right of the stage.  
  
The Whisky a Go Go was one of the places that popularized go-go dancing. Elmer Valentine, in a 2006 *[Vanity Fair](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanity_Fair_\(magazine\))* article, recalled arranging to have a female DJ play records between Rivers' sets so patrons could continue dancing. But because there was not enough room on the floor for a DJ booth, he had a glass-walled booth mounted high above the floor.  
  
A contest was held for the girl DJ job but when the young winner called Valentine on the night of the opening tearfully said her mother forbade her from doing it, Valentine recruited the club's cigarette girl, Patty Brockhurst. Valentine quickly hired two more girl dancers, one of whom, Joanna Labean, designed the official go-go-girl costume of fringed dress and white boots.  
  
Rivers rode the Whisky-born go-go craze to national fame with records recorded partly "live at the Whisky." In addition, [the Miracles](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Miracles) recorded the song " [Going to a Go-Go](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Going_to_a_Go-Go_\(song\)) " in 1966 (which was [covered](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cover_version) in 1982 by [The Rolling Stones](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones)), and Whisky a Go Go [franchises](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franchising) sprang up all over the country.  
  
In 1966, the Whisky was one of the centers of what fans call the [Sunset Strip police riots](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunset_Strip_curfew_riots).  
  
Arguably, the [rock and roll](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_and_roll) scene in Los Angeles was born when the Whisky started operation.  
  

## Starwood

The **Starwood Club** was a popular [nightclub](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nightclub "Nightclub") in [West Hollywood, California](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California "West Hollywood, California") from early in 1973 to 1979. Many [punk bands](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punk_band "Punk band") and [heavy metal bands](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heavy_metal_band "Heavy metal band") started their careers playing at the Starwood.  
  
The Starwood Nightclub and music venue was located on the northwest corner of [Santa Monica Blvd.](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Monica_Blvd. "Santa Monica Blvd.") and North Crescent Heights Blvd. It had been a fashionable jazz nightclub called PJ's in the 1960s, which attracted a large number of film and TV personalities and some old school jazz musicians. PJ's hosted such acts as [The Bobby Fuller Four](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Bobby_Fuller_Four "The Bobby Fuller Four"), [The Standells](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Standells "The Standells"), [Rufus Thomas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rufus_Thomas "Rufus Thomas"), and [Trini Lopez](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trini_Lopez "Trini Lopez") who recorded live albums there. It was managed by [Elmer Valentine](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elmer_Valentine "Elmer Valentine") before he left to found the [Whisky A Go-Go](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whisky_A_Go-Go "Whisky A Go-Go").  
  
By 1966 PJ's had been bought by an organized crime figure, [Eddie Nash](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eddie_Nash "Eddie Nash") and reputed organized crime figure [Dominic Lucci](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Dominic_Lucci&action=edit&redlink=1 "Dominic Lucci (page does not exist)"), and was managed by Gary Fontenot (not an organized crime figure) for many years. In 1973, after Nash bought out Lucci's ownership interest in the club, it became The Starwood. It closed in 1981, torn down by the City, after too many citations for underage drinking and noise abatement issues. Before it was demolished it caught fire, though not burning totally. This occurred while unexplained fires befell other Nash-owned properties at the time. Subsequently the structure was torn down, and a [mini-mall](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mini-mall "Mini-mall") was built on the site.

[The Motels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Motels "The Motels"), [The Pop](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=The_Pop&action=edit&redlink=1 "The Pop (page does not exist)"), Foxtrot, Windance, [The Plimsouls](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Plimsouls "The Plimsouls"), [The Quick](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Quick_\(US_band\) "The Quick (US band)"), [The Plugz](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Plugz "The Plugz"), [Snow](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snow "Snow"), [SMILE](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SMILE "SMILE"), [Suburban Lawns](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suburban_Lawns "Suburban Lawns"), [Quiet Riot](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quiet_Riot "Quiet Riot"), [The Runaways](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Runaways "The Runaways"), [Pegasus](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pegasus "Pegasus"),

## Troubadour

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/2612618.jpg?559)

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/9103314.jpg?738)

**The Troubadour** is a [nightclub](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nightclub "Nightclub") located in [West Hollywood, California](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California "West Hollywood, California"), USA, at 9081 Santa Monica Boulevard just east of Doheny Drive and the border of [Beverly Hills](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beverly_Hills,_California "Beverly Hills, California"). It was opened in 1957 by [Doug Weston](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doug_Weston "Doug Weston") as a coffee house on [La Cienega Boulevard](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Cienega_Boulevard "La Cienega Boulevard"), then moved to its current location shortly after opening and has remained open continuously since. It was a major center for [folk music](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Folk_music "Folk music") in the 1960s, and subsequently for [singer-songwriters](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singer-songwriters "Singer-songwriters") and [rock](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_and_roll "Rock and roll").  
  
The Troubadour played an important role in the careers of [Elton John](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elton_John "Elton John"), [Linda Ronstadt](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linda_Ronstadt "Linda Ronstadt"), [James Taylor](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Taylor "James Taylor"), the [Eagles](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eagles_\(band\) "Eagles (band)"), [The Byrds](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Byrds "The Byrds"), [Love](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Love_\(band\) "Love (band)"), [Joni Mitchell](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joni_Mitchell "Joni Mitchell"), [Hoyt Axton](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hoyt_Axton "Hoyt Axton"), [Carole King](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carole_King "Carole King"), [Bonnie Raitt](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bonnie_Raitt "Bonnie Raitt"), [J.D. Souther](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J.D._Souther "J.D. Souther"), [Jackson Browne](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jackson_Browne "Jackson Browne"), [Van Morrison](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Van_Morrison "Van Morrison"), [Buffalo Springfield](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buffalo_Springfield "Buffalo Springfield") and other prominent and successful performers, who played performances there establishing their future fame. In 1962, comedian [Lenny Bruce](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lenny_Bruce "Lenny Bruce") was arrested on [obscenity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obscenity "Obscenity") charges for using the word " [schmuck](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schmuck_\(pejorative\) "Schmuck (pejorative)") " on stage; one of the arresting officers was [Sherman Block](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sherman_Block "Sherman Block"), who would later become [Los Angeles County Sheriff](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_County_Sheriff "Los Angeles County Sheriff"). On August 25, 1970, [Neil Diamond](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neil_Diamond "Neil Diamond") (who had just recorded his first live album at the Troubadour) introduced Elton John, who performed his first show in the [United States](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States") at the Troubadour. In 1974, [John Lennon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Lennon "John Lennon") and his friend [Harry Nilsson](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Nilsson "Harry Nilsson") were ejected from the club for drunkenly [heckling](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heckler "Heckler") the [Smothers Brothers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Smothers_Brothers "The Smothers Brothers"). [Randy Newman](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randy_Newman "Randy Newman") started out at the club and comics [Cheech & Chong](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cheech_%26_Chong "Cheech & Chong") and [Steve Martin](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Martin "Steve Martin") were discovered there. In 1975, Elton John returned to do a series of special anniversary concerts. In November 2007, [James Taylor](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Taylor "James Taylor") and [Carole King](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carole_King "Carole King") played a series of concerts commemorating the nightclub's 50th anniversary and reuniting the two from their 1970 performance.

## THe Roxy

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/1915254.jpg?593)

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/9228386.jpg?604)

**The Roxy Theatre** (often just **The Roxy**) is a famous [nightclub](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nightclub), on the [Sunset Strip](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunset_Strip), in [West Hollywood, California](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California). The Roxy is owned by [Lou Adler](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lou_Adler) and Adler's son, Nic, who operates the club.  
  
The Roxy was opened on September 23, 1973, by [Elmer Valentine](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elmer_Valentine) and [Lou Adler](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lou_Adler), along with original partners [David Geffen](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Geffen), [Elliot Roberts](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elliot_Roberts) and [Peter Asher](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Asher). They took over the building previously occupied by a [strip club](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strip_club) owned by Chuck Landis called the Largo. (Adler was also responsible for bringing the stage play *[The Rocky Horror Show](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Rocky_Horror_Show)* to the [United States](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States), and it opened its first American run at The Roxy Theatre in 1974, before it was made into the movie *[The Rocky Horror Picture Show](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Rocky_Horror_Picture_Show)* the next year.)  
  
[Neil Young](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neil_Young) and his band The Santa Monica Flyers played the Roxy for the first week it was open. Only three months later, the original [Genesis](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genesis_\(band\)) lineup with [Peter Gabriel](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Gabriel) played several consecutive days at the Roxy, a run that some band members and many fans consider to be amongst their finest performances (due partially to the intimate atmosphere and good acoustics of the venue, as well as the legendary reputation of "Hollywood" amongst performers around the world).  
  
[Paul Reubens](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Reubens), then a struggling comedian, introduced his [Pee-wee Herman](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pee-wee_Herman) character in a raunchy revue here in 1981 that included other aspiring comics including [Phil Hartman](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phil_Hartman) and [Elayne Boosler](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elayne_Boosler).  
  
The small *On The Rox* bar above the club has hosted a wide variety of [debauchery](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debauchery) in its history. The bar was a regular hangout for [John Lennon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Lennon), [Harry Nilsson](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Nilsson), [Alice Cooper](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alice_Cooper) and [Keith Moon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keith_Moon) during Lennon's "lost weekend" in 1973-74 and hosted parties arranged by [Heidi Fleiss](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heidi_Fleiss) in the 1980s.  
  
In 1984, [Ratt](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ratt) recorded the video for their hit single Back For More from the album [Out of the Cellar](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Out_of_the_Cellar) at The Roxy. The band has stated that The Roxy is one of their favorite clubs to perform at. Below is Ratt's video "Back For More"

## The Viper Room

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/2966193.jpg?531)

![Picture](https://80shairbandworld.weebly.com/uploads/3/7/3/7/37372977/1695710_orig.jpg)

Band stickers plaster every surface inside the restrooms at The Viper Room in Hollywood, California

**The Viper Room** is a [nightclub](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nightclub "Nightclub") located on the [Sunset Strip](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunset_Strip "Sunset Strip") in [West Hollywood](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Hollywood "West Hollywood"), [California](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California "California"). It was opened in 1993 and was partly owned by actor [Johnny Depp](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johnny_Depp "Johnny Depp") until 2004. The club became known for being a hangout of Hollywood elite, and was the site where actor [River Phoenix](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/River_Phoenix "River Phoenix") died of a drug overdose on [Halloween](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halloween "Halloween") morning in 1993. The Viper Room has undergone several changes in ownership, and continues to host music of multiple genres, including [metal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_music "Metal music"), [punk rock](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punk_rock "Punk rock"), and [alternative rock](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alternative_rock "Alternative rock").  
  
The space where the club is located was originally a jazz bar called the Melody Room, a hang out of mobsters [Bugsy Siegel](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bugsy_Siegel "Bugsy Siegel") and [Mickey Cohen](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mickey_Cohen "Mickey Cohen"). In the 1970s and 1980s it operated as a club called The Central, which was close to shutting down before [Chuck E. Weiss](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chuck_E._Weiss "Chuck E. Weiss"), who had performed there for years, suggested to Depp that they revitalize the spot and rename it "The Viper Room". [Tom Waits](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Waits "Tom Waits") also had a hand in redeveloping the spot.  
  
Despite the death of River Phoenix the year the venue opened, the club became and remained a hangout for Hollywood’s most popular young actors and musicians. Regulars included [Jennifer Aniston](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jennifer_Aniston "Jennifer Aniston"), [Lisa Marie Presley](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Presley "Lisa Marie Presley"), [Jared Leto](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jared_Leto "Jared Leto"), [Christina Applegate](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christina_Applegate "Christina Applegate"), [Angelina Jolie](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angelina_Jolie "Angelina Jolie"), [Rosario Dawson](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rosario_Dawson "Rosario Dawson"), [Tobey Maguire](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tobey_Maguire "Tobey Maguire"), and [Leonardo DiCaprio](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio "Leonardo DiCaprio"). [Adam Duritz](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_Duritz "Adam Duritz"), the lead singer of [Counting Crows](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Counting_Crows "Counting Crows"), worked as a Viper Room bartender in late 1994 – early 1995 to escape his newfound fame. In 1997 The Viper Room was also a place of a few early solo live performances by [John Frusciante](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Frusciante "John Frusciante") at the time of his bad physical condition caused by [drug abuse](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drug_abuse "Drug abuse"). The [Pussycat Dolls](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pussycat_Dolls "Pussycat Dolls") performed there from 1995 to 200 At Depp's request, [Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Petty_and_the_Heartbreakers "Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers") performed at the club's opening night. The Viper Room continues to frequently host [metal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_music "Metal music") and [punk rock](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punk_rock "Punk rock") bands. The first live US performance of [doom metal](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doom_metal "Doom metal") supergroup [Shrinebuilder](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shrinebuilder "Shrinebuilder") in 2009 was held at the venue, as part of [Club My War](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Club_My_War "Club My War"), which hosts bands at The Viper Room on a near monthly basis. On April 16, 2011, a nightclub named "The Viper Room" opened its doors in the city of Nijmegen in the Netherlands. The club is named after the club in Hollywood and is decorated in the same style as the US club.
