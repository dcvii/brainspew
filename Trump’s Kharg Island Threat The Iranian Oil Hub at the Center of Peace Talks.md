Kharg Island is not just another piece of Iranian territory in the Persian Gulf. It is the choke point for Iran’s oil exports, a critical energy hub for China, and now one of President Trump’s most powerful bargaining chips in U.S.-Iran peace talks.

Trump’s threat to seize Kharg Island puts Washington’s pressure campaign directly on Tehran’s economic lifeline. Roughly 90 percent of Iran’s oil exports move through the island, making it one of the most valuable and vulnerable targets in the region. If the United States controls Kharg, it controls the pressure valve on Iran’s oil revenue, its military funding, and its leverage with Beijing.

That is why Kharg Island matters. Taking it would not just hurt Iran’s Ministry of Petroleum, led by Mohsen Paknejad. It would strike at the revenue stream that supports the Iranian government, the IRGC, and Tehran’s broader regional ambitions.

### Why Kharg Island Controls Iran’s Oil Exports

[Iran](https://sofrep.com/news/the-middle-east-far-from-peace-deals/) ’s mainland coastline is too shallow for many large oil tankers, which is why Kharg Island became the deep-water export point for much of the country’s crude oil.

As this is a national prize of great interest, the Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps has or had a presence on the island and is prepared to defend it fiercely. They do consider this a prized possession, and this was expected to be defended heavily; however, after the strikes recently, most of the military targets have been eliminated. The US was careful not to target [oil](https://amzn.to/4vkxkEu) facilities, though, as this could cause issues later. Perhaps if we do seize the island outright, we would need these facilities up and running.

### What Happens After We Take It?

As of this writing, there is not much information available on what would happen if we, the United States, took the island. There are several possibilities, such as using it as a bargaining chip in return for validation of their part of any ceasefire deal or perhaps denuclearization. Perhaps it would be controlled in the same way in which the United States took control of Venezuelan oil after the capture of [Nicolas Maduro](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/was-us-capture-venezuelas-president-legal-2026-01-03/).

There are other questions as well regarding what would happen to the local population if we were to hold it long-term. Most information on the seizure of [Kharg Island](https://sofrep.com/news/irans-kharg-island-defenses-blasted/) focuses on deploying ground troops to maintain and hold the island, but there is no information on how the facilities would be operated or maintained, since many of the workers come and go from Iran.
