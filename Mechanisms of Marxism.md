I've met a lot of very intelligent people, very good at business, who run on Marxist software. It fascinates me. Here's my attempt at an explanation.

🧵

Marxism isn't an economic theory that survives by its intellectual merits. Böhm-Bawerk refuted it in 1896. No serious economics department teaches it as valid. It's been settled for 130 years.

So why do brilliant people still believe in it? Because Marxism isn't a theory. It's a secular religion. And it survives through psychological mechanisms, not rational ones.

First mechanism: resentment disguised as analysis.

Girard explains that mimetic desire can turn around. Instead of wanting to be like the one who succeeds, you want to destroy them. Marxism turns that envy into academic vocabulary. You're not jealous anymore. You "deconstruct relations of domination." Same emotion. Different packaging.

Second mechanism: the guilt of success.

This is the most fascinating. The Marxist entrepreneur has succeeded and feels guilty for having succeeded. Marxism offers him an indulgence. "Yes, I'm a capitalist, but at least I know the system is unjust." It's the modern equivalent of buying your pardon from the Church. You atone for your success by professing the right ideology.

Third mechanism: social signaling.

In educated circles, tech, startups, being "critical of capitalism" is a status signal. It says "I'm not a vulgar capitalist." It's Bourdieu applied to himself: you buy cultural capital by criticizing the system that allows you to have it. The irony is magnificent.

Fourth mechanism: the best story ever written.

Marxism has a hero (the proletariat), a villain (the capitalist), a diagnosis (exploitation), a promise (the revolution), and a paradise (the classless society). It's Star Wars in political version.

The Austrian school in comparison? No hero. No villain. Individuals interacting in a complex system with emergent outcomes. It's true. But it's not sexy. No one makes a revolution for "Hayek's spontaneous order."

Fifth mechanism: the trap of intelligence.

And this is the most counterintuitive. Very intelligent people are MORE likely to be Marxists, not less. Why? Because Marxism is a sophisticated intellectual system with its own internal logic. The more intelligent you are, the more capable you are of building a coherent edifice within the system. And the more beautiful the edifice is on the inside, the less you want to look at whether the foundations hold.

The house of cards is impeccably built on sand. You have to be intelligent to build an impeccable house of cards. Mediocre people can't manage it, their house collapses on its own and they move on to something else. Brilliant people build a house so beautiful that they refuse to look at the sand underneath.

Sixth mechanism: Nietzsche and inverted morality.

Nietzsche described how the weak invert morality to turn their weakness into virtue. Marxism does that on a collective scale. And the Marxist entrepreneur does the opposite: "I'm rich but morally superior to the rich because I recognize the injustice." Luxury resentment. The premium version of bad faith.

The summary in one sentence.

Marxism survives not because it's right. It survives because it fulfills psychological functions that truth doesn't: a culprit (scapegoat), a virtue (resentment transformed), a redemption (guilt atoned), a status (social signaling), and a beautiful story (monomyth).

The Austrian school has the right answers. Marxism has the right emotions. And emotions beat answers. Almost always.

Almost.
