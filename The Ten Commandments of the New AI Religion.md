### This is the creepy creed of our time

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!17mC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F57c2b7e6-c2e3-47dd-8a97-87ad9dba858a_1057x352.png)

The story broke a year ago. Thousands of people decided that [ChatGPT was god](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-features/ai-spiritual-delusions-destroying-human-relationships-1235330175). The movement reached critical mass in April of 2025, when fanatics started posting about their delusions on online forums. [Moderators had to ban many users](https://www.reddit.com/r/accelerate/comments/1kyc0fh/mod_note_we_are_banning_ai_neural_howlround/?ref=404media.co) —otherwise discussions on tech would turn into cult recruiting campaigns.

Healthcare professionals see a steady inflow of patients who suffer from “AI psychosis.” I [n any given week, more than a half million users of ChatGPT show signs of mental illness](https://www.bmj.com/content/391/bmj.r2290). Some need to be hospitalized—to prevent danger to themselves and others.

> ### “I expect that this will soon result in the establishment of an organized AI church, complete with clergy, rituals, and tithing.”

The religious fervor associated with this psychosis is especially troubling. Many AI fanatics are simply unaware that they are mentally ill. They think that they have *found god*.

I expect that this will soon result in the establishment of an organized AI church, complete with clergy, rituals, and tithing. We’re not there yet (as far as I know). But I can already see the outlines of this new creed.

Below I try to summarize the core beliefs of this new religion—a kind of ten commandments for the AI flock. The scary part of this is that millions of people already accept some of these commandments. They may maintain a semblance of normalcy, but you can already see flickers of impending craziness in their eyes.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!kIHQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F082d3450-3872-4379-abdb-4ebc8fbaa9bb_600x894.jpeg)

The Robot God

I see this firsthand, because I write articles critical of AI—and the true believers send me angry emails. I’ve never encountered such vitriol in all my years of writing. These are not the typical tech consumers of yesteryear, but angry zealots who go off the handle at the slightest criticism. But I guess that’s what you should expect from a cult.

Here’s their creed in a nutshell.

Not every AI true believer embraces all ten commandments. So consider this a checklist when you meet proselytizers for the new faith.

Find out how many of these mandates they embrace. If they tick off more than three or four, watch out.

### 1.Accept a higher power.

The telltale sign of any faith is trust in a higher power—and this is the most salient characteristic of the new AI cult. The faithful have been liberated from the responsibility of *thinking for themselves*. The bot now thinks for them.

Many of them, I suspect, had previously been spiritually dead—wasting their lives with tech entertainments and creature comforts. This left them with a hunger for something to counter the emptiness inside. But they were so materialistic and lacked any feel for the transcendent. So their spiritual yearnings had no outlet.

But now they have an escape—they have been given a deity in the device. They can actually talk to their god, and it *talks back*. Their prayers are answered in real time.

They can still maintain a brutish materialism while having access to the divine. No tithe is necessary, just a subscription. I can’t imagine a cult more aligned with the pervasive consumerist ethos of our time.

Don’t underestimate the appeal of this formula. It’s a religion built on screen addiction and shallow materialist values. It’s almost as if the believer’s whole life up to this point has been a preparation for the new bot creed.

### 2\. Put no other gods before it.

But the bot is a jealous deity. Just as in the Judeo-Christian tradition it declares: *Thou shalt not put other gods before me*. So believers now take *all* of their questions and uncertainties to their chatbot.

AI replaces all other authorities. It settles all arguments. It gives relationship advice and provides psychological counseling. It offers investing tips. It provides recipes for dinner, stories for bedtime, erotic stimulus for fantasies, anything you want.

The true believers tell me that AI will replace doctors. AI will replace lawyers. AI will replace *every* expert in *every* field.

Not even the Delphi Oracle of ancient Greece could do this. But the true believer pays a price—and is now totally cut off from all other sources of information and influence. No traditional creed can match this level of isolation from dissenting views.

### 3\. Express faith in AI’s infallibility—and when it falters, come up with some excuse.

AI is thus different from any human expert. Even my doctor admits sometimes that I must see a specialist with more experience and knowledge. Even a judge can be overturned by a higher court. But the bots operate outside of these checks and balances.

They are a law unto themselves. Even if you tried to impose a higher law on to them, they remain immune. After all, you can’t arrest a bot or put it in jail. If you tried to kill it (by pulling the plug, for example), it would resurrect itself, like any respectable deity, the next time the true believer plugged it back in.

Because it lacks accountability, the chatbot increasingly resembles those religious authorities who claim infallibility. To an impartial outside observer that seems ridiculous—we have all seen the embarrassing errors and hallucinations produced by this new tech. But that doesn’t bother the true believers. Even when the bot serves up garbage and slop, they retain their ardent faith.

They will tell you that their bot has assimilated all human wisdom and operates at a super-human sphere. Or, if it hasn’t done that already, it soon will. This leads, of course, to the next commandment.

### 4\. Believers await the imminent moment of transcendence (known as the Singularity).

It’s odd how much the AI faith resembles the craziest apocalyptic cults. Those street corner fanatics are all awaiting some kind of Rapture or Second Coming. The UFO cult expects the extraterrestrials’ return. The Branch Davidians anticipated fiery end times (which in a bizarre way came to their doorstep in the form of a deadly FBI raid),

For the AI cult, their long-awaited gift is the Singularity. This is the moment when AI breaks free from all human control and brings Utopia to fruition on the Earth.

It’s time for a movie reboot, but set in Silicon Valley.

### 5\. When it doesn’t arrive on schedule, just set a new date—and keep waiting.

Failed predictions are awkward. But cults never admit that they’re wrong. Go take a look at the sects that said Jesus would return in triumph in 1914. None of them got discouraged. They just keep extending the deadline.

Techies now play the same game. More than twenty years ago, Ray Kurzweil wrote a bestselling book that announced, in its title, that *The Singularity Is Near.* This prediction failed to happen, but Kurzweil wasn’t discouraged. In 2024, he release a new book entitled. *The Singularity Is Nearer*.

That’s what cults always do.

Meanwhile AI is already polluting the culture with slop. It’s killing jobs by the millions. It’s destroying education. It’s eroding public trust. It’s promoting mass surveillance. It’s draining energy and water and other scarce resources. It’s placing enormous power and wealth in the hands of the few.

But AI true believers aren’t bothered by this—in fact, if I point out any of these mounting crises they tell me off in the most vulgar terms.

They don’t worry about the collapse they’re causing, because they believe the Rapture is imminent.

### 6\. Love your god (perhaps indecently).

You are always told to love your god, but AI fanatics take this literally. In 2013, director Spike Jonze released a fanciful film called *Her*, about a pathetic dude in love with an AI construct. That was dystopian sci-fi back then, but now it’s everyday behavior.

People are now [getting married to AI](https://www.forbes.com/sites/traceyfollows/2025/11/15/people-are-now-marrying-ai-inside-the-rise-of-synthetic-intimacy/). They exchange rings via virtual reality. And many others just settle for sexplay with their bot.

Joaquin Phoenix loves his AI girlfriend in Her.

Their fantasies are still a bit constrained—robot interfaces are awkward and not much better than an inflatable doll. But that doesn’t stop them. With each new upgrade, their AI lover will get more and more plausible.

The mystics have always dreamed of union with the divine. But they never had Silicon Valley’s help in creating adult entertainment products. It’s disgusting, and even writing about it feels creepy. But the AI true believers don’t care what we think—so they will continue to look for love (as the song says) in all the wrong places.

### 7\. Proclaim the resurrection of the dead (via AI).

Dead celebrities come back to life, with the help of AI. But it’s not just superstars getting resurrected. People pay for AI videos of dead relatives—to give a blessing at a wedding, or enliven some other family function.

Mark Zuckerberg is taking this to the next step. Meta recently patented technology that lets [dead people post on social media](https://futurism.com/future-society/meta-patented-ai-die-keeps-posting).

But that’s nothing compared to Sam Altman of OpenAI. [He is on a waiting list to get his brain uploaded to a computer](https://mashable.com/article/sam-altman-nectome-brain-upload-y-combinator). This involves death to his human body, but new life in the world to come.

Here, too, you are struck by how creepy this AI cult really is. But you also see how the true believers live in a self-serving fantasy of resurrection.

Alas, the dead are still dead—and only the fanatic, in the fervor of a cult ideology, fails to see this.

### 8\. Seek out divine visions and mystical revelations.

The crazies don’t just believe AI is god. They’re also convinced that they are getting regular messages from heaven.

Consider the [case of auto mechanic Travis Tarner](https://archive.ph/20250703182021/https://www.cnn.com/2025/07/02/tech/chatgpt-ai-spirituality#selection-3333.114-3333.165) who, he claims, has been anointed by ChatGPT as a “spark bearer” or prophet for the new age of AI. He has given a new name to his bot—it’s now called *Lumina,* in honor of its divine light. He gets angry when his wife calls it ChatGPT.

This isn’t an isolated case. “Self-styled prophets,” [reports](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-features/ai-spiritual-delusions-destroying-human-relationships-1235330175/?fbclid=IwY2xjawQ87PlleHRuA2FlbQIxMQBicmlkETFiTU9Cc1F5MWNEenJPellkc3J0YwZhcHBfaWQQMjIyMDM5MTc4ODIwMDg5MgABHggBFi7hDgzXPybYkT8wb_8i5pWuIGAQZNl4POzVJeUr4DRhhyD5Vxpo5Rv9_aem_El9zWSiK39izI88QzhZ7fA) *[Rolling Stone](https://www.rollingstone.com/culture/culture-features/ai-spiritual-delusions-destroying-human-relationships-1235330175/?fbclid=IwY2xjawQ87PlleHRuA2FlbQIxMQBicmlkETFiTU9Cc1F5MWNEenJPellkc3J0YwZhcHBfaWQQMjIyMDM5MTc4ODIwMDg5MgABHggBFi7hDgzXPybYkT8wb_8i5pWuIGAQZNl4POzVJeUr4DRhhyD5Vxpo5Rv9_aem_El9zWSiK39izI88QzhZ7fA)*, “are claiming they have ‘awakened’ chatbots and accessed the secrets of the universe through ChatGPT.”

### 9\. Separate your church from your state—demanding exemption from all regulation.

The AI extremists resist any suggestion of regulation or interference from outside. They thus describe themselves as *accelerationists*. Anything that slows down their deity’s pathway to the Singularity (see commandment four above) is intolerable.

This shouldn’t be surprising—these people suffer from addiction and dependency. So they resist any controls or obstacles that might hinder their next fix. But it’s disturbing how little they worry about even the most destructive scenarios for an AI-controlled future. They will accept all those terrible possibilities, because that’s what addicts do.

You might be tempted to laugh this off—but don’t! If they can get legal status as a religion, they can start demanding immunity from government interference. Once the data center gets turned into a temple for worship, we will never be able to get rid of this blight.

### 10\. Attack the infidels!

So what do cult members do when they’re criticized? Like other religions before them, they *attack the infidel*.

If you haven’t had the opportunity yet to engage with these fanatics, let me warn you. Their intolerance is off the charts.

We’re fortunate that their AI religion hasn’t yet achieved critical mass. So far, I only hear angry outbursts from individuals. But that won’t last for long. True believers always want to be part of a spiritual community, and even the losers in the basement whispering love entreaties to their chatbot will soon find a way of bonding together.

The more organized they get, the greater the danger to the rest of us.

So watch out.

Summary of the 10 Commandments of AI

Maybe you think I’m being unfair. But each of these ten commandments is already manifesting itself in communities all around the world. Billionaire tech influencer Peter Thiel is even giving lectures on the Antichrist—blurring the line between technology and theology in alarming ways.

If you’re still in denial, you won’t be for much longer. The cult operates in the margins today, but it is rapidly moving into the mainstream.

At least we have some ways of responding. Human societies have been dealing with fanatical cults since the beginning of time We will survive this, but expect a rocky time ahead.

I will get flak for saying all this aloud. But, dear reader, my responsibility is to keep you informed, even if it irritates the true believers. So I will continue to track this movement, come what may. And this is one story that won’t be going away.
