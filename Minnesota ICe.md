To my eyes, the videos show clearly that the ICE officer did not need to shoot Renee Good to protect himself.

I believe two things, which are entirely compatible with each other:

\[1\] The killing of Good should be investigated and prosecuted if there is probable cause. If a jury of the officer’s peers determines he is guilty beyond a reasonable doubt, he should be convicted.

\[2\] It was almost inevitable that this newer set of protester tactics—using automobiles illegally to block or interfere with ICE vehicles—would eventually lead to someone being killed. The officer who shot Good had previously almost died after being run over in the line of duty. Protesters who use cars illegally should be arrested immediately and without exception, or protesters, officers, and bystanders will continue to be injured or killed.

Since roughly 2020—and escalating sharply through 2025 and into 2026 as immigration enforcement increased—there has been a clear pattern in anti-ICE protests. Protesters are no longer relying mainly on static demonstrations or passive road blockades. Instead, vehicles are being used dynamically to interfere with ICE operations: following agents throughout the day, shadowing convoys, positioning cars to slow or block enforcement vehicles, and in some cases attempting to ram them.

Documented incidents include:

• May 2025, California: A couple arrested after driving into oncoming traffic and attempting to ram ICE agents’ vehicles during a warrant operation.

• July 10, 2025, Harris County, Texas: A man chased and rammed an ICE agent’s car in an attempt to interfere with deportations.

• July 31, 2025, Colorado Springs: Two individuals rammed their vehicle into ICE officers during an enforcement action.

• September 26, 2025, Broadview, Illinois: Protesters tried to stop a federal vehicle by standing in front of it, forcing the driver to proceed through the crowd.

• October 2025, Chicago (Brighton Park): An ambush involving as many as ten vehicles, including ramming ICE and Border Patrol vehicles; one armed driver was shot in self-defense.

• October 2025, Chicago: Another incident involving ramming during a raid that escalated into a shootout.

• October–November 2025, Chicago: Protesters repeatedly confronted unmarked vehicles, struck them with objects, or surrounded them. During a November caravan, agents were harassed all day; one vehicle rammed an ICE car and shots were fired at agents.

• November 17, 2025, North Carolina: An individual attempted to ram an ICE vehicle, triggering a high-speed chase that injured an agent.

• December 2025, St. Paul, Minnesota: A man struck two agents with his vehicle during an apprehension.

• December 2025, Charlotte: Protesters used cars to block or potentially ram vehicles during arrests while also doxxing agents.

• June 2025, Los Angeles: A protester attempted to ram a car into an ICE convoy while tailing agents to expose locations and incite crowds.

• June 2025, Portland: Vehicles were used to approach and disrupt agents and surrounding crowds.

• January 7, 2026, Minneapolis (Renee Good incident): Good led what amounted to a parade of cars following ICE throughout the day, then positioned her vehicle to block a convoy, culminating in a fatal shooting.

Taken together, these incidents show a coordinated, real-time escalation—a marked shift from earlier protest tactics and one that substantially increases the risk of serious injury or death.
